<h2>How to mitigate these risks</h2>
<p>While no investment is free of danger, there are practical steps that can reduce exposure. First, diversify your portfolio across multiple asset classes and limit the percentage allocated to any single cryptocurrency—many advisors suggest keeping crypto exposure under 5 percent of total investable assets. Second, use reputable exchanges with strong security measures and consider moving long‑term holdings to a hardware wallet to protect against hacks. Third, stay updated on regulatory news by following official government channels and reputable legal analysts. Fourth, consider using dollar‑cost averaging to smooth entry points rather than lump‑sum buying at peaks. Finally, regularly review the project’s development activity on platforms like GitHub; a steady stream of commits and transparent governance signals health, while prolonged silence may warrant caution.</p>
