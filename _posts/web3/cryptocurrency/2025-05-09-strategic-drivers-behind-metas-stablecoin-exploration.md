### 1. Reducing Transaction Costs and Friction

Meta aims to offer low-fee transfers that bypass legacy banking fees and settlement delays, especially for micropayments. Traditional wire transfers and card networks can levy fees of 1–3% per transaction, whereas stablecoins settle on public blockchains with minimal network costs. By integrating stablecoins into platforms like Instagram and Facebook, Meta could enable near-instant payouts to creators worldwide without exorbitant fees.

### 2. Enhancing User Engagement and Retention

Embedding a native payment token can deepen user engagement by simplifying transactions—such as tipping, creator subscriptions, and in-app purchases—without leaving Meta's ecosystem. Easier, built-in payments may increase time spent on Meta's apps, drive more creator activity, and generate additional on-platform revenue streams.

### 3. Capturing Value Within the Meta Ecosystem

A Meta-branded stablecoin would circulate primarily within its network, allowing the company to capture float income (interest earned on pooled balances) and transaction fees that currently accrue to external payment processors. Over time, this could represent a significant new revenue line, leveraging Meta's vast user base of over 3 billion people.
