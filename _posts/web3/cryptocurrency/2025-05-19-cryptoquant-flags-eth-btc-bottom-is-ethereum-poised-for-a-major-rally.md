<p>Ethereum (ETH) has recently shown signs of a significant rebound against Bitcoin (BTC), with the ETH/BTC ratio surging 38% from a five-year low. This shift suggests that Ethereum may have reached a bottom relative to Bitcoin, potentially heralding a new phase of growth for ETH and the broader altcoin market.</p>



<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading">ETH/BTC Ratio Surges: A Potential Turning Point</h2>



<p>The ETH/BTC ratio, which measures Ethereum&#8217;s performance relative to Bitcoin, has climbed 38% after hitting its lowest point since January 2020. Such historical lows have often preceded substantial rallies in Ethereum&#8217;s favor. On-chain analytics firm CryptoQuant notes that Ethereum has entered an extreme undervaluation zone relative to Bitcoin, based on the ETH/BTC MVRV metric—the first such instance since 2019.</p>



<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Indicators Supporting Ethereum&#8217;s Potential Upswing</h2>



<p>Several key metrics bolster the case for Ethereum&#8217;s resurgence:</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Increased Trading Volume:</strong> The ETH-to-BTC spot trading volume ratio has spiked to 0.89, its highest since August 2024, indicating growing investor interest in Ethereum.</li>



<li><strong>ETF Holdings:</strong> There has been a sharp uptick in the ETF holdings ratio of ETH relative to BTC since late April, suggesting that institutional investors are allocating more resources to Ethereum.</li>



<li><strong>Reduced Selling Pressure:</strong> Exchange inflow data reveals a decline in ETH being sent to exchanges, implying that holders are less inclined to sell, which could support price stability and growth.</li>
</ul>



<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Market Sentiment and the Onset of &#8216;Alt Season&#8217;</h2>



<p>The recent developments have fueled expectations for a new &#8216;alt season,&#8217; a period when alternative cryptocurrencies outperform Bitcoin. CryptoQuant&#8217;s analysis suggests that Ethereum&#8217;s current position could lead the broader altcoin market into a new phase of growth .</p>



<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Conclusion</h2>



<p>Ethereum&#8217;s recent performance against Bitcoin, supported by various on-chain metrics and institutional interest, indicates a potential bottoming out and the beginning of a new growth phase. While market conditions can change rapidly, the current data points to a favorable outlook for Ethereum and possibly the wider altcoin market.</p>
