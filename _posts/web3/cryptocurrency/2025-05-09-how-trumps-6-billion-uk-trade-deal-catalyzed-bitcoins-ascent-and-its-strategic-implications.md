President Trump's announcement of a $6 billion trade agreement with the United Kingdom triggered a remarkable rally in Bitcoin, pushing its price above $100 000 for the first time in three months—a move driven by renewed “risk-on” sentiment and expectations of softer tariff pressures. The trade deal, which raises an estimated $6 billion in tariff revenue while opening $5 billion in new export opportunities, alleviated investor fears about escalating trade tensions and spurred capital flows into both equities and cryptocurrencies. Simultaneously, the Trump administration's March executive order establishing a Strategic Bitcoin Reserve has bolstered the narrative of Bitcoin as a legitimate, government-backed store of value, further underpinning long-term investor confidence. This article explores the background of the trade deal, its immediate impact on Bitcoin markets, the broader policy context, and the implications for investors navigating a crypto-friendly regulatory landscape.
