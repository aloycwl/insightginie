Twenty One Capital, led by Strike founder Jack Mallers and backed by Tether, SoftBank, and Bitfinex, is launching a public Bitcoin treasury vehicle with an initial allocation of 42,000 BTC (approximately $3.9 billion), aiming to deliver capital-efficient exposure and a pure-play Bitcoin investment model via its Nasdaq-listed ticker XXI. The company plans to outpace incumbents like MicroStrategy by maximizing Bitcoin-per-share growth, offering Bitcoin-native financial products (lending, debt/equity instruments, advisory services), and producing original pro-Bitcoin content. Structured as a SPAC merger with Cantor Equity Partners, Twenty One Capital will raise $585 million through convertible notes and PIPE financing to fund further BTC acquisitions. Its strategy pivots on shareholder-friendly mechanisms, operational flexibility, and ecosystem innovation, setting a new benchmark for institutional Bitcoin adoption—albeit with risks around market volatility, regulatory shifts, and execution of its aggressive acquisition roadmap.
