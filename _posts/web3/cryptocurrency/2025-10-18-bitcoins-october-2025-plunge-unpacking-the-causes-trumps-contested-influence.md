The cryptocurrency world collectively held its breath in October 2025 as Bitcoin, the undisputed king of digital assets, experienced a sharp and unsettling decline. What began as a ripple of concern quickly escalated into a torrent of fear, uncertainty, and doubt (FUD), wiping billions off market capitalization and leaving investors scrambling for answers. Was it a natural market correction, a confluence of macroeconomic pressures, or did the shadow of political rhetoric, particularly from figures like Donald Trump, play a significant role? This article delves deep into the multifaceted reasons behind Bitcoin's recent downturn, dissecting the market dynamics, regulatory shifts, and the often-debated 'Trump factor' to provide a comprehensive understanding.
