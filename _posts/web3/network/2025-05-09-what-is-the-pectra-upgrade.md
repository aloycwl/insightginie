The Pectra upgrade is Ethereum's 16th major network update, merging the Prague execution-layer hard fork with the Electra consensus-layer enhancements into a single hard fork known informally as “Pectra.” Activated on May 7, 2025 at block 18,944,512, it represents the largest feature set since the 2022 Merge, incorporating 11 Ethereum Improvement Proposals (EIPs) designed to streamline staking, enhance wallet usability, and lay groundwork for future scalability improvements. Key changes include raising the maximum effective stake per validator from 32 ETH to 2,048 ETH, improving account abstraction for smart-contract wallets, and optimizing data handling for rollups. While not expected to immediately power a price rally, Pectra marks a critical step toward a more efficient, user-friendly, and scalable Ethereum.

Pectra combines two coordinated updates: the Prague hard fork on the execution layer and the Electra upgrade on the consensus layer, hence the portmanteau “Prague/Electra” → “Pectra.” The execution-layer Prague fork implements EIPs that improve node performance and wallet functionality, while the consensus-layer Electra fork refines validator operations and sets the stage for future data-availability schemes like Verkle trees.

### Core Objectives

1. **Streamlined Staking:** Increases the maximum stake per validator from 32 ETH to 2,048 ETH, allowing larger institutional or pooled validators to operate more efficiently.
2. **Account Abstraction:** Enhances smart-contract wallet capabilities, enabling more flexible transaction sponsorship and gas-fee management.
3. **Rollup & Data Handling:** Introduces groundwork for Proto-Danksharding (EIP-4844) and Verkle trees, reducing layer-2 transaction costs and improving L2 throughput.
