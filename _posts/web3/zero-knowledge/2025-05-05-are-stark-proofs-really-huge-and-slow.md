In the world of blockchain scalability, **STARK proofs** (Scalable Transparent Argument of Knowledge) have emerged as a promising cryptographic tool. Designed to offer a transparent and scalable alternative to traditional proof systems, STARKs are gaining traction for their potential in enhancing the security and efficiency of blockchain networks.

However, there is a prevalent misconception that STARK proofs are always large and slow, making them impractical for real-world applications. While it's true that earlier versions of STARK proofs faced limitations in terms of size and speed, advancements in the technology have significantly reduced these concerns. In this article, we'll explore what STARK proofs are, clarify common misunderstandings, and provide insights into how they are evolving to become faster and more efficient.
