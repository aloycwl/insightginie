Sovereign rollups have emerged as a promising blockchain scalability solution, offering increased independence and flexibility compared to traditional rollups. However, a common misconception is that sovereign rollups inherit the security of the base layer's decentralized applications (DA) for data availability (DA) security. In reality, sovereign rollups do not inherit DA security automatically, and the security model for these rollups requires a more nuanced understanding.

This guide will explore the concept of sovereign rollups, the importance of data availability (DA) in blockchain security, and why sovereign rollups do not automatically inherit DA security. By addressing common misunderstandings, we aim to clarify the relationship between sovereign rollups and DA security, providing a comprehensive understanding of this crucial blockchain concept.
