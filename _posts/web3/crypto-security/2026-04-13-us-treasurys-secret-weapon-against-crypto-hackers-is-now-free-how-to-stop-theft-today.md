<ul>

<li><strong>Flow Tracing:</strong> Visualizes the movement of funds from a source address through various hops, highlighting potential mixing services.</li>
<li><strong>Risk Scoring:</strong> Assigns a dynamic risk score to addresses based on their interaction history with illicit entities.</li>
<li><strong>Automated Alerts:</strong> For developers integrating the API, real-time notifications trigger when a user interacts with a flagged address.</li>
</ul>
<p>This suite of tools effectively turns every user into a node in a global defense network. By making these insights free, the Treasury ensures that the cost of defense is no longer a barrier to entry for security.</p>

<h3>Do I need technical coding knowledge to use these tools?</h3>
<p>Not necessarily. The Treasury provides user-friendly web interfaces where you can search wallet addresses manually. However, for automated protection within an app or exchange, basic technical knowledge is required to integrate the provided APIs.</p>
<h3>Will using these tools slow down my transactions?</h3>
<p>When integrated correctly, the latency is negligible. The APIs are designed for high-frequency trading environments and real-time screening, ensuring that security checks happen in milliseconds without impacting user experience.</p>
<h3>Does this mean the government is tracking my personal wallet?</h3>

It does not inherently grant the government access to your private keys or real-time monitoring of your personal activity unless you are interacting with flagged entities.</p>
<h3>How often is the database updated?</h3>
<p>The Treasury updates its sanctions lists and associated blockchain data in real-time or near real-time as new designations are made and new intelligence is verified. This ensures that the &#8220;secret weapon&#8221; remains effective against emerging threats.</p>
