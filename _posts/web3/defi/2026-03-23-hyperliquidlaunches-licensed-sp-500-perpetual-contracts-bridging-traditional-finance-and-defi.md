<h1>Hyperliquid Launches Licensed S&#038;P 500 Perpetual Contracts: Bridging Traditional Finance and DeFi</h1>

<h2>What Are Perpetual Contracts?</h2>
<p>Perpetual contracts, often referred to as &#8220;perps,&#8221; are a type of derivative that allows traders to speculate on the price of an underlying asset without an expiry date. Unlike traditional futures, which settle on a specific date, perps use a funding rate mechanism to keep the contract price anchored to the spot price of the underlying asset. This structure makes them ideal for continuous trading and has contributed to their popularity in cryptocurrency markets.</p>
<p>Key characteristics of perpetual contracts include:</p>
<ul>
<li>No expiration date – positions can be held indefinitely.</li>
<li>Funding payments exchanged between long and short positions every few hours.</li>
<li>High leverage options, often up to 100x or more depending on the platform.</li>
<li>Ability to go both long and short, enabling profit in rising or falling markets.</li>
</ul>
<h2>Hyperliquid’s Licensed Approach</h2>

<h2>Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)</h2>
<h3>What is a perpetual contract?</h3>
<p>A perpetual contract is a type of derivative that allows traders to speculate on an asset’s price without an expiration date. Instead of expiring, the contract uses a funding rate mechanism to keep its price aligned with the underlying spot market.</p>
<h3>Is the S&#038;P 500 perpetual on Hyperliquid regulated?</h3>
<p>Yes. Hyperliquid has partnered with a licensed entity to obtain the necessary permissions to list the S&#038;P 500 index as an underlying asset, providing regulatory oversight and investor protection.</p>
<h3>What collateral can I use to trade the S&#038;P 500 perpetual?</h3>
<p>Accepted collateral typically includes major stablecoins such as USDC and USDT, as well as leading cryptocurrencies like ETH. Users should check the platform’s deposit page for the most current list.</p>
<h3>How does the funding rate work?</h3>
<p>Funding rates are periodic payments exchanged between long and short position holders. If the market is bullish (more longs than shorts), longs pay shorts; if bearish, shorts pay longs. The rate is calculated based on the difference between the perpetual contract price and the index price.</p>
<h3>Can I use leverage on this product?</h3>
<p>Yes. Hyperliquid offers adjustable leverage levels, allowing traders to increase their exposure relative to their deposited margin. Higher leverage increases both potential profit and risk, so proper risk management is essential.</p>
<h3>Are there any geographic restrictions?</h3>
<p>Access may be subject to local regulations. Users should verify that trading crypto derivatives is permitted in their jurisdiction before opening an account.</p>
<h3>How do I close my position?</h3>
<p>Positions can be closed at any time by submitting an opposite order of equal size (e.g., selling to close a long). The platform will realize any profit or loss based on the current mark‑to‑market price and adjust the margin accordingly.</p>
<h3>What happens if the funding rate becomes extremely high?</h3>
<p>Extremely high funding rates can increase the cost of holding a position. Traders may choose to reduce leverage, close the position, or switch to the opposite side to receive funding payments instead of paying them.</p>
<h3>Does Hyperliquid offer stop‑loss or take‑profit orders?</h3>
<p>Yes. The platform includes advanced order types such as stop‑loss, take‑profit, and conditional orders to help traders automate risk management.</p>
