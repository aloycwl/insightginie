<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Introduction</h2>

<p>Quantitative AI is at the intersection of <strong>finance, data science, and artificial intelligence</strong>. It leverages mathematical models, statistical methods, and machine learning algorithms to analyze financial markets and implement trading strategies systematically. Unlike discretionary trading (based on human intuition), Quantitative AI relies on <strong>rules, data, and automation</strong>.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">1. What is Quantitative Trading?</h2>

<p>Quantitative trading (or “quant trading”) is the use of mathematical and computational techniques to make trading decisions. Instead of subjective judgment, quants rely on:</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Historical data analysis</strong></li>

<li><strong>Mathematical models</strong></li>

<li><strong>Algorithmic execution</strong></li>
</ul>

<p>Quant trading can be as simple as a moving average crossover strategy or as complex as deep reinforcement learning models predicting market microstructure.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">2. Systematic Strategies</h2>

<p>A <strong>systematic strategy</strong> is one that is fully defined by a set of rules, with no room for human override during execution. These strategies are <strong>repeatable, backtestable, and scalable</strong>.</p>

<p>Types of systematic strategies include:</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Momentum</strong> – Buying assets that are trending upward and selling those trending downward.</li>

<li><strong>Mean Reversion</strong> – Betting prices will revert back to historical averages.</li>

<li><strong>Arbitrage</strong> – Exploiting price differences across markets or instruments.</li>

<li><strong>Statistical Learning Models</strong> – Using advanced data-driven methods to generate trading signals.</li>
</ul>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">3. Where Does AI Fit In?</h2>

<p>Artificial Intelligence (AI) enhances quant strategies by introducing <strong>adaptivity and predictive power</strong>. Specifically:</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Machine Learning (ML):</strong> Learns patterns from historical market data (e.g., Random Forests, XGBoost).</li>

<li><strong>Deep Learning (DL):</strong> Captures complex nonlinear relationships (e.g., LSTM for time-series forecasting, Transformers for sequence modeling).</li>

<li><strong>Hybrid Approaches:</strong> Combine rule-based strategies with ML/DL models (e.g., using ML to select which momentum signals to trade).</li>
</ul>

<p>The taxonomy can be seen as:</p>

<ol class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Rules-Based Strategies</strong> (deterministic, human-designed)</li>

<li><strong>ML-Based Strategies</strong> (data-driven, adaptive)</li>

<li><strong>Hybrid Strategies</strong> (mix of rules and AI for robustness)</li>
</ol>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">4. Why Taxonomy Matters</h2>

<p>Understanding the <strong>taxonomy of strategies</strong> helps practitioners categorize their research and avoid confusion. For example:</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>If your strategy uses <strong>hard-coded indicators</strong>, it’s rule-based.</li>

<li>If your strategy <strong>learns signal thresholds from data</strong>, it’s ML-based.</li>

<li>If you combine <strong>both approaches</strong>, it’s hybrid.</li>
</ul>

<p>This framework ensures clarity when building, testing, and presenting strategies.</p>
