<p>In today&#8217;s fast-paced forex markets, <strong>precision timing</strong> and <strong>trend alignment</strong> are key to successful trading. This daily strategy leverages <strong>multi-timeframe analysis</strong>, combining the <strong>H4 chart</strong> for trend direction with the <strong>M15 chart</strong> for pinpoint entry setups.</p>

<p>By blending higher timeframe trend logic with lower timeframe entries—such as <strong>double highs/lows</strong>, <strong>clean left structures</strong>, or <strong>breaks in structure (BoS)</strong>—traders can achieve <strong>greater confidence</strong> in their positions while minimizing risk and maximizing reward.</p>

<p>This professional, detailed guide will walk you through the methodology, setup conditions, stop-loss and take-profit placement, and profit securing techniques that make this strategy reliable for both swing traders and intraday participants.</p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Step-by-Step Guide</strong></h3>

<h4 class="wp-block-heading">1. <strong>Determine Trend Using the H4 Chart</strong></h4>

<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Look at <strong>swing structure</strong>: higher highs = bullish, lower lows = bearish.</li>

<li>Mark key <strong>support/resistance zones</strong>.</li>

<li>Determine whether the market is <strong>trending</strong> or <strong>ranging</strong>.</li>
</ul>

<p>🟢 <strong>If H4 is bullish</strong>, prepare to <strong>buy</strong> on M15<br>🔴 <strong>If H4 is bearish</strong>, prepare to <strong>sell</strong> on M15</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h4 class="wp-block-heading">2. <strong>Setup Conditions on the M15 Chart</strong></h4>

<p>Once the H4 direction is clear, zoom into M15 and wait for one of the following:</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Double High (for sells)</strong> or <strong>Double Low (for buys)</strong>: Identifies exhaustion or liquidity trap</li>

<li><strong>Clean Left</strong>: A structure where the left side is free of recent traffic, indicating potential for smooth move</li>

<li><strong>Break in Structure (BoS)</strong>: Price breaks a previous swing high/low, then returns to <strong>tap</strong> into that zone</li>
</ul>

<p>📌 <strong>Entry Trigger</strong>: Candle that forms the double top/bottom or taps back into the BoS zone.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h4 class="wp-block-heading">3. <strong>Entry and Stop-Loss Placement</strong></h4>

<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Enter</strong> on the confirmation candle (e.g., bearish engulfing after double top, bullish engulfing after double bottom)</li>

<li><strong>Place stop-loss</strong> slightly above (or below) the M15 entry candle&#8217;s wick – ensures you&#8217;re not stopped by noise.</li>
</ul>

<p>✅ This tight SL placement enhances your <strong>risk-to-reward ratio</strong> and gives better control over drawdown.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h4 class="wp-block-heading">4. <strong>Take-Profit (TP) Strategy</strong></h4>

<p>Use the H4 chart to determine realistic profit zones:</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Identify <strong>previous highs/lows</strong>, fair value gaps, or key resistance/support</li>

<li><strong>Measure risk-to-reward</strong> for each TP zone:
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>TP1: <strong>1:1</strong>, take <strong>50%</strong></li>

<li>TP2: <strong>1:2</strong>, take <strong>20%</strong></li>

<li>TP3: <strong>Leave runner</strong> to trail with structure</li>
</ul>
</li>

<li>Always <strong>review stop-loss range</strong> before entry to confirm you&#8217;re not risking too much relative to reward</li>
</ul>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Risk Management Tips</strong></h3>

<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Never risk more than <strong>1-2% per trade</strong></li>

<li>Always <strong>measure SL distance</strong> before entry — don’t force trades with wide SLs</li>

<li>Use <strong>trade journaling</strong> to document which setups yield best results (double top vs BoS, etc.)</li>

<li>Backtest and forward test before scaling position size</li>
</ul>
