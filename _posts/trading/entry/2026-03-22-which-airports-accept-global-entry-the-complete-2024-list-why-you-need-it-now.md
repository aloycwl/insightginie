<h2>What Exactly Is Global Entry?</h2>
<p>Before diving into the airport list, it is crucial to understand the product. <strong>Global Entry</strong> is a U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) program that allows expedited clearance for pre-approved, low-risk travelers upon arrival in the United States. While many confuse it with TSA PreCheck, Global Entry is the superior tier for international travelers because it includes TSA PreCheck benefits plus expedited customs processing.</p>
<p>Members use automated kiosks at select airports, bypassing the traditional paper declaration forms and long immigration lines. The program uses biometric data (fingerprints and photo) to verify identity, making the process both faster and more secure.</p>
<h2>Which Airports Accept Global Entry? The Comprehensive List</h2>
<p>One of the most common misconceptions is that Global Entry is limited to a handful of major hubs like JFK or LAX. In reality, the program has expanded aggressively. As of 2024, Global Entry kiosks or facial recognition lanes are available at over 75 international airports across the United States and in select preclearance locations abroad.</p>

<h2>Global Entry vs. TSA PreCheck: A Quick Comparison</h2>
<p>Travelers often hesitate, unsure which program to choose. Let&#8217;s clarify:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>TSA PreCheck:</strong> Domestic security only. No customs benefits. Cost: ~$78/5 years.</li>
<li><strong>Global Entry:</strong> International customs + Domestic security (TSA PreCheck included). Cost: $100/5 years.</li>
<li><strong>Verdict:</strong> Unless you <em>never</em> leave the United States, Global Entry is the superior choice. Even if you only take one international trip in five years, the time saved on that single return leg justifies the extra $22.</li>
</ul>
<h2>How to Apply: A Step-by-Step Guide</h2>
<p>Getting approved requires passing a rigorous background check. Here is the process:</p>
<ol>
<li><strong>Create an Account:</strong> Visit the Trusted Traveler Programs (TTP) website and create a login.</li>
<li><strong>Submit Application:</strong> Fill out the Global Entry application. You will need your passport and driver&#8217;s license details. There is a non-refundable $100 application fee.</li>
<li><strong>Wait for Conditional Approval:</strong> This can take anywhere from a few days to several months. Do not book an interview until you receive this status.</li>
<li><strong>Schedule an Interview:</strong> Once conditionally approved, schedule an interview at an enrollment center. <em>Pro Tip:</em> If your local airport has a long wait time, look for &#8220;Enrollment on Arrival&#8221; locations. These allow you to complete your interview immediately upon returning from an international trip, often with no appointment needed.</li>
<li><strong>Receive Your PASSID:</strong> After a successful interview, your Known Traveler Number (KTN) will be active. Add this number to your airline profiles to automatically trigger TSA PreCheck on your boarding passes.</li>
</ol>

<h2>Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)</h2>
<h3>Can I use Global Entry at any U.S. airport?</h3>
<p>No, Global Entry is specifically for international arrivals. You use it when entering the U.S. from abroad. However, the TSA PreCheck benefit included with Global Entry works at domestic security checkpoints at over 200 U.S. airports.</p>
<h3>How long does Global Entry last?</h3>
<p>Global Entry membership is valid for 5 years from the date of your approval (not the application date). You can renew it starting one year before it expires.</p>
<h3>What happens if I am denied Global Entry?</h3>
<p>If denied, you will receive a letter explaining the general reason (e.g., incomplete info, criminal record). You can request a review, but the $100 fee is generally non-refundable. In some cases, you may be offered TSA PreCheck instead.</p>
<h3>Do I need my physical Global Entry card to fly?</h3>
<p>For air travel, no. Your Known Traveler Number (KTN) is linked to your airline reservation. You only need the physical card if you are using Global Entry at land borders (like NEXUS/SENTRI lanes) or sea ports.</p>
<h3>Can I use Global Entry if I am not a U.S. citizen?</h3>
<p>Yes. Citizens of several countries including Germany, Mexico, the UK, Canada, and others are eligible to apply for Global Entry, provided they meet all requirements.</p>
