<h1>Global Entry Is Back Online: What It Means for Travelers Amid Rising TSA Wait Times</h1>
<p>After a temporary suspension caused by the recent federal government shutdown, the Global Entry program has been restored, offering expedited clearance for pre‑approved international travelers. The resumption comes at a critical moment when TSA checkpoint lines are swelling, leaving many passengers facing longer waits and heightened stress. Understanding how Global Entry fits into the current security landscape can help travelers make smarter decisions and reduce time spent in line.</p>
<h2>Why Global Entry Went Offline</h2>
<p>The shutdown affected numerous Department of Homeland Security (DHS) functions, including the processing and maintenance of Global Entry enrollment centers. When funding lapsed, agents tasked with conducting background checks and updating the Trusted Traveler Program database were placed on furlough, causing the system to pause new approvals and, in some locations, suspend kiosk operations. Although existing members could still use their cards at participating airports, the lack of support staff led to intermittent service disruptions that frustrated frequent flyers.</p>
<h2>Impact of the Shutdown on TSA Wait Times</h2>
<p>With TSA officers also facing staffing challenges during the shutdown, average wait times at security checkpoints rose sharply. Data from the Transportation Security Administration showed that peak‑hour waits increased from an average of 15 minutes to over 30 minutes at major hubs such as Atlanta, Chicago O’Hare, and Los Angeles. The combination of reduced TSA personnel and the temporary unavailability of Global Entry kiosks created a perfect storm, prompting many travelers to seek alternative ways to breeze through security.</p>
<h2>How Global Entry Works</h2>
<p>Global Entry is a U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) program that allows low‑risk travelers to receive expedited clearance when entering the United States from abroad. Members use automated kiosks at participating airports, scan their passport or U.S. permanent resident card, provide fingerprint verification, and complete a customs declaration electronically. The process typically takes less than five minutes, compared to the traditional inspection that can exceed 20 minutes during busy periods.</p>
<p>In addition to international arrival benefits, Global Entry includes TSA PreCheck eligibility, letting members enjoy expedited domestic screening with benefits such as keeping shoes, belts, and light jackets on, and leaving laptops and liquids in their carry‑on bags.</p>
<h2>Benefits of Global Entry’s Return</h2>
<ul>
<li>Restored access to kiosks at over 50 U.S. airports, reducing international arrival processing time.</li>
<li>Reinstated TSA PreCheck privileges, cutting domestic security waits.</li>
<li>Renewed confidence for frequent flyers who rely on predictable travel schedules.</li>
<li>Potential relief for TSA checkpoints as some passengers shift to the expedited lane, easing overall congestion.</li>
</ul>
<h2>Comparing Wait Times: Global Entry vs. TSA PreCheck vs. Regular Lanes</h2>
<p>To illustrate the impact, consider a typical mid‑morning departure at Dallas/Fort Worth International Airport:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Regular TSA lane:</strong> Average wait 28 minutes, with variability depending on flight volume.</li>
<li><strong>TSA PreCheck lane:</strong> Average wait 7 minutes, thanks to limited‑item screening and dedicated lanes.</li>
<li><strong>Global Entry kiosk (arrival):</strong> Average processing time 4 minutes after deplaning.</li>
<li><strong>Combined Global Entry + TSA PreCheck:</strong> Total door‑to‑gate time can be under 15 minutes for international arrivals connecting domestically.</li>
</ul>
<h2>Practical Tips for Travelers</h2>
<p>Even with Global Entry back online, smart planning can further reduce airport stress. Here are actionable strategies:</p>
<ul>
<li>Enroll or renew your Global Entry membership well before your trip; the background check can take several weeks.</li>
<li>Schedule your interview at an enrollment center that offers same‑day availability; many centers now offer online appointment booking.</li>
<li>Always carry your Global Entry card and a valid passport; the kiosk will not function without both.</li>
<li>If you are traveling domestically only, consider applying for TSA PreCheck directly, which shares the same enrollment process.</li>
<li>Monitor real‑time wait times via airport apps or the TSA’s MyTSA service to choose the least congested checkpoint.</li>
<li>During peak travel periods, arrive at least 90 minutes before domestic flights and two hours before international departures to accommodate unexpected delays.</li>
</ul>

During the shutdown, she reported that her typical 45‑minute customs clearance stretched to over 90 minutes, causing her to miss connecting flights. After Global Entry resumed, she cleared customs in under six minutes and made her connection with ample time to spare.</p>

<h2>Future Outlook</h2>
<p>Looking ahead, the Department of Homeland Security has announced plans to expand Global Entry enrollment centers and invest in upgraded kiosk technology that includes facial recognition. These enhancements aim to further reduce processing times and improve resilience against future funding lapses. Travelers should stay informed about policy updates, as any changes to enrollment fees or eligibility criteria could affect accessibility.</p>
<h2>Conclusion</h2>
<p>The return of Global Entry arrives just as travelers grapple with longer TSA wait times stemming from the recent shutdown. By providing a fast, reliable pathway through both international customs and domestic security, Global Entry helps alleviate congestion, reduces travel‑related anxiety, and restores a measure of predictability to air travel. For frequent flyers, business travelers, and families alike, taking advantage of this program—or its TSA PreCheck component—can translate into significant time savings and a smoother journey from curb to gate. Travelers who enroll now will benefit from shorter lines and greater peace of mind for the rest of the year.</p>
<h2>FAQ</h2>
<div class='faq'>
<h3>What is Global Entry and who is eligible?</h3>
<p>Global Entry is a trusted traveler program run by U.S. Customs and Border Protection that offers expedited clearance for pre‑approved, low‑risk travelers arriving in the United States. U.S. citizens, U.S. lawful permanent residents, and citizens of certain partner countries (such as the United Kingdom, Germany, the Netherlands, and South Korea) may apply, provided they pass a rigorous background check and in‑person interview.</p>
<h3>How long does it take to get approved for Global Entry?</h3>
<p>After submitting an online application, applicants typically wait a few weeks to several months for a conditional approval notice. Once conditionally approved, they must schedule an interview at an enrollment center; interview availability varies by location. After a successful interview, final approval and card issuance usually occur within 7‑10 business days.</p>
<h3>Can I use Global Entry for domestic flights?</h3>
<p>While Global Entry itself focuses on international arrivals, every approved member automatically receives TSA PreCheck eligibility, which can be used for domestic flights. Simply add your Known Traveler Number (KTN) to your airline reservation to access the expedited security lane.</p>
<h3>What should I do if the Global Entry kiosk is not working at my airport?</h3>
<p>First, ensure you have both your Global Entry card and a valid passport. If the kiosk remains unresponsive, seek assistance from a CBP officer at the arrivals area; they can process you manually or direct you to an alternative kiosk. Reporting the issue helps authorities address technical problems promptly.</p>
<h3>Are there any fees associated with Global Entry?</h3>
<p>The application fee for Global Entry is $100, which covers a five‑year membership. Some credit cards offer reimbursement of this fee as a benefit, so check your card’s terms before paying.</p>
<h3>How does Global Entry compare to other trusted traveler programs like NEXUS or SENTRI?</h3>
<p>NEXUS is designed for travel between the U.S. and Canada, offering expedited processing at land borders, airports, and marine ports. SENTRI focuses on U.S.–Mexico land crossings. Global Entry provides the broadest international airport coverage and includes TSA PreCheck, making it the most versatile option for frequent air travelers.</p>
</div>
