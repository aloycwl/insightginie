<h1>Global Entry Fees Increase for the First Time: What Travelers Need to Know in 2025</h1>
<p>The U.S. Customs and Border Protection agency announced the first increase in Global Entry fees since the program launched, raising the cost from $100 to $120 for a five-year membership. This change affects millions of frequent flyers who rely on expedited customs processing at airports across the country. In this article we break down what the new fee means, why it happened, who it affects, and whether the program still delivers enough value to justify the expense. We also share practical tips to offset the higher cost and look at alternatives for travelers who want faster airport experiences without paying more.</p>
<h2>What Is Global Entry?</h2>
<p>Global Entry is a trusted traveler program administered by U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) that allows pre-approved, low-risk travelers to skip the regular customs lines and use automated kiosks upon arrival in the United States. Members also receive TSA PreCheck benefits, which let them keep shoes, belts, and light jackets on during security screening and enjoy faster access to domestic flight lanes. The application process includes an online form, a background check, and an in-person interview at a Global Entry enrollment center. Once approved, the membership is valid for five years and can be renewed online.</p>
<h2>A Historic Fee Increase: The Details</h2>
<p>The fee adjustment marks the first time the price has changed since Global Entry began in 2008. The new rate of $120 represents a 20 percent increase over the previous $100 fee. CBP cited rising operational costs, technology upgrades for the kiosk system, and increased demand for enrollment appointments as the primary drivers behind the decision. The agency emphasized that the adjustment aligns the program’s pricing with the cost of providing secure, efficient service to a growing member base.</p>
<h2>Why Did the Fee Go Up?</h2>
<p>Several factors contributed to the fee hike. First, the cost of maintaining and upgrading the biometric kiosks at more than 60 airports has risen due to inflation and supply-chain pressures. Second, CBP has invested in enhanced security screening technologies, including facial recognition and advanced data analytics, to keep the program safe and effective. Third, the volume of applications has surged, leading to longer wait times for interviews and requiring additional staffing at enrollment centers. Finally, the agency conducted a cost-recovery analysis that showed the existing fee no longer covered the full expense of administering the program.</p>
<h2>Breakdown of the New Global Entry Cost</h2>
<p>The new Global Entry fee is a flat $120 for a five-year term, which works out to $24 per year. When broken down further, the cost per trip can be surprisingly low for frequent travelers. For example, a business traveler who takes four international round-trips per year saves roughly 30 minutes per customs stop, amounting to two hours of saved time annually. At $24 per year, the effective cost of that time saved is less than $1 per hour. Families can also benefit, as children under 18 can be added to a parent&#8217;s membership at no extra charge, making the per-person cost even lower for households.</p>
<h2>Who Is Affected by the Increase?</h2>
<p>The fee increase applies to all new applications submitted on or after the effective date, as well as renewals processed after that date. Existing members whose current five-year term is not yet up for renewal will continue to pay the old $100 rate until their next renewal window. This means that travelers who recently joined or renewed before the change can lock in the lower price for another five years. However, anyone planning to apply for the first time or renew after the fee change will need to budget the additional $20.</p>
<h2>Is Global Entry Still Worth It? A Cost-Benefit Analysis</h2>
<p>To evaluate the value proposition, consider both tangible and intangible benefits. Tangible benefits include time saved at customs and TSA PreCheck lanes, which can translate into lower stress and a smoother travel experience. Intangible benefits encompass the peace of mind that comes with knowing you have a trusted traveler status and the ability to use Global Entry kiosks at participating international airports for expedited re-entry. When comparing the $120 fee to alternatives such as paying for premium airport lounge access or purchasing separate TSA PreCheck ($85 for five years), Global Entry often remains the more economical choice, especially for those who travel internationally at least once a year.</p>
<h2>How to Make the Most of Your Membership</h2>
<p>Maximize your Global Entry membership by following these practical steps: Always carry your Global Entry card or use the mobile app to confirm your status before heading to the airport. Arrive at the enrollment center with all required documentation, including a valid passport and a second form of ID, to avoid delays during the interview. Keep your information up to date in the Trusted Traveler Programs (TTP) portal to ensure smooth renewals. Take advantage of the automatic TSA PreCheck inclusion by adding your Known Traveler Number (KTN) to every airline reservation. Finally, consider combining Global Entry with other programs like CLEAR or NEXUS if you frequently travel to specific regions, as the benefits can stack for even greater time savings.</p>
<h2>Alternatives and Complementary Programs</h2>
<p>If the new fee feels steep, there are several alternatives worth exploring. TSA PreCheck alone costs $85 for five years and provides expedited security screening but does not include customs benefits. The NEXUS program, priced at $50 for five years, offers similar advantages to Global Entry plus expedited processing at Canadian land borders and airports, making it ideal for frequent cross-border travelers. The SENTRI program, also $50 for five years, focuses on expedited processing at U.S.-Mexico land borders. For travelers who prefer a purely private solution, CLEAR offers biometric identity verification at select airports and stadiums for $179 per year, though it does not replace customs clearance.</p>
<h2>Future Outlook: Will Fees Rise Again?</h2>
<p>While CBP has stated that the current fee adjustment is intended to cover costs for the near future, travelers should stay alert for potential periodic reviews. Factors such as technological advancements, changes in immigration policy, and fluctuations in enrollment volume could prompt another fee adjustment in the coming years. Keeping an eye on official announcements from the Department of Homeland Security and enrolling during promotional periods—when some credit cards offer statement credits for the application fee—can help mitigate future increases.</p>
<h2>Conclusion</h2>
<p>The first-ever increase in Global Entry fees signals a maturing program that continues to evolve to meet the demands of modern air travel. Although the $20 bump may seem unwelcome at first glance, the program still delivers substantial time savings, convenience, and security benefits for a relatively low annual cost. By understanding the reasons behind the change, evaluating your personal travel patterns, and leveraging strategies to offset the expense, you can decide whether Global Entry remains a smart investment for your travel portfolio. For most frequent international flyers, the answer is likely yes.</p>
<h2>FAQ</h2>
<h3>What is the new Global Entry fee?</h3>
<p>The new fee is $120 for a five-year membership, up from the previous $100.</p>
<h3>When does the fee increase take effect?</h3>
<p>The increase applies to applications and renewals submitted on or after the effective date announced by CBP, which was early 2025.</p>
<h3>Do I have to pay the new fee if I already have Global Entry?</h3>
<p>No. Current members will continue to pay the $100 rate until their existing five-year term expires and they seek renewal.</p>
<h3>Can I add family members to my Global Entry account?</h3>
<p>Yes. Children under 18 can be added to a parent&#8217;s membership at no extra cost, while spouses or partners must submit their own applications and pay the applicable fee.</p>
<h3>Is there a way to get a discount or fee waiver?</h3>
<p>Some premium credit cards offer a statement credit that covers the Global Entry application fee, either in full or partially. Check your card&#8217;s benefits portal for details.</p>
<h3>How long does the interview process take?</h3>
<p>Interview appointments typically last 15-20 minutes, although wait times for scheduling can vary based on location and demand.</p>
<h3>What happens if I miss my renewal deadline?</h3>
<p>If your membership expires, you must submit a new application and pay the current fee. There is no grace period, so it’s advisable to start the renewal process at least 60 days before expiration.</p>
<h3>Does Global Entry work at all U.S. airports?</h3>
<p>Global Entry kiosks are available at more than 60 major international airports across the United States. Smaller regional airports may not have kiosks, but members can still use the dedicated lanes when available.</p>
<h3>Should I consider NEXUS or SENTRI instead?</h3>
<p>If you frequently travel to Canada, NEXUS offers similar benefits at a lower cost. For regular cross-border travel via Mexico, SENTRI provides comparable advantages. Evaluate your primary travel destinations to choose the program that best fits your needs.</p>
