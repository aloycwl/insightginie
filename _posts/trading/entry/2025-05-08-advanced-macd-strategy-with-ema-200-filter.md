The Moving Average Convergence Divergence (MACD) is one of the most versatile and widely used indicators in technical analysis. When paired with a long-term trend filter like the **Exponential Moving Average (EMA-200)**, it becomes a highly reliable strategy for identifying **momentum shifts in the direction of the dominant trend**.

This article outlines a refined trading approach using the MACD crossover in conjunction with the EMA-200 as a trend filter. The strategy specifically looks for:

* **Buy signals** when the MACD line crosses **under the 50% histogram** and the **price is above EMA-200**
* **Sell signals** when the MACD line crosses **above the 50% histogram** and the **price is below EMA-200**

This creates a powerful, trend-confirmed system with reduced noise and increased accuracy.

---

### **Understanding the Indicators**

#### ✅ MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence)

The MACD is calculated by subtracting the 26-period EMA from the 12-period EMA. It includes:

* **MACD Line**
* **Signal Line** (9-period EMA of MACD Line)
* **Histogram** (difference between MACD Line and Signal Line)

A **crossover** between the MACD and Signal Line, especially in relation to the **50% level of the histogram**, reveals momentum strength.

#### ✅ EMA-200 (200-Period Exponential Moving Average)

EMA-200 is a long-term moving average that represents the dominant trend. It acts as a dynamic support/resistance line.

* **Price above EMA-200** = Bullish trend
* **Price below EMA-200** = Bearish trend

---

### **Core Strategy Rules**

#### 🟩 **Buy Signal**

* MACD Line **crosses below the 50% line** of the histogram (indicating waning bearish momentum or hidden bullish pressure)
* Price is **above the EMA-200**
* Optional: Confirm with a bullish candlestick pattern (e.g., engulfing or hammer)

#### 🟥 **Sell Signal**

* MACD Line **crosses above the 50% line** of the histogram (indicating waning bullish momentum or hidden bearish pressure)
* Price is **below the EMA-200**
* Optional: Confirm with a bearish candlestick pattern (e.g., shooting star or bearish engulfing)

---

### **Why This Works**

| Element | Purpose |
| --- | --- |
| MACD Histogram 50% Crossover | Identifies deeper momentum shift |
| EMA-200 Trend Filter | Ensures you only trade in direction of trend |
| Price Action Confirmation | Adds extra reliability to your entries |

This filters out **false signals** that often occur in choppy, sideways markets and ensures **alignment with higher time-frame trends**.

---

### **Step-by-Step Entry Guide**

#### ✅ Buy Setup:

1. Check if price is **above the EMA-200**
2. Observe the MACD Line as it **crosses below 50% of the histogram**
3. Confirm bullish price behavior (optional)
4. Enter **long** position
5. Place **stop-loss** below recent swing low
6. Set **take-profit** based on risk-reward ratio (1:2 or 1:3) or next resistance

#### ✅ Sell Setup:

1. Check if price is **below the EMA-200**
2. Observe the MACD Line as it **crosses above 50% of the histogram**
3. Confirm bearish price behavior (optional)
4. Enter **short** position
5. Place **stop-loss** above recent swing high
6. Set **take-profit** based on risk-reward ratio or next support
