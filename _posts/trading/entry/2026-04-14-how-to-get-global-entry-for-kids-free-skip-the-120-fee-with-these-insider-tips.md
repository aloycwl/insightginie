<h1>How to Get Global Entry for Kids Free: Skip the $120 Fee with These Insider Tips</h1>
<p>International travel with children can be challenging enough without the added stress of long customs lines. Global Entry offers a solution, allowing families to breeze through immigration and customs when returning to the United States. However, the $120 application fee per person can quickly add up for families with multiple children. The good news? There are several legitimate ways to get Global Entry for your kids without paying this fee. In this comprehensive guide, we&#8217;ll reveal the strategies, programs, and loopholes that can save your family hundreds of dollars while enjoying the benefits of expedited travel.</p>
<h2>What is Global Entry and Why It&#8217;s Valuable for Families</h2>
<p>Global Entry is a U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) program that allows pre-approved, low-risk travelers to enjoy expedited clearance when arriving in the United States. Participants use automated kiosks at select airports to complete their customs declaration and fingerprint verification, significantly reducing wait times that can often exceed an hour during peak travel seasons.</p>
<p>For families, Global Entry offers numerous benefits beyond just skipping long lines:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Reduced stress</strong>: Traveling with tired, hungry children is challenging enough without adding hours of waiting in crowded customs halls.</li>
<li><strong>Faster connections</strong>: Shorter processing times mean less risk of missing connecting flights, especially important when traveling internationally with kids.</li>
<li><strong>Streamlined experience</strong>: The automated kiosks are simple to use, making the process straightforward even for children who can navigate touchscreens.</li>
<li><strong>TSA PreCheck inclusion</strong> Global Entry membership also includes TSA PreCheck, allowing families to use dedicated security lanes at domestic airports.</li>
</ul>
<p>While the $120 fee per applicant may seem reasonable for adults, when you multiply it by each family member, the cost can become prohibitive. This is where our money-saving strategies come into play.</p>
<h3>Understanding the Global Entry Application Process</h3>
<p>Before diving into the fee-saving methods, it&#8217;s essential to understand the standard Global Entry application process:</p>
<ol>
<li>Create a Trusted Traveler Programs (TTP) account on the CBP website</li>
<li>Complete the online application with personal information and travel history</li>
<li>Pay the $120 application fee for each applicant</li>
<li>Schedule an in-person interview at an enrollment center</li>
<li>Receive conditional approval pending completion of background checks</li>
<li>Receive your Known Traveler Number (KTN) via email, typically within a few weeks</li>
</ol>
<p>With this process in mind, let&#8217;s explore how to minimize or eliminate the application fee for your children.</p>
<h2>Official Waivers for Children</h2>
<p>The most straightforward way to get Global Entry for kids without paying the fee is through official waiver programs. While CBP doesn&#8217;t offer blanket fee waivers for all children, there are specific circumstances under which the application fee may be waived:</p>
<h3>Children of Active-Duty Military Personnel</h3>
<p>Children of active-duty U.S. military personnel may be eligible for fee waivers when applying for Global Entry. To qualify, you&#8217;ll need to provide documentation of the military service member&#8217;s status during the application process. This includes:</p>
<ul>
<li>Valid military ID</li>
<li>Orders showing active duty status</li>
<li>DD-214 form for veterans</li>
</ul>
<p>The military family fee waiver extends to spouses and dependent children under 18, making it an excellent option for military families who frequently travel internationally.</p>
<h3>Children of Enrolled Nexus Members</h3>
<p>The Nexus program, which serves U.S. and Canadian citizens/residents who travel frequently between the two countries, offers some fee waiver benefits. While Nexus itself doesn&#8217;t automatically waive Global Entry fees for children, Nexus members sometimes receive promotions or special offers that can be extended to family members.</p>
<p>Additionally, children under 18 of current Global Entry members may receive discounts on their applications during promotional periods. Keep an eye on the CBP website and trusted traveler program newsletters for these limited-time offers.</p>
<h2>Family Application Discounts</h2>

<h3>Seasonal Promotions</h3>
<p>During certain times of the year, CBP and participating organizations may run promotions that include fee waivers or discounts for specific applicant groups, including children. These are typically announced on:</p>
<ul>

<li>Trusted Traveler Program newsletters</li>
</ul>
<p>Sign up for notifications from these sources to be among the first to learn about any limited-time offers that could benefit your family.</p>

<h2>Alternative Programs That Offer Similar Benefits</h2>
<p>When Global Entry fees for children are prohibitive, consider these alternative programs that offer similar expedited travel benefits:</p>
<h3>TSA PreCheck</h3>
<p>TSA PreCheck costs only $85 for five years (significantly less than Global Entry&#8217;s $120 for five years) and provides expedited security screening at domestic airports. While it doesn&#8217;t include customs benefits like Global Entry, it does offer:</p>
<ul>
<li>Keep shoes, belts, and light jackets on</li>
<li>Leave laptops in cases</li>
<li>No need to remove liquids and gels from carry-on bags</li>
</ul>
<p>For families that primarily travel domestically or rarely leave North America, TSA PreCheck may be a more cost-effective alternative to Global Entry for children.</p>
<h3>Clear + TSA PreCheck</h3>
<p>Clear is a separate program that uses biometric verification to expedite identity checks at security checkpoints. When combined with TSA PreCheck, it offers comprehensive benefits:</p>
<ul>
<li>Clear&#8217;s biometric screening at select airports</li>
<li>TSA PreCheck benefits for domestic travel</li>
<li>Combined cost of approximately $189 per year (less than the $120 Global Entry fee for five years when annualized)</li>
</ul>
<p>Clear frequently offers promotions that include family memberships, potentially reducing costs for multiple family members.</p>
<h3>SENTRI and Nexus Programs</h3>
<p>For families frequently traveling to Mexico or Canada, respectively, the SENTRI and Nexus programs may offer better value than Global Entry:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>SENTRI</strong>: For frequent land border crossings into Mexico, offers expedited lanes at land ports of entry</li>
<li><strong>Nexus</strong>: For frequent U.S.-Canada travel, includes air travel benefits and expedited processing at land borders</li>
</ul>
<p>Both programs cost $120 for five years (same as Global Entry) but may be more valuable depending on your travel patterns.</p>
<h2>Common Mistakes to Avoid When Applying</h2>
<p>When seeking Global Entry for children without paying the fee, avoid these common pitfalls:</p>
<h3>Assuming All Children Automatically Qualify</h3>
<p>Many parents mistakenly believe that children under a certain age automatically qualify for Global Entry without an application or fee. This is not the case. All applicants, regardless of age, must complete the application process and pay the fee unless they qualify for a specific waiver program.</p>
<h3>Missing Application Deadlines</h3>
<p>When applying during promotional periods or special enrollment events, missing deadlines can result in missing out on fee waivers. Set reminders for important dates and submit applications well in advance of any deadlines.</p>
<h3>Providing Incomplete Documentation</h3>
<p>When applying for waiver programs, incomplete or missing documentation can result in denial or delays. Ensure you have all required documents ready before beginning the application process.</p>
<h3>Overlooking Annual Fee Credit Cycles</h3>
<p>When using credit card benefits to cover Global Entry fees, remember that these credits typically have annual cycles. If you&#8217;ve already used your credit for yourself, you may need to wait until the next cycle to cover a child&#8217;s application.</p>

<h2>Frequently Asked Questions</h2>
<h3>Q: What is the minimum age for Global Entry?</h3>
<p>A: There is no minimum age requirement for Global Entry. Children of any age can apply, though very young children may need assistance during the interview and enrollment process.</p>
<h3>Q: How long does Global Entry approval take for children?</h3>
<p>A: The processing time is typically the same for all applicants, regardless of age. Most applicants receive conditional approval within a few weeks of their interview, with final approval pending completion of background checks.</p>
<h3>Q: Can I get a refund if my child&#8217;s Global Entry application is denied?</h3>
<p>A: Unfortunately, the application fee is non-refundable, even if the application is denied. However, applicants are usually given specific reasons for denial and may reapply after addressing those issues.</p>
<h3>Q: Do children need their own Global Entry membership?</h3>
<p>A: Yes, each family member must have their own Global Entry membership to use the benefits. Memberships cannot be shared or transferred between individuals.</p>
<h3>Q: How long does Global Entry membership last for children?</h3>
<p>A: Global Entry membership is valid for five years from the approval date, the same as for adult members. Children will need to reapply before their membership expires to maintain benefits.</p>
<h3>Q: Can my child use Global Entry without a parent present?</h3>
<p>A: Children can use Global Entry when traveling alone, though they must be able to complete the process independently. Younger children may need assistance from CBP officers if they cannot operate the kiosks or understand the process.</p>
<h3>Q: Is Global Entry worth it for families who only travel internationally once every few years?</h3>
<p>A: For infrequent international travelers, the math may not justify the cost. However, when you factor in the time savings during peak travel periods, reduced stress, and included TSA PreCheck benefits, many families find it worthwhile even with occasional international travel.</p>
<h3>Q: Can I use a credit card statement credit to cover multiple family members&#8217; Global Entry fees?</h3>
<p>A: This depends on your specific credit card&#8217;s terms. Some cards allow the statement credit to be used for multiple applicants, while others limit it to the primary cardholder only. Check your card&#8217;s benefits details or contact the issuer directly for clarification.</p>
<h3>Q: Are there any discounts for families with three or more children applying for Global Entry?</h3>
<p>A: CBP does not offer family discounts based on the number of children. However, third-party promotions or special enrollment events may occasionally offer group discounts or fee waivers for multiple applicants.</p>
<h3>Q: How does Global Entry work for families with infants or toddlers?</h3>
<p>A: Families with infants or toddlers can still use Global Entry, though they&#8217;ll need assistance throughout the process. CBP officers are accustomed to helping young children with fingerprint verification and completing customs declarations. Parents may carry infants through the fingerprint scanner or have their fingerprints collected while holding the child.</p>
