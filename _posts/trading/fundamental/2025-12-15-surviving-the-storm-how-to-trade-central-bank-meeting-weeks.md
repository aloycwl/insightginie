In the modern financial ecosystem, few events command as much respect—and fear—as a central bank meeting week. Whether it's the Federal Reserve (Fed), the European Central Bank (ECB), or the Bank of Japan (BoJ), these institutions hold the keys to global liquidity. For traders, these weeks represent a paradox: they offer the highest potential for profit due to explosive volatility, but they also carry the highest risk of “whipsaw” losses.

When the Federal Open Market Committee (FOMC) or its global peers meet, the technical trends you've been tracking often evaporate, replaced by raw, fundamental repricing. To survive and thrive during these periods, you must shift your mindset from a technical chartist to a risk manager.
