In the financial markets, absolute truth is secondary to relative perception. A company can report record-breaking profits and see its stock price plummet, or an economy can show slowing growth while its currency rallies. To the uninitiated, this behavior looks irrational or manipulated. To the seasoned professional, it is simply the mechanics of **Market Expectations vs. Data Surprises**.

Understanding the gap between what the market expects (the consensus) and what actually happens (the data) is the single most important skill for trading volatility. It is not the news itself that moves the needle—it is the surprise factor.
