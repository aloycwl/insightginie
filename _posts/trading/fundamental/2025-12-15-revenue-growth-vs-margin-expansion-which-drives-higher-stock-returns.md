In the intricate world of financial analysis, investors are often forced to choose between two competing narratives. On one side, there is the allure of **Revenue Growth**—the “land grab” strategy focused on capturing market share and exploding the top line. On the other side sits **Margin Expansion**—the discipline of efficiency, pricing power, and squeezing more profit out of every dollar earned.

For the novice investor, a rising stock price is simply a good sign. But for the seasoned analyst, understanding *why* a stock is rising is the difference between a lucky trade and a repeatable strategy. Is the company selling more widgets, or is it simply making more money on the widgets it already sells?

This guide dissects the tug-of-war between revenue and margins, explaining how to analyze these metrics to identify high-quality businesses and avoid value traps.
