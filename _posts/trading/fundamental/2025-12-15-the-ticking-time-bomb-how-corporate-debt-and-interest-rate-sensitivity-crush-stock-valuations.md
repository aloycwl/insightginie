For over a decade following the Global Financial Crisis, the corporate world lived in a utopia of “easy money.” With central banks keeping interest rates near zero, corporations binged on cheap debt. They borrowed to fund operations, acquire competitors, and, most aggressively, to buy back their own shares. It was a strategy that worked brilliantly—until it didn't.

Today, the macroeconomic landscape has shifted violently. As interest rates have normalized at higher levels to combat inflation, the cost of capital has skyrocketed. For investors, the Income Statement (Revenue and Profits) is no longer the only document that matters. The Balance Sheet has returned with a vengeance.

Understanding the relationship between **corporate debt levels** and **interest rate sensitivity** is now the most critical skill for risk management. Companies that thrived on cheap leverage are now facing a reckoning, and investors who ignore these mechanics risk holding the bag when the bill comes due.
