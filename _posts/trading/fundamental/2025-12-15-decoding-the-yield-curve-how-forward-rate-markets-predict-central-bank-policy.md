In the global financial theater, Central Bank officials are the directors, but the **Forward Rate Market** is the audience screaming out the plot twists before they happen.

For retail traders, the focus is often on the “now”—the current price of the EUR/USD or the current level of the S&P 500. However, institutional money does not live in the present. It lives in the future. Hedge funds and banks trade based on where interest rates will be in six, twelve, or twenty-four months.

Understanding **Forward Rate Markets** and **Policy Pricing** is the difference between reacting to the news and anticipating it. This guide demystifies the complex world of Short-Term Interest Rates (STIRs) and reveals how to use market pricing to gauge the true sentiment of the global economy.
