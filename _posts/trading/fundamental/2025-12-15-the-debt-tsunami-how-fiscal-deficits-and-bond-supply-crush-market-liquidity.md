For decades, the global financial system operated under a comforting assumption: government debt is a risk-free asset, and there is always unlimited demand for it. Central banks acted as the buyer of last resort, suppressing volatility and keeping interest rates artificially low.

That era is over.

We have entered a new paradigm defined by aggressive **Fiscal Deficits** and overwhelming **Bond Supply Pressure**. Governments across the developed world are spending trillions more than they collect in taxes, bridging the gap by issuing a mountain of new bonds. This flood of supply is colliding with a shrinking pool of buyers, creating a volatility storm that affects every asset class—from mortgages to tech stocks.

Understanding the mechanics of how deficits translate into market pressure is no longer optional for investors; it is a survival skill.
