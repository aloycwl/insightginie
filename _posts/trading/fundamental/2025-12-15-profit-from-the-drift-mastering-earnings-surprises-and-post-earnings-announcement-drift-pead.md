Every quarter, Wall Street engages in a chaotic ritual known as earnings season. For the uninitiated, it looks like a gamble: a company reports numbers, and the stock price reacts violently, either gapping up or crashing down. Many traders try to guess the outcome, effectively flipping a coin. However, seasoned professionals know that the real opportunity often lies not in predicting the initial jump, but in trading the movement that follows.

This phenomenon is known as **Post-Earnings Announcement Drift (PEAD)**. It is one of the most extensively studied anomalies in finance, challenging the Efficient Market Hypothesis and offering astute investors a distinct edge. Understanding how earnings surprises trigger this drift can transform how you approach market trends.
