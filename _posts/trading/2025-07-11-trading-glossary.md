Price Action Trading
--------------------

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Bearish | An expectation that price is going to decrease. |
| Breakdown | When the price falls below a support level. |
| Breakout | When the price moves outside a defined support or resistance level with increased volume. |
| Bullish | An expectation that price is going to increase. |
| Candlestick Chart | A type of financial chart used to describe price movements of a security, derivative, or currency. |
| Chart Patterns | Patterns formed by the price movements on a chart, such as triangles, channels, and head and shoulders. |
| Consolidation | A period where price moves within a confined range, indicating that the market is undecided. |
| Doji | A candlestick pattern that signals indecision in the market. |
| Engulfing Pattern | A candlestick pattern that can signal a reversal in the market. |
| Fibonacci Retracement | A tool used to identify potential reversal levels based on the Fibonacci sequence. |
| Hammer | A candlestick pattern that indicates a potential reversal in the downtrend. |
| Momentum | The rate of acceleration of a security's price or volume. |
| Pin Bar | A type of candlestick pattern that indicates a rejection of a certain price level. |
| Price Action | The movement of a security's price plotted over time. |
| Pullback | A temporary reversal of the prevailing trend in price action. |
| Rally | A period in which price consistently moves upwards. |
| Range | The difference between the high and low prices during a specific time period. |
| Resistance Level | A price level where a trend can stall or reverse due to a concentration of selling interest. |
| Reversal | A change in the direction of the price movement of a security. |
| Shooting Star | A type of candlestick pattern that suggests a bearish reversal. |
| Support Level | A price level where a downtrend can be expected to pause due to a concentration of demand. |
| Swing High | A peak formed when the high of a price is greater than a given number of highs positioned around it. |
| Swing Low | A trough formed when the low of a price is lower than a given number of lows positioned around it. |
| Trend Lines | Straight lines drawn on a chart to highlight the prevailing market direction. |
| Volume | The number of shares or contracts traded in a security or market during a given period. |

Volume Trading
--------------

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| ADTV (Average Daily Trading Volume) | The average number of shares traded in a day in a specific security or market. |
| AVOL (Average Volume) | The average volume of shares traded over a specific period. |
| Block Trade | A large volume of securities transaction, typically involving 10,000 shares of stock or more. |
| CV (Cumulative Volume) | The total volume accumulated over a period. |
| Dark Pool Volume | Trading volume that occurs in private exchanges or forums, outside of public stock exchanges. |
| HV (High Volume) | Refers to a higher-than-average number of shares or contracts traded. |
| Liquidity | The degree to which an asset or security can be quickly bought or sold in the market without affecting its price. |
| LV (Low Volume) | Refers to a lower-than-average number of shares or contracts traded. |
| Market Depth | The market's ability to sustain relatively large market orders without impacting the price of the security. |
| MVOL (Market Volume) | The volume of all stocks or securities traded in a particular market. |
| OBV (On-Balance Volume) | A technical trading momentum indicator that uses volume flow to predict changes in stock price. |
| RVOL (Relative Volume) | A measure of current volume compared to the average volume for the same time of day. |
| Tick Volume | A measure of trading activity that indicates the number of price changes in a security. |
| TV (Total Volume) | The total volume of shares or contracts traded in a specific market or security. |
| VAP (Volume at Price) | A histogram on a chart that shows the amount of volume for each individual price level. |
| VIX (Volatility Index) | A real-time market index representing the market's expectations for volatility over the coming 30 days. |
| Volume | The number of shares or contracts traded in a security or market during a given period. |
| Volume Oscillator | An indicator that measures the difference between two volume moving averages. |
| Volume Profile | A charting tool that displays trading activity over a specified time period at certain price levels. |
| VPOC (Volume Point of Control) | The price level for the time period with the highest traded volume. |
| VPT (Volume Price Trend) | An indicator that shows the relationship between the volume and price movement of a security. |
| VROC (Volume Rate of Change) | An indicator that measures the rate of change in volume. |
| VSA (Volume Spread Analysis) | A methodology based on the analysis of volume and price. |
| VWAP (Volume Weighted Average Price) | A trading benchmark that provides the average price a security has traded at throughout the day, based on both volume and price. |
| VWMA (Volume Weighted Moving Average) | A moving average that gives more weight to price bars with higher volume. |

Trading
-------

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Ask | The lowest price a seller is willing to accept for a security. |
| Bear Market | A market condition where prices are falling or are expected to fall. |
| Bid | The highest price a buyer is willing to pay for a security. |
| Bull Market | A market condition where prices are rising or are expected to rise. |
| Candlestick Chart | A type of financial chart used to describe price movements of a security, derivative, or currency. |
| Day Trading | The practice of buying and selling financial instruments within the same trading day. |
| Diversification | A risk management strategy that mixes a wide variety of investments within a portfolio. |
| ETF (Exchange-Traded Fund) | A type of security that tracks an index, commodity, or basket of assets but trades like a stock on an exchange. |
| Futures | Financial contracts obligating the buyer to purchase an asset or the seller to sell an asset at a predetermined future date and price. |
| HFT (High-Frequency Trading) | A type of trading that uses powerful computers to transact a large number of orders at very fast speeds. |
| IPO (Initial Public Offering) | The first time that the stock of a private company is offered to the public. |
| Leverage | The use of borrowed money to increase potential return of an investment. |
| Margin | Borrowing money from a broker to purchase stock, using the investment as collateral. |
| NASDAQ | An American stock exchange and the second-largest exchange in the world by market capitalization. |
| NYSE (New York Stock Exchange) | The largest stock exchange in the world by market capitalization. |
| Options | Contracts that give the buyer the right, but not the obligation, to buy (or sell) a security at a predetermined price within a specific time period. |
| P/E Ratio (Price-to-Earnings Ratio) | A valuation ratio of a company's current share price compared to its per-share earnings. |
| Rally | A rapid increase in the general price level of the market or of the price of a stock. |
| SEC (Securities and Exchange Commission) | A U.S. government agency that oversees securities transactions, activities of financial professionals and mutual fund trading to prevent fraud and intentional deception. |
| Short Selling | The sale of a security that is not owned by the seller, or that the seller has borrowed. |
| Stop-Loss Order | An order placed with a broker to buy or sell once the stock reaches a certain price. |
| TA (Technical Analysis) | A trading discipline employed to evaluate investments and identify trading opportunities by analyzing statistical trends gathered from trading activity. |
| Volatility | The degree of variation of a trading price series over time, usually measured by the standard deviation of logarithmic returns. |
| Yield | The income return on an investment, such as the interest or dividends received from holding a particular security. |

Crypto
------

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Altcoin | Any cryptocurrency other than Bitcoin. |
| ATH (All-Time High) | The highest price ever reached by a cryptocurrency. |
| ATL (All-Time Low) | The lowest price ever reached by a cryptocurrency. |
| Bear Market | A market where prices are declining. |
| Blockchain | A digital ledger recording all the transactions made in a particular cryptocurrency sequentially. |
| BTC | Bitcoin, the first and most well-known cryptocurrency. |
| Bull Market | A market where prices are rising. |
| Cold Wallet/Cold Storage | A secure offline wallet for storing cryptocurrency. |
| DAO (Decentralized Autonomous Organization) | An organization represented by rules encoded as a computer program, transparent and member-controlled without central authority. |
| DApp (Decentralized Application) | An application that runs on a decentralized network, avoiding a single point of failure. |
| DEX (Decentralized Exchange) | A cryptocurrency exchange which operates without a central authority. |
| DeFi (Decentralized Finance) | Financial services built on blockchain technology including lending, borrowing, and trading. |
| DYOR (Do Your Own Research) | Advice to investors to research and analyze a coin or investment themselves before investing. |
| ERC-20 | A standard for creating and issuing smart contracts on the Ethereum blockchain. |
| FA (Fundamental Analysis) | A method of measuring a security's intrinsic value by examining economic and financial factors. |
| FOMO (Fear Of Missing Out) | Fear of missing profit opportunities in crypto, often triggered by social media hype. |
| FUD (Fear, Uncertainty, and Doubt) | Strategy to influence perception via negative, dubious, or false information. |
| Gas | A fee paid to execute transactions or contracts on the Ethereum blockchain. |
| HODL | A buy-and-hold strategy originally based on a misspelling of “hold”. |
| ICO (Initial Coin Offering) | A fundraising method where crypto tokens are sold in exchange for Bitcoin or Ether. |
| Mining | Using computing power to process transactions, secure the network, and synchronize participants. |
| Moon/Mooning | Describes a cryptocurrency experiencing a strong upward trend. |
| NFT (Non-Fungible Token) | A unique digital asset recorded on a blockchain that certifies authenticity and ownership. |
| Private Key | A secure code giving a user access to their cryptocurrency. |
| Public Key | An alphanumeric code used to receive cryptocurrency. |
| ROI (Return on Investment) | Performance metric evaluating the efficiency of an investment. |
| Satoshi | The smallest unit of Bitcoin, named after its creator, Satoshi Nakamoto. |
| Shill | Enthusiastic promotion of a cryptocurrency for personal gain. |
| Smart Contract | Self-executing contracts with terms written directly into code. |
| Stablecoin | A cryptocurrency designed to maintain a stable value. |
| TA (Technical Analysis) | A trading discipline using statistical trends from trading activity to identify opportunities. |
| TVL (Total Value Locked) | Total value of assets staked in a DeFi protocol. |
| Wallet | A method of storing cryptocurrencies either digitally or offline. |
| Whale | An individual or entity holding a large amount of cryptocurrency, capable of influencing market prices. |

Smart Money Concepts
--------------------

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| ADR (Average Daily Range) | The average range that a currency pair moves in a day, used in ICT for setting targets and stop losses. |
| Asian Range | The price range formed during the Asian trading session, often monitored for its influence on later sessions. |
| BOS (Break of Structure) | Structure break that indicates continuation, used for better trade entries. |
| Breaker | A market structure concept where a support or resistance level is broken and then retested. |
| CHoCH (Change of Character) | A shift in market structure, indicating potential reversal or continuation. |
| COT (Commitment of Traders) | A report detailing traders' positions in futures markets, useful for gauging sentiment. |
| Equilibrium | A price level or zone where supply and demand are in balance. |
| Fibonacci Retracement | A technical analysis tool that marks areas of support or resistance using key Fibonacci levels. |
| FVG (Fair Value Gap) | A price area with no trading activity, signaling a potential target for future trades. |
| HTF (Higher Time Frame) | Longer time frame charts used for broader market trend analysis. |
| ICT (Inner Circle Trader) | A trading methodology developed by Michael J. Huddleston. |
| Institutional Order Flow | Large-scale buying and selling by institutions, which can influence price movements. |
| Kill Zone | Specific time periods during the trading day when ICT strategies are considered most effective. |
| Liquidity Void | An area on the chart with low liquidity, often leading to swift price movements. |
| LTF (Lower Time Frame) | Shorter time frame charts for detailed market analysis. |
| Mitigation Block | A price level where previously unfulfilled orders are likely to be filled. |
| MS (Market Structure) | The highs and lows in a market that form patterns used to analyze direction. |
| MSB (Market Structure Break) | Same as CHoCH – a change in market structure indicating trend shift. |
| MSS (Market Structure Shift) | Same as above – a transition in market structure dynamics. |
| OTE (Optimal Trade Entry) | A high-probability entry zone defined by ICT methodology. |
| OB (Order Block) | A block of price where institutional orders are believed to reside. |
| POI (Point of Interest) | Key price levels or zones with expected trading significance. |
| Price Delivery | The movement of price toward a forecasted level or zone. |
| Psychological Levels | Rounded price numbers often acting as support or resistance due to trader behavior. |
| SMT (Smart Money Theory) | Analyzing divergence between related assets to forecast price action. |
| Stop Hunt | A market maneuver aimed at triggering stop-losses before resuming original direction. |
| Swing High/Low | The peak or trough of a price movement. |
| Trader's Trinity | An ICT concept using three key Fibonacci retracement levels in analysis. |
| Value Area | The price range where the bulk of trading volume has occurred. |
