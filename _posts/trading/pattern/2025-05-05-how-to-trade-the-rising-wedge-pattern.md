In technical analysis, chart patterns are powerful tools used to predict future price movements and identify potential trading opportunities. One of the most widely recognized patterns is the **Rising Wedge**. This pattern often signals a potential reversal of an uptrend, making it a valuable tool for traders looking to capitalize on trend reversals.

A **Rising Wedge** is a price pattern characterized by converging trendlines — the upper trendline sloping upwards and the lower trendline sloping upwards at a more gradual angle. While it may appear to be a continuation pattern during an uptrend, the Rising Wedge often foreshadows a bearish reversal once the price breaks below the lower trendline.

In this guide, we will explore how to identify, trade, and manage risks when using the Rising Wedge pattern to inform your trading decisions.
