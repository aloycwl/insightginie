In technical analysis, chart patterns play a crucial role in predicting potential price movements and market trends. One such pattern that traders frequently rely on is the **Descending Triangle Chart Pattern**. This pattern is a **bearish continuation** pattern, typically signaling that the market is likely to break down further after a period of consolidation.

The Descending Triangle is formed by two converging trendlines: a **horizontal support line** (lower trendline) and a **sloping resistance line** (upper trendline). As the price action moves within these boundaries, it suggests that while sellers are pushing the price lower, buyers are still trying to defend the support. Eventually, the balance shifts, leading to a breakdown and often a continuation of the prevailing downtrend.

In this guide, we'll dive into how to identify and trade the **Descending Triangle Pattern**, providing you with actionable strategies to maximize your trading success.
