In technical analysis, chart patterns provide traders with valuable insights into potential market movements. One of the most reliable patterns is the **Falling Wedge**, which is commonly used to predict trend reversals. This pattern occurs during a downtrend and typically signals the end of the bearish market and the potential start of an uptrend.

The **Falling Wedge** is characterized by two converging trendlines: the upper trendline sloping downward at a steeper angle than the lower trendline. This creates a wedge shape where the price moves within narrowing bounds. Although it often appears during a downtrend, the Falling Wedge is considered a **bullish reversal pattern**.

In this guide, we will explore how to identify, trade, and manage risk with the Falling Wedge pattern to enhance your trading strategy and improve your chances of success in the markets.
