In today's complex financial landscape, businesses worldwide face an ever-increasing pressure to combat financial crime. Anti-Money Laundering (AML) and Counter-Terrorism Financing (CTF) regulations are becoming stricter, and the penalties for non-compliance are severe. At the heart of these efforts lies a critical practice: customer due diligence. But not all due diligence is created equal. Understanding the nuances between **Customer Due Diligence (CDD)** and **Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD)** is not just good practice; it's essential for safeguarding your organization, maintaining regulatory compliance, and preventing illicit financial activities.

Many organizations, particularly financial institutions and designated non-financial businesses and professions (DNFBPs), grapple with distinguishing these two vital components of their compliance framework. While both aim to understand customers and assess risk, their application, intensity, and triggers differ significantly. This comprehensive guide will demystify CDD and EDD, providing you with the clarity needed to implement a robust and effective due diligence program.
