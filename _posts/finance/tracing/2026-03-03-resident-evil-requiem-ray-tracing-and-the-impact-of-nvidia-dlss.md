<h2>Introduction to Resident Evil Requiem</h2>

<h2>What is Ray Tracing?</h2>
<p>Ray tracing is a rendering technique that simulates the way light interacts with objects in a virtual environment. Unlike traditional rendering methods, which use pre-calculated lighting, ray tracing generates realistic lighting effects by tracing the path of light rays as they bounce off surfaces. This results in more lifelike shadows, reflections, and global illumination, creating a more immersive gaming experience.</p>
<h2>The Role of Nvidia DLSS</h2>
<p>Nvidia DLSS is an AI-driven technology that enhances game performance by rendering images at a lower resolution and then upscaling them to a higher resolution using deep learning algorithms. This allows games to run more smoothly without sacrificing visual quality. In Resident Evil Requiem, DLSS works in tandem with ray tracing to deliver stunning visuals while maintaining high frame rates.</p>
<h3>How Ray Tracing Enhances Resident Evil Requiem</h3>
<p>The implementation of ray tracing in Resident Evil Requiem brings a new level of realism to the game. Players can expect to see realistic reflections in mirrors and windows, dynamic shadows that change based on the position of light sources, and ambient occlusion that adds depth to the environment. These enhancements contribute to the game&#8217;s eerie atmosphere, making the horror elements even more impactful.</p>
<h3>Performance Benefits of DLSS</h3>
<p>While ray tracing significantly improves visual fidelity, it can also be demanding on hardware. This is where Nvidia DLSS comes into play. By leveraging AI to upscale images, DLSS allows players to enjoy the benefits of ray tracing without compromising performance. In Resident Evil Requiem, DLSS ensures that the game runs smoothly on a wide range of hardware configurations, making it accessible to a broader audience.</p>
