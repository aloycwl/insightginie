The shadowy world of money laundering has always been a cat-and-mouse game between criminals and law enforcement. For decades, the image of laundered money evoked suitcases of cash, offshore accounts, and complex shell corporations. While these methods persist, the digital age has ushered in a radical transformation, empowering scammers with unprecedented tools and techniques. Today, money laundering isn't just about cleaning 'dirty money'; it's about an intricate, technologically advanced process that leverages global connectivity, anonymity, and human psychology. Understanding this evolution is crucial for individuals, businesses, and financial institutions alike to stay one step ahead.
