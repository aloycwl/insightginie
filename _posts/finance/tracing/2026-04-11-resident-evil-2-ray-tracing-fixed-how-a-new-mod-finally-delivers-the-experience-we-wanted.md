<h2>The Problem with Official Ray Tracing in RE2</h2>
<p>To understand why this mod is such a game-changer, we first need to look at what went wrong with the official implementation. Ray tracing in games is intended to simulate how light interacts with surfaces in the real world. In theory, this should enhance depth, ambient occlusion, and reflection quality. In Resident Evil 2, however, the implementation suffered from several critical issues:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Crushed Black Levels:</strong> The lighting engine often struggled to balance the bright highlights with dark shadows, causing interior scenes to lose all detail in the dark areas, making navigation nearly impossible without constantly using the flashlight.</li>
<li><strong>Inconsistent Global Illumination:</strong> In some areas, the ray tracing caused lighting to flicker or behave erratically when camera angles shifted, breaking the immersion completely.</li>
<li><strong>Performance Penalties vs. Visual Gain:</strong> For many users, the performance hit was significant, and the visual improvement over the high-quality screen-space reflections was negligible, making it a bad trade-off.</li>
</ul>
