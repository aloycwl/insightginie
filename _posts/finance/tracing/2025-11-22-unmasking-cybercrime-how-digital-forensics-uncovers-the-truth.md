In our increasingly interconnected world, digital technologies have become both a blessing and a curse. While they facilitate communication, commerce, and innovation, they also open new avenues for malicious actors. Cybercrime is a booming industry, ranging from data breaches and ransomware attacks to identity theft and corporate espionage. When these digital transgressions occur, a specialized field steps in to piece together the evidence, identify culprits, and bring justice: **cyber forensics**.

Often portrayed in movies as high-tech sleuthing, the reality of cyber forensics is a meticulous, detail-oriented process that combines technological expertise with investigative acumen. It's about more than just finding a hacker; it's about understanding *how* an incident occurred, *what* data was compromised, and *who* was responsible, all while maintaining the integrity of digital evidence for legal proceedings.
