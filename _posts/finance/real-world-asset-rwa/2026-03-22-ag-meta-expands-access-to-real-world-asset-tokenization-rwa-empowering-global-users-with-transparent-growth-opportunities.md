<h2>Understanding Real-World Asset Tokenization</h2>
<p>Real‑world asset tokenization refers to the process of representing physical assets—such as real estate, commodities, art, or intellectual property—on a blockchain as digital tokens. Each token embodies a fractional share of the underlying asset, enabling investors to buy, sell, or trade portions of high‑value items without needing to purchase the whole.</p>
<h3>Why Tokenization Matters</h3>
<ul>
<li>Liquidity: Assets that are traditionally illiquid become tradable 24/7 on global markets.</li>
<li>Accessibility: Fractional ownership lowers the entry barrier, allowing small investors to participate.</li>
<li>Transparency: Blockchain’s immutable ledger provides clear ownership records and transaction history.</li>
<li>Efficiency: Smart contracts automate processes like dividend distribution, reducing administrative overhead.</li>
</ul>
