The financial landscape is undergoing a profound transformation, driven by the relentless march of technology. At the heart of this evolution lies the phenomenon of digital deposits, fundamentally altering how banks manage their liquidity. Once a predictable and relatively stable component of a bank's balance sheet, customer deposits are now subject to unprecedented volatility and sensitivity, thanks to the ease and speed of digital transactions. Understanding this seismic shift is not just an academic exercise; it's critical for the resilience of individual financial institutions and the stability of the entire global financial system. This article delves into how digital savings are impacting bank liquidity, exploring both the inherent risks and the emerging opportunities for financial institutions.
