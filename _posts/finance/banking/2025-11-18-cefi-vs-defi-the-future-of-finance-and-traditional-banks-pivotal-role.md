The financial world is undergoing a seismic shift, driven by technological innovation and a growing demand for greater transparency, accessibility, and control. At the heart of this transformation lie two competing, yet sometimes complementary, paradigms: Centralized Finance (CeFi) and Decentralized Finance (DeFi). While CeFi represents the familiar world of traditional banking and regulated financial institutions, DeFi offers a radical alternative built on blockchain technology and smart contracts. This article delves into the core differences between these two systems, examining their respective strengths and weaknesses, and critically analyzing the evolving — and often challenging — role of traditional banks in this new financial frontier.
