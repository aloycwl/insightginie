Every time you swipe, tap, or click to pay with a credit or debit card, a complex financial dance begins behind the scenes. While the transaction might feel instantaneous and free to the consumer, businesses know better. Lurking within the cost of accepting card payments are **interchange fees** – a critical, often misunderstood, component of the payment ecosystem. For merchants, these fees represent a significant operational expense. For consumers, they're the invisible engine funding everything from rewards programs to the very infrastructure that makes digital payments possible.

But what exactly are interchange fees? Who sets them? And, perhaps most importantly, who actually pays them? In this comprehensive guide, we'll pull back the curtain on interchange fees, demystifying their mechanics, identifying the key players, and revealing their true impact on businesses and consumers alike.
