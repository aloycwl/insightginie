The financial landscape has undergone a seismic shift, particularly in the payments sector. For years, the narrative pitted established banks against agile fintech startups in a fierce battle for market share. Traditional institutions, with their deep pockets and vast customer bases, seemed poised for an arduous fight against nimble, tech-driven innovators offering faster, cheaper, and more user-friendly payment solutions. However, the story has evolved. What began as a perceived zero-sum game has transformed into a complex dance of competition and, increasingly, cooperation – a phenomenon often dubbed “coopetition.”

This article delves into the dynamic interplay between banks and fintechs in payments, exploring the unique strengths each brings to the table, the drivers behind their shifting relationship, and the models of collaboration that are shaping the future of digital transactions.
