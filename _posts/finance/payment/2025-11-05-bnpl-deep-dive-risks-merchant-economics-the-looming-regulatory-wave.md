The digital checkout experience has been revolutionized by a simple, yet powerful, concept: **Buy Now, Pay Later (BNPL)**. What began as a niche offering has exploded into a global phenomenon, reshaping consumer spending habits and redefining retail finance. For consumers, it offers immediate gratification and perceived financial flexibility. For merchants, it promises increased sales and customer loyalty. But beneath the surface of convenience and boosted conversions lies a complex ecosystem laden with [inherent risks](#risks), intricate [merchant economics](#economics), and an increasingly urgent demand for comprehensive [regulation](#regulation).

As chief editor, I've watched countless trends emerge and fade. BNPL, however, feels different. It's not just a payment method; it's a fundamental shift in how people access goods and services. Understanding its multifaceted impact is crucial for businesses, policymakers, and consumers alike.
