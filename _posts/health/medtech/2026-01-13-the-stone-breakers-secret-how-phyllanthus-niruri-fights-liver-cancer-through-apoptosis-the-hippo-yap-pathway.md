Liver cancer remains one of the most aggressive and challenging malignancies globally, with high mortality rates despite advances in medical science. The search for novel therapeutic strategies, particularly those with fewer side effects or complementary benefits, is continuous. In this quest, traditional herbal medicine offers a rich reservoir of potential candidates. Among them, *Phyllanthus niruri*, commonly known as 'Chanca Piedra' or 'Stone Breaker,' has garnered significant scientific interest for its diverse pharmacological properties, including its potential anti-cancer effects, particularly against liver cancer cells.

Recent research has begun to unravel the intricate mechanisms by which *Phyllanthus niruri* exerts its therapeutic actions. Two key cellular processes that are increasingly central to cancer research are **apoptosis** (programmed cell death) and the **Hippo-YAP pathway**, a critical regulator of cell proliferation, survival, and organ size. Understanding how *Phyllanthus niruri* interacts with these pathways could pave the way for new, natural-compound-based approaches to liver cancer treatment.
