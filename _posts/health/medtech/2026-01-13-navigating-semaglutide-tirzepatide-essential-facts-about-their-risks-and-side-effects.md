In the evolving landscape of modern medicine, Semaglutide (marketed as Ozempic, Wegovy, and Rybelsus) and Tirzepatide (known as Mounjaro and Zepbound) have emerged as powerful tools in the fight against type 2 diabetes and obesity. These medications, belonging to the class of GLP-1 receptor agonists (and GIP/GLP-1 receptor agonists for Tirzepatide), have demonstrated remarkable efficacy in improving glycemic control and facilitating significant weight loss. Their rise in popularity, fueled by compelling clinical trial results and widespread media attention, has understandably piqued the interest of millions seeking solutions for chronic health conditions.

However, as with any potent pharmaceutical intervention, the benefits come with a spectrum of potential risks and side effects. While the transformative effects of Semaglutide and Tirzepatide are well-documented, a comprehensive understanding of their potential downsides is paramount for both patients and healthcare providers. This article delves into the essential facts about the risks associated with these medications, moving beyond the headlines to provide a balanced, informed perspective on what you need to know before, during, and after treatment.
