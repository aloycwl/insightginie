Migraines affect over one billion people worldwide, making them one of the most prevalent—and debilitating—neurological disorders. Characterized by intense, throbbing headaches often accompanied by nausea, light sensitivity, and visual disturbances, migraines can significantly impact a person's quality of life. While medications like triptans and beta-blockers are commonly used for prevention and relief, many sufferers are now turning to **natural, evidence-based alternatives**—notably **magnesium** and **riboflavin (vitamin B2)**—for migraine prevention.

This article explores the science behind magnesium and riboflavin as migraine-preventive supplements, their recommended dosages, potential side effects, and how they compare with conventional treatments.
