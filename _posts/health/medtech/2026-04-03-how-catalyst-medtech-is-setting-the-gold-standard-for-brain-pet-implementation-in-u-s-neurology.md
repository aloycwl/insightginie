<h2>The Challenge of Modern Brain PET Implementation</h2>
<p>Historically, the implementation of brain PET scanning in clinical neurology practices has been fraught with complexities. Facilities often struggle with the fragmented nature of the workflow, which includes:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Operational Hurdles:</strong> Navigating the intersection of radiopharmaceuticals, specialized imaging equipment, and rigid regulatory compliance.</li>
<li><strong>Technological Disparities:</strong> A lack of standardized imaging protocols leads to inconsistent image quality, hampering accurate diagnostic interpretation.</li>
<li><strong>Interdisciplinary Silos:</strong> A disconnect between referring neurologists, nuclear medicine technologists, and radiologists often results in diagnostic delays and inefficiencies.</li>
</ul>
<p>Without a unified infrastructure, many clinical practices have found it prohibitive to offer advanced PET imaging, despite its recognized superiority in identifying early-stage cognitive impairment and differentiating neurodegenerative pathologies.</p>
