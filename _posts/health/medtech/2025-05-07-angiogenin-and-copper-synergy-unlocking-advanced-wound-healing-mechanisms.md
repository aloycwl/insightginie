Wound healing is a complex, multistage biological process essential for tissue repair and homeostasis. Among the numerous molecular players involved, *angiogenin* and *copper* have emerged as powerful agents that significantly influence tissue regeneration. While angiogenin plays a pivotal role in angiogenesis — the formation of new blood vessels — copper acts as a vital cofactor for numerous enzymatic reactions critical to cellular regeneration and immune modulation. The interplay between angiogenin and copper, often referred to as “angiogenin and copper crossing,” is increasingly recognized for its therapeutic potential in accelerating and improving wound healing outcomes. This article delves into their biological significance, mechanisms of action, and future applications in regenerative medicine.
