In the world of regenerative medicine and skincare science, few compounds have garnered as much attention as **GHK (Glycyl-L-Histidyl-L-Lysine)**, particularly in its copper-binding form, **GHK-Cu**. This naturally occurring tripeptide, first identified in human plasma in the 1970s, has been shown to play a critical role in **wound healing, skin regeneration, hair growth, and even anti-aging therapies**. Today, GHK is at the forefront of biotechnology and cosmeceutical innovation.

Whether you are a skincare enthusiast, a biohacker, or a researcher, understanding the potential of GHK could unlock new avenues for health and longevity.
