Heart Rate Variability (HRV) is a powerful, non-invasive biomarker of autonomic nervous system function and overall health. Often used in clinical settings to assess cardiovascular health, stress, and recovery, HRV has gained significant attention in the wellness and fitness industries. Recent advancements in **digital phenotyping**—the use of digital devices to assess and monitor human behavior—have made HRV data more accessible and actionable, enabling individuals and healthcare providers to track HRV outside of traditional clinical environments.

Wearable sensors, including **smartwatches**, **fitness trackers**, and **heart rate monitors**, are now capable of providing continuous HRV monitoring, allowing for personalized health insights. This article explores how HRV is measured through wearable sensors, the benefits of HRV tracking, and the potential of digital phenotyping to optimize health and wellness.
