Digital phenotyping, the practice of collecting and analyzing data from personal devices to understand an individual's health and behavior, has gained significant attention in recent years. As we move toward a more data-driven healthcare model, the ability to fuse multi-modal data from different sources — including wearables, smartphones, sensors, and environmental data — holds immense potential for creating comprehensive health profiles. One of the key advancements in digital phenotyping is **multi-modal data fusion**. This approach integrates diverse data types to provide a holistic view of an individual's health and behavior, allowing for more accurate predictions and personalized interventions.

In this article, we explore the concept of **multi-modal data fusion** in digital phenotyping, how it works, its benefits, challenges, and key applications in health monitoring and disease prediction.
