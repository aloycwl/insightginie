In the era of smartphones and ubiquitous connectivity, every swipe, tap, and scroll contributes to a behavioral fingerprint. **Digital phenotyping**—the moment-by-moment quantification of human behavior through digital devices—uses passive data sources to monitor psychological and physiological health. Among the most revealing data streams are **screen time** and **app usage patterns**.

These digital traces offer a unique, continuous view into individuals' routines, interests, cognitive engagement, and emotional well-being. In this article, we explore how screen time and app usage behavior are leveraged in digital phenotyping to assess mental health, detect early warning signs of disorders, and support personalized interventions.
