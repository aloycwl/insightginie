As the demand for real-time, individualized mental and behavioral health insights grows, **digital phenotyping** is emerging as a transformative approach to healthcare. By leveraging everyday digital interactions, digital phenotyping captures moment-by-moment data to reflect an individual's psychological and physiological state. A foundational aspect of this approach is the method of **data collection**—namely, **passive** and **active** techniques.

Understanding the strengths, limitations, and ideal applications of **passive vs. active data collection methods** is crucial for building ethical, accurate, and scalable digital health tools. In this article, we explore these two methodologies in-depth and analyze how each contributes to effective digital phenotyping strategies in clinical and research contexts.
