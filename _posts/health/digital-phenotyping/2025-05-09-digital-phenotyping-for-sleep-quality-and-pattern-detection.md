Sleep is a critical pillar of human health, affecting cognitive function, emotional stability, and physical well-being. However, millions suffer from undiagnosed or poorly managed sleep disorders such as insomnia, sleep apnea, and circadian rhythm disturbances. Enter **digital phenotyping**—a powerful, data-driven approach that leverages passive and active signals from digital devices to provide **continuous, real-world insights into sleep quality and patterns**.

Using ubiquitous technologies such as smartphones, smartwatches, and other wearables, digital phenotyping offers scalable, non-invasive, and cost-effective tools for assessing sleep health at both individual and population levels. This article explores how sleep-related behavioral data is collected, analyzed, and interpreted through digital phenotyping—and how it is reshaping sleep research and clinical care.
