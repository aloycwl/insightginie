With the rapid growth of mobile technology, our smartphones have become sophisticated sensors that continuously gather data about our behaviors and environments. At the forefront of this innovation is **digital phenotyping**—the real-time, passive quantification of individual behavior using smartphone data. One of the most powerful streams of data for understanding human activity and behavior is **GPS-based location tracking**, which enables researchers and clinicians to analyze **mobility patterns**, social behaviors, and even mental health states.

This article explores the concept of GPS-based mobility monitoring within the digital phenotyping framework, including its methodology, healthcare applications, benefits, challenges, and emerging trends.
