Eating disorders—including anorexia nervosa, bulimia nervosa, and binge-eating disorder—are serious mental health conditions that impact physical and psychological well-being. Traditionally, diagnosis and monitoring of these disorders rely heavily on patient self-reporting and clinical assessments, which can be hindered by stigma, denial, or intermittent care access.

**Digital phenotyping**—the passive, real-time collection of behavioral and physiological data from personal digital devices—offers a transformative approach to detecting and monitoring eating disorders. By analyzing data from smartphones, wearables, and social media activity, healthcare providers can gain timely, objective, and contextual insights into disordered eating behaviors and emotional triggers.
