As digital health technologies evolve, smartphones are becoming central to personalized healthcare and behavioral monitoring. Among these, **digital phenotyping**—the moment-by-moment quantification of individual-level human behavior using data from personal digital devices—has emerged as a powerful tool in medical research and clinical applications. A key component of this field is **physical activity monitoring**, particularly through **smartphone accelerometers**, which offer non-invasive, continuous, and scalable means of tracking movement and behavior patterns.

This article explores the principles, methods, applications, and implications of using smartphone accelerometers in digital phenotyping, particularly for physical activity tracking in health monitoring and behavioral analysis.
