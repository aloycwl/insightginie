As digital interactions become inseparable from daily life, our behavior while typing—once considered trivial—is now a powerful window into our cognitive and emotional states. **Digital phenotyping**, the continuous and passive collection of behavioral data via digital devices, has embraced **keystroke dynamics and typing behavior** as critical features in the emerging field of behavioral health analytics.

From detecting early signs of **neurodegenerative diseases** to inferring **mood variations** and **stress levels**, the patterns in how we type—speed, rhythm, errors, pauses—serve as a form of digital fingerprinting that reveals much more than content ever could.

This article explores how keystroke dynamics are used in digital phenotyping, the behavioral insights derived, research applications in mental health, and the privacy implications of this sensitive data stream.
