Digital phenotyping, the science of collecting and analyzing data from digital devices to understand human behavior and health, has rapidly expanded in scope. Among its most innovative tools is **facial expression monitoring** via cameras—enabling the real-time detection of emotional and cognitive states using standard smartphones, tablets, or laptops.

Facial expressions are one of the most fundamental and universally understood forms of human communication. By analyzing subtle muscle movements and facial micro-expressions, AI-powered computer vision systems can derive clinically relevant indicators of **stress**, **depression**, **anxiety**, **fatigue**, and even **neurological disorders**. This article examines how facial expression monitoring is transforming digital phenotyping, its current applications, ethical considerations, and its future in healthcare and well-being.
