The rapid advancement of digital health technologies has transformed how healthcare professionals monitor and manage patient care, particularly with the emergence of **digital phenotyping**. Digital phenotyping involves collecting and analyzing data from a variety of digital sources — including smartphones, wearables, and sensors — to understand an individual's health, behaviors, and well-being. As healthcare systems move toward more personalized and patient-centric care models, integrating digital phenotyping into **clinical workflows** and **telehealth applications** is becoming increasingly important. This integration can enhance remote monitoring, provide real-time insights into a patient's condition, and help healthcare providers make more informed decisions.

In this article, we explore the role of **clinical integration** and **telehealth applications** in digital phenotyping, examining how these technologies can enhance patient outcomes, reduce healthcare costs, and support more personalized treatment plans.
