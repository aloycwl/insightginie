Radiotherapy remains a cornerstone in the treatment of lung cancer, particularly non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC), which accounts for the majority of cases. However, a major challenge in thoracic radiation therapy has always been **minimizing damage to the heart**, especially for tumors located near the mediastinum. Long-term cardiac toxicity can severely impact survival and quality of life for survivors. A new **heart-sparing radiotherapy technique** is now emerging as a significant advancement, offering hope for improved treatment precision and reduced side effects.

This article explores how this novel approach works, its clinical potential, and what it could mean for the future of lung cancer therapy.
