Metabolic dysfunction-associated steatohepatitis (MASH), previously known as NASH (nonalcoholic steatohepatitis), is emerging as one of the leading causes of chronic liver disease globally. Closely tied to obesity, type 2 diabetes, and metabolic syndrome, MASH can silently progress to cirrhosis, liver failure, or even liver cancer without noticeable symptoms in early stages. Despite the urgency, there are currently no FDA-approved therapies specifically for MASH.

Enter **semaglutide**, a GLP-1 receptor agonist already approved for managing type 2 diabetes and obesity. Recent clinical trial data now reveals that semaglutide does more than manage blood sugar and weight—it may **clear liver inflammation and significantly reduce fibrosis in MASH patients**, offering new hope for treating this progressive condition.
