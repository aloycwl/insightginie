Tiny Target, Giant Impact: How Scientists Modify Mosquito DNA with Project Wolbachia
====================================================================================

Mosquitoes, those minuscule bloodsuckers, are more than just an annoyance; they are the deadliest animals on Earth. Responsible for transmitting diseases like dengue, Zika, malaria, chikungunya, and yellow fever, these tiny insects cause millions of infections and hundreds of thousands of deaths annually. For decades, humanity has battled mosquitoes with insecticides, repellents, and habitat destruction, often with limited, unsustainable success. But what if the solution wasn't about eradicating them, but rather reshaping their very nature? This is the revolutionary premise behind modifying the DNA of something as small as a mosquito, a feat of modern science epitomized by the groundbreaking [Project Wolbachia](https://www.worldmosquitoprogram.org/en/wolbachia/about-wolbachia).
