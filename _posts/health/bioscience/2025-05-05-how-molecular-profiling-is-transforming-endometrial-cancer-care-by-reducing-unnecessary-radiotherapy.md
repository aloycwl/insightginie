Endometrial cancer, the most common gynecologic malignancy in developed countries, has traditionally been treated with surgery followed by radiation therapy in cases considered high risk. However, recent advances in **molecular profiling** are reshaping how clinicians determine post-surgical treatment plans. By analyzing the genetic and molecular makeup of tumors, physicians can now **better predict recurrence risk**, enabling many patients to **safely avoid unnecessary radiotherapy** and its associated side effects.

This shift toward **personalized cancer care** marks a significant milestone in gynecologic oncology. Molecular profiling not only enhances treatment precision but also improves patient outcomes and quality of life. In this guide, we explore how this innovative approach works, its clinical implications, and what it means for patients with early-stage endometrial cancer.
