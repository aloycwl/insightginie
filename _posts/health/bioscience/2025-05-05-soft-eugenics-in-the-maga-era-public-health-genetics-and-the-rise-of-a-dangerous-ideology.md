In recent years, under the influence of hardline populist ideologies like those associated with the “Make America Great Again” (MAGA) movement, there has been a disturbing re-emergence of policies and rhetoric that mirror the tenets of **eugenics**—not in its overt, 20th-century form, but in a subtler, more insidious variant: **soft eugenics**. Rather than explicit sterilization or racial classification, today's soft eugenics often operates through systemic neglect, underfunded healthcare, and implicit support for genetic determinism.

This article explores how **MAGA-aligned policies and cultural narratives are reinforcing a worldview where the vulnerable are left behind**—with sickness and poverty seen as personal failings—and how this supports a modern eugenic logic that promotes the survival and reproduction of the “clever” or “fit” over the weak or disadvantaged.
