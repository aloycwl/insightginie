Intrapleural fibrinolysis therapy (IPFT) using **alteplase** and **dornase alfa** has become a widely accepted, minimally invasive alternative to surgery for managing **complicated parapneumonic effusions** and empyema. Clinical trials, most notably the MIST2 study, have demonstrated its safety and efficacy in breaking down loculations and improving drainage outcomes.

However, as with any powerful therapeutic intervention, rare but serious complications can occur. Among these, **haemorrhagic pericardial effusion** is emerging as an extremely rare but potentially fatal adverse event. This article explores the clinical context, pathophysiology, case evidence, and critical recommendations for clinicians using IPFT.
