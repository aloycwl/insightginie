Respiratory Syncytial Virus (RSV) is a common respiratory virus that affects the lungs and breathing passages. While it typically causes mild, cold-like symptoms in adults and older children, RSV can lead to severe respiratory illnesses in infants, young children, and older adults.

### Symptoms of RSV

Common symptoms include:

* Runny nose
* Decrease in appetite
* Coughing
* Sneezing
* Fever
* Wheezing

In infants, RSV can cause more serious conditions like bronchiolitis and pneumonia, leading to hospitalization.

### Transmission and Risk Factors

RSV spreads through respiratory droplets when an infected person coughs or sneezes. It can also survive on surfaces, making it easy to contract through touch . Those at higher risk include:

* Premature infants
* Children under two years with chronic lung or heart conditions
* Adults over 65
* Individuals with weakened immune systems
