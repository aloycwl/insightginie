In recent years, *Fusobacterium nucleatum* (Fn) has emerged as a key microbial player in the pathogenesis of various cancers, particularly **colorectal cancer (CRC)**. This anaerobic bacterium, traditionally known for its role in oral infections, has been linked to **tumor progression, immune modulation, and metastasis** in cancerous tissues. As part of the growing interest in the cancer microbiome, researchers are investigating natural substances that can **interfere with Fn's pathogenic mechanisms**. One promising candidate is **L-fucose**, a deoxy sugar naturally found in dietary sources and host glycans. Studies indicate that L-fucose may **mitigate Fn's carcinogenic effects** by interfering with bacterial adhesion, altering host-microbe interactions, and suppressing inflammation.
