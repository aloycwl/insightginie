The classification of breast cancer based on HER2 (human epidermal growth factor receptor 2) expression has traditionally guided treatment decisions, particularly the use of targeted therapies. Recent advancements have identified a subset of breast cancers termed “HER2 ultra-low,” characterized by minimal HER2 expression. Accurate detection of this category is crucial, as emerging therapies like trastuzumab deruxtecan (Enhertu) show efficacy in these patients. Artificial intelligence (AI) is playing a pivotal role in enhancing the precision of HER2 ultra-low detection, thereby expanding treatment options and improving patient outcomes.
