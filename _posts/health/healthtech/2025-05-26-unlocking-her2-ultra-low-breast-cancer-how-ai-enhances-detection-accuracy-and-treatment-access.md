<p>The classification of breast cancer based on HER2 (human epidermal growth factor receptor 2) expression has traditionally guided treatment decisions, particularly the use of targeted therapies. Recent advancements have identified a subset of breast cancers termed &#8220;HER2 ultra-low,&#8221; characterized by minimal HER2 expression. Accurate detection of this category is crucial, as emerging therapies like trastuzumab deruxtecan (Enhertu) show efficacy in these patients. Artificial intelligence (AI) is playing a pivotal role in enhancing the precision of HER2 ultra-low detection, thereby expanding treatment options and improving patient outcomes.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Understanding HER2 Ultra-Low Breast Cancer</h2>

<p>HER2 status in breast cancer is determined by immunohistochemistry (IHC) scoring:</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>HER2-Positive:</strong> IHC 3+ or IHC 2+ with positive in situ hybridization (ISH)</li>

<li><strong>HER2-Low:</strong> IHC 1+ or IHC 2+ with negative ISH</li>

<li><strong>HER2-Ultra-Low:</strong> IHC 0 with faint or barely perceptible staining in ≤10% of tumor cells</li>
</ul>

<p>Identifying HER2 ultra-low tumors is challenging due to the subtlety of expression, yet it is essential for determining eligibility for certain targeted therapies.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Challenges in Detecting HER2 Ultra-Low Expression</h2>

<p>Traditional methods for assessing HER2 status, such as IHC and ISH, are subject to variability and may lack the sensitivity required to detect ultra-low expression levels. Studies have shown that interobserver consistency among pathologists is low when distinguishing between HER2-null, HER2-ultra-low, and HER2-low cases, potentially impacting treatment decisions. </p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">The Role of AI in Enhancing Detection Accuracy</h2>

<p>Artificial intelligence has emerged as a valuable tool in improving the accuracy and consistency of HER2 status assessment:</p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Improved Diagnostic Precision</h3>

<p>AI algorithms can analyze digital pathology images to detect subtle patterns indicative of HER2 expression. In a multinational study involving 105 pathologists across 10 countries, AI assistance increased the accuracy of identifying HER2 clinical categories from 90.1% to 95.0%, and concordance among pathologists improved significantly. </p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Enhanced Sensitivity in Low-Expression Cases</h3>

<p>AI tools have demonstrated increased sensitivity in detecting low levels of HER2 expression. For instance, sensitivity in identifying HER2-ultra-low cases improved from 50.08% without AI assistance to 93.22% with AI support. </p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Reduction in Misclassification</h3>

<p>The use of AI has led to a decrease in the misclassification of HER2 status. Overscoring of HER2-null cases was reduced from 45.09% without AI assistance to 11.7% with AI support, ensuring that patients receive appropriate treatment recommendations. </p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Clinical Implications and Future Directions</h2>

<p>The integration of AI into HER2 testing protocols holds significant promise:</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Expanded Treatment Eligibility:</strong> More accurate detection of HER2 ultra-low expression allows for the inclusion of additional patients in targeted therapy regimens.</li>

<li><strong>Personalized Treatment Plans:</strong> Enhanced diagnostic precision facilitates the development of tailored treatment strategies based on individual HER2 expression profiles.</li>

<li><strong>Streamlined Diagnostic Processes:</strong> AI-assisted analysis can expedite the diagnostic workflow, reducing turnaround times and improving efficiency in pathology laboratories.</li>
</ul>

<p>Continued research and validation of AI tools are essential to fully realize their potential in clinical practice.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Conclusion</h2>

<p>Accurate detection of HER2 ultra-low breast cancer is critical for guiding effective treatment strategies. The application of artificial intelligence in pathology offers a transformative approach to enhance diagnostic accuracy, reduce variability, and expand access to targeted therapies. As AI technologies continue to evolve, their integration into clinical workflows will be instrumental in advancing personalized medicine and improving outcomes for breast cancer patients.</p>
