Transthyretin (TTR), a protein primarily produced in the liver, plays a crucial role in transporting thyroid hormones and retinol-binding protein. However, misfolding of TTR can lead to amyloid fibril deposition, resulting in transthyretin amyloidosis (ATTR), a progressive and often fatal disease affecting the heart and nervous system. Monitoring TTR levels is essential for early diagnosis, treatment efficacy assessment, and disease progression tracking. With advancements in healthcare technology, remote monitoring of TTR levels is emerging as a transformative approach in patient care.
