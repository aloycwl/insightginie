In recent years, the medical community has seen a dramatic shift in how prostate cancer—especially low-risk cases—is diagnosed and managed. At the center of this transformation is artificial intelligence (AI), which is reshaping patient outcomes by helping clinicians make more accurate decisions. One of the most profound changes? A significant reduction in unnecessary prostatectomies, or the surgical removal of the prostate gland.

With AI stepping in as a powerful diagnostic ally, physicians are now better equipped to identify which prostate cancers are aggressive and which can be safely monitored. This shift not only prevents overtreatment but also preserves the quality of life for thousands of men every year.
