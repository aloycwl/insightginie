Depression and anxiety disorders affect millions worldwide, prompting the development of various antidepressant medications. Among the most commonly prescribed are Selective Serotonin Reuptake Inhibitors (SSRIs) and Serotonin-Norepinephrine Reuptake Inhibitors (SNRIs). Understanding their differences, benefits, and potential risks is crucial for patients and healthcare providers alike.
