Transdermal Optical Imaging (TOI) holds immense promise for non-invasive diagnostics, offering a window into subsurface biological processes without incisions. Yet, its widespread clinical adoption is persistently hampered by a triad of formidable obstacles: **Skin Tone, Lighting, and Motion: Challenges in Transdermal Optical Imaging**. These factors introduce significant variability and noise, critically undermining the reliability and accuracy of the acquired optical data. A meticulous examination of these interlinked challenges is crucial for advancing the field.
