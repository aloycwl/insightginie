Imagine a world where a patient's race, gender, or skin tone could silently skew their medical diagnosis—without a single word spoken. This isn't a dystopian fantasy; it's the unsettling reality of **transdermal optical imaging (TOI)** and the biases embedded within it. As healthcare increasingly relies on AI-driven diagnostics, the promise of objectivity collides with the specter of systemic inequity. TOI, a cutting-edge technology that analyzes blood flow and vital signs through the skin, holds immense potential—but only if we confront its hidden biases head-on.
