Imagine a world where your smartphone could detect early signs of hypertension, stress, or even cardiovascular disease—without a single needle prick or invasive procedure. This isn't science fiction; it's the promise of **transdermal optical imaging (TOI)**, a groundbreaking technology poised to redefine personalized medicine. By leveraging light to penetrate the skin and capture physiological data, TOI bridges the gap between traditional diagnostics and the future of non-invasive, real-time health monitoring. But how does it work, and why should healthcare providers and patients alike pay attention?
