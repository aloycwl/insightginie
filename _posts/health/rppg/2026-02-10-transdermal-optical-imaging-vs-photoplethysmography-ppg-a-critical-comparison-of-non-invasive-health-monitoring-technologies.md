Imagine a world where your skin becomes a window into your health—no needles, no invasive procedures, just a camera capturing vital signs with precision. This isn't science fiction; it's the promise of **Transdermal Optical Imaging (TOI)** and **Photoplethysmography (PPG)**, two cutting-edge technologies revolutionizing non-invasive health monitoring. But how do they stack up against each other? While both leverage light to extract physiological data, their methodologies, applications, and limitations reveal stark contrasts that could redefine medical diagnostics and wearable tech.
