<p>Quantum computing is on the cusp of shifting from experimental novelty to tangible utility—and <strong>Phasecraft</strong>, a UK-based quantum algorithms firm, is accelerating that shift. On <strong>September 2, 2025</strong>, the company raised <strong>$34 million</strong> in Series B funding to supercharge its development of efficient, hardware-agnostic quantum algorithms and bring meaningful applications closer to reality.</p>



<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>1. What the Funding Means for Phasecraft</strong></h3>



<p>This capital injection, co-led by <strong>Plural, Playground Global</strong>, and <strong>Novo Holdings’ Quantum Fund</strong>,—with continued backing from <strong>LocalGlobe, AlbionVC</strong>, and <strong>Parkwalk Advisors</strong>—marks a pivotal moment in Phasecraft’s journey. It underscores both investor confidence in quantum software innovation and a strategic pivot toward algorithm development over hardware creation.</p>



<p>Phasecraft plans to expand its team—currently around 40 employees—doubling down on R&amp;D and scaling commercial outreach, including an expanding presence in the U.S., following its hiring of a former AWS quantum scientist.</p>



<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>2. The Power of Hardware-Agnostic Quantum Algorithms</strong></h3>



<p>Unlike companies manufacturing quantum processors, Phasecraft focuses on software—specifically <strong>quantum algorithms</strong> optimized for <strong>NISQ</strong> (Noisy Intermediate-Scale Quantum) devices, which are imperfect but available now.</p>



<p>Their approach is <strong>hardware-agnostic</strong>, meaning their algorithms can run across platforms like <strong>Google Quantum AI, IBM, Quantinuum</strong>, and <strong>QuEra</strong>. This flexibility reduces dependency on a single technology path and increases compatibility across evolving quantum hardware.</p>



<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>3. Targeted Applications — Where Phasecraft is Making a Difference</strong></h3>



<p>Phasecraft is already collaborating with industry leaders in multiple domains:</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Materials science</strong>: Simulating complex materials with algorithms that make computation up to <em>millions of times more efficient</em>.</li>



<li><strong>Energy systems</strong>: Partnering with organizations like the UK’s National Energy System Operator and BT to optimize energy grid structures—vital for climate resilience.</li>



<li><strong>Pharmaceuticals and chemistry</strong>: Novo Holdings’ investment signals Phasecraft’s work is aligned with molecular simulation and drug discovery applications. Hybrid quantum-classical algorithms using approaches like density functional theory enable deeper insights into chemical behavior.</li>
</ul>



<p>These partnerships indicate early-stage commercialization—and important proof-of-concept validations—for quantum algorithms in real-world scenarios.</p>



<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>4. Messaging and Market Position</strong></h3>



<p>Phasecraft is positioning itself as a bridge builder between today&#8217;s imperfect quantum systems and tomorrow&#8217;s practical applications. <strong>CEO Ashley Montanaro</strong> foresees “scientifically important” quantum calculations by <strong>spring 2026</strong>, with commercial applications following within a few years.</p>



<p>Investors echo this optimism. <strong>Peter Barrett</strong> of Playground Global emphasizes Phasecraft is moving the industry from discovery to design—ushering in a new era of dynamism in chemistry, materials science, and medicine. <strong>Ian Hogarth</strong> of Plural lauds the firm’s ability to achieve algorithmic efficiencies by factors of millions.</p>



<p>Novo Holdings highlights the potential for quantum algorithms to dramatically shorten drug development time, a game-changer for healthcare.</p>



<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>5. Challenges and the Road Ahead</strong></h3>



<p>Despite the optimism, caution persists. Experts warn quantum computing remains uneven in maturity, requiring tempered expectations. The journey to <strong>quantum advantage</strong>—where quantum systems can outperform classical ones in meaningful tasks—is still unfolding.</p>



<p>Moreover, transitioning from R&amp;D partnerships to full-scale commercialization requires institutional buy-in and proof of consistent value beyond academic validation</p>



<p>Nevertheless, the convergence of investor backing, industrial partnerships, and algorithmic innovation puts Phasecraft in a strong position to lead in quantum software.</p>



<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Conclusion: Why This Matters</strong></h3>



<p>Phasecraft’s $34M Series B funding symbolizes more than a cash infusion—it represents a seismic shift in quantum computing’s trajectory. By focusing on <strong>software-driven impact</strong>, the firm is bringing powerful algorithms—and, with them, real-world applications—into clearer view. From battery materials to life-saving drugs and smarter energy systems, Phasecraft is building bridges that make quantum computing useful today—not just a promise for tomorrow.</p>
