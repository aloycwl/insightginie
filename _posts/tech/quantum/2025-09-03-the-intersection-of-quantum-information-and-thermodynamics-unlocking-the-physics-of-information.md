Physics and information theory are deeply intertwined, and nowhere is this more evident than at the crossroads of **quantum information and thermodynamics**. While thermodynamics traditionally governs the flow of heat and energy, quantum information theory provides the framework for understanding computation and communication at the smallest scales. Their intersection reveals profound insights: that information itself has physical consequences, and that quantum mechanics reshapes our understanding of energy, entropy, and the limits of computation.

This article explores the principles, implications, and applications of this fascinating intersection, showing how it shapes both fundamental science and emerging technologies.
