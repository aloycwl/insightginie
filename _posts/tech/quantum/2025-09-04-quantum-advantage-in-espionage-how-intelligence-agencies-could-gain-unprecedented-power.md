For centuries, espionage has been shaped by the tools of its time. From coded letters in the Renaissance to signal intelligence during World War II, every era's technology has redefined the balance of power. Now, humanity stands at the threshold of another seismic shift—**quantum computing.**

As quantum technology matures, intelligence agencies are poised to gain disproportionate advantages over both rivals and citizens. The ability to break encryption, sift through massive datasets, and simulate complex systems could elevate espionage from a secretive art to a **data-dominating science.**
