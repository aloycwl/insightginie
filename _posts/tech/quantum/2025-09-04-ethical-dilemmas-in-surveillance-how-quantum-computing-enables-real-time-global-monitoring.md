Advancements in **quantum computing** are poised to transform the world of surveillance. With unprecedented processing power, quantum systems could enable **real-time global monitoring** on a scale never before imagined. While the technology promises enhanced security and efficiency, it also raises profound ethical questions about privacy, civil liberties, and societal trust.

This article delves into the emerging ethical dilemmas surrounding quantum-enabled surveillance, the potential risks, and how policymakers, technologists, and society might navigate this complex landscape.
