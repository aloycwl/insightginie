For centuries, our understanding of computation has been inextricably linked to silicon and electrons. But what if the ultimate computer wasn't a sleek metal box, but a living cell? What if the future of processing power lay not in microchips, but in molecules like DNA, proteins, and even entire biological organisms? Welcome to the astonishing world of bio-computing, a field that seeks to harness the immense computational power inherent in biological systems. From the abstract concept of DNA logic gates to the tangible reality of self-healing 'living machines,' this journey represents one of humanity's most ambitious attempts to redefine intelligence and engineering. Prepare to explore the incredible history of how biology began to compute, challenging our very definitions of life and technology.
