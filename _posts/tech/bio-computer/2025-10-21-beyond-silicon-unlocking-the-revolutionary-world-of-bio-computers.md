For decades, our digital world has been powered by silicon – the inert, stable foundation of microchips. But what if the next leap in computing wasn't forged in a sterile cleanroom, but grown in a petri dish? Imagine computers that can heal themselves, powered by sugar, and capable of processing data with unparalleled parallelism. This isn't science fiction; it's the burgeoning reality of **bio-computers**, a revolutionary field where biology and technology fuse to redefine what computation means.
