In the relentless march of technological progress, humanity constantly pushes the boundaries of what's possible. From the first abacus to quantum processors, our tools for computation have grown exponentially in power and complexity. Now, we stand on the precipice of an entirely new paradigm: bio-computers. These fascinating, unsettling devices blur the lines between biology and technology, leveraging living matter – from DNA strands to neural networks grown in a lab – to process information. But as these 'living machines' grow more sophisticated, a profound question emerges from the scientific ether: **Can bio-computers evolve beyond human control?**
