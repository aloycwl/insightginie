Imagine computers that aren't made of silicon and wires, but of DNA, proteins, and living cells. This isn't science fiction; it's the revolutionary frontier of **bio-computing**. Promising unprecedented processing power, energy efficiency, and miniaturization, bio-computers could solve problems currently beyond our grasp, from drug discovery to advanced AI. Yet, with this incredible potential comes a unique and profound challenge: the risk of *biological contamination*. Unlike traditional electronics, bio-computers are inherently biological, making them susceptible to the very organisms and molecules that could compromise their integrity and function. This article delves into the fascinating world of bio-computers, uncovers the critical threat of biological contamination, and explores the vital strategies needed to safeguard their future.
