In an era defined by rapid technological advancement, the lines between disciplines blur with unprecedented speed. While artificial intelligence, quantum computing, and advanced robotics often dominate headlines, a far more insidious and potentially catastrophic threat is quietly emerging from the shadows: **bio-cybercrime**. This isn't just about hacking computers; it's about the weaponization of biology through digital means. As our capabilities in gene editing, synthetic biology, and interconnected healthcare systems grow, so too does the vulnerability to attacks that could redefine warfare, public health crises, and even human identity. The question isn't if this threat will fully materialize, but whether the world is adequately prepared for its devastating arrival.
