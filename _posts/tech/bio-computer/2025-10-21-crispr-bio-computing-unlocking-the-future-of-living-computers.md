For decades, the world has been powered by silicon chips, the silent workhorses of our digital age. Yet, as the demands for computational power grow exponentially, and the physical limits of silicon become increasingly apparent, scientists are looking for revolutionary alternatives. Enter the fascinating realm of **bio-computing**, where the very building blocks of life – DNA, RNA, and proteins – are harnessed to process information. And at the heart of this audacious quest lies a tool that has already transformed genetic engineering: CRISPR.
