<h2>Comparative Look: Why Weather Patterns Change So Quickly</h2>
<p>Understanding why we experience these &#8220;rollercoaster&#8221; weather patterns can help in planning for the future. Meteorologists at AccuWeather point to the jet stream—a fast-moving river of air high in the atmosphere. When the jet stream dips southward, it brings colder, wetter air; when it pushes northward, it allows warmer, drier air to dominate.</p>
<p>Currently, we are caught in the transition zone of these shifts. A northward nudge is giving us this warm weekend, while a southward shift beginning Monday is pulling in the moisture responsible for the upcoming rain. This volatility is a hallmark of this time of year.</p>
