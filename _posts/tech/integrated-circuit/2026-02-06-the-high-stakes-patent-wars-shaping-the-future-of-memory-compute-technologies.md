The battle for dominance in memory compute technologies has escalated into one of the most contentious patent wars in the semiconductor industry. As companies race to develop next-generation memory solutions—like high-bandwidth memory (HBM), compute express link (CXL), and processing-in-memory (PIM)—intellectual property disputes have become a weapon of choice. These conflicts aren't just about legal bragging rights; they dictate who controls the backbone of AI, data centers, and high-performance computing. The stakes couldn't be higher, and the outcomes will redefine technological leadership for decades.
