Imagine a world where your smartphone processes complex AI tasks in real-time without draining its battery, or where autonomous drones make split-second decisions without relying on cloud connectivity. This isn't a distant futuristic vision—it's the immediate promise of **Edge AI inference**, and in-memory computing chips are the unsung heroes making it possible. The shift from traditional von Neumann architectures to in-memory processing isn't just an incremental upgrade; it's a fundamental rethinking of how hardware and software interact at the edge.
