Processing-in-Memory (PIM) architectures promise to revolutionize computing by eliminating the von Neumann bottleneck—yet their potential remains untapped without robust compiler support. The challenge isn't just hardware innovation; it's ensuring algorithms map efficiently to PIM's distributed, memory-centric execution model. Without intelligent compiler optimizations, even the most advanced PIM hardware risks underperforming, leaving performance gains on the table. This gap between algorithmic intent and hardware capability demands a critical rethinking of compiler design.
