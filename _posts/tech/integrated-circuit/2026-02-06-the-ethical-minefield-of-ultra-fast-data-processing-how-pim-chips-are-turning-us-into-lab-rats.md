Real-time decision-making sounds great in theory. Need to detect fraud? Boom—PIM chip to the rescue. Want to optimize traffic flow in a smart city? Easy peasy. But what happens when those decisions have irreversible consequences?

Consider autonomous vehicles. A PIM chip could process sensor data in milliseconds, making split-second decisions that determine whether you live or die. But what if the algorithm's “optimal” decision is to swerve into a pedestrian to avoid a collision? Who gets to decide what's “optimal”? And more importantly, who gets to live with the consequences?

Ethics in ultra-fast data processing isn't just about speed—it's about the values we encode into these systems. And right now, those values are about as well-defined as a Rorschach test. We're building the future on a foundation of ambiguity, and the cracks are already starting to show.
