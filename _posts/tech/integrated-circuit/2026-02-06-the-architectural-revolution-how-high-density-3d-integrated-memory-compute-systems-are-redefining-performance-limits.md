The relentless pursuit of computational supremacy has hit a critical inflection point. High-density 3D integrated memory compute systems are no longer a speculative vision—they are the linchpin of next-generation computing architectures. By collapsing the spatial divide between processing and memory, these systems obliterate the von Neumann bottleneck, delivering performance gains that were once deemed impossible. Yet, their rise is not merely a technological triumph; it is a fundamental reimagining of how hardware should be designed, optimized, and deployed.
