<h2>Understanding Insider Trading and Its Significance</h2>
<p>Before we dive into the specifics of Semtech&#8217;s situation, it&#8217;s crucial to understand the concept of insider trading and why it matters to investors. Insider trading refers to the buying or selling of a company&#8217;s stock by individuals with access to non-public information about the company. This can include executives, directors, and other key employees.</p>
<p>While not all insider trading is illegal, it&#8217;s important to note that these transactions are closely monitored by the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC). Legal insider trading occurs when corporate insiders trade their company&#8217;s securities but report these trades to the SEC. This transparency allows investors to gain insights into how those closest to the company view its prospects.</p>
