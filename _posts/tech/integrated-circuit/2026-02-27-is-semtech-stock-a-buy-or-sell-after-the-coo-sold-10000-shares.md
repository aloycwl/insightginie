Understanding Insider Trading and Its Significance
--------------------------------------------------

Before we dive into the specifics of Semtech's situation, it's crucial to understand the concept of insider trading and why it matters to investors. Insider trading refers to the buying or selling of a company's stock by individuals with access to non-public information about the company. This can include executives, directors, and other key employees.

While not all insider trading is illegal, it's important to note that these transactions are closely monitored by the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC). Legal insider trading occurs when corporate insiders trade their company's securities but report these trades to the SEC. This transparency allows investors to gain insights into how those closest to the company view its prospects.
