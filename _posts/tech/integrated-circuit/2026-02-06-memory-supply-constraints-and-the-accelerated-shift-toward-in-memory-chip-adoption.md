The tech industry is at a crossroads where memory supply constraints are no longer a distant concern but a pressing reality. As demand for high-performance computing surges across AI, data centers, and edge devices, the limitations of traditional memory architectures have become glaringly apparent. The bottleneck isn't just about capacity—it's about latency, power efficiency, and the sheer inability of conventional DRAM to keep pace with modern workloads. This tension has ignited a rapid shift toward in-memory computing, a paradigm that promises to redefine how data is processed, stored, and accessed.
