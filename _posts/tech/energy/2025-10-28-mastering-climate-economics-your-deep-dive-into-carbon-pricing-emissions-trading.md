Among the most powerful and debated tools in the arsenal of climate policy are **carbon pricing** and **emissions trading systems** (ETS). These economic mechanisms aim to put a cost on carbon pollution, thereby incentivizing businesses and consumers to reduce their greenhouse gas emissions. But how do they work, what are their economic implications, and can they truly deliver a net-zero future? Let's embark on a comprehensive exploration of these vital concepts.
