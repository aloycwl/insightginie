Singapore, a tiny island nation renowned for its innovation and strategic foresight, faces a colossal challenge: securing a sustainable and reliable energy future. As a country with limited natural resources and a rapidly growing economy, the quest for energy independence is paramount. While solar and other renewable sources are being aggressively pursued, the elephant in the room – or perhaps the atom in the reactor – is nuclear energy. Often seen as a double-edged sword, nuclear power promises immense energy output with zero carbon emissions but carries the heavy baggage of safety concerns and waste management dilemmas. This article delves into the intricate web of public perception surrounding nuclear energy in Singapore, exploring the factors that shape opinion and the potential path forward for this critical discussion.
