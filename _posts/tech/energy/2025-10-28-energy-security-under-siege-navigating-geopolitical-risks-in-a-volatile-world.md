In an increasingly interconnected yet fractured world, the concept of energy security has transcended mere resource availability to become a central pillar of national and international stability. It is no longer just about having enough oil, gas, or electricity; it's about the resilience of supply chains, the vulnerability of infrastructure, and the intricate web of geopolitical relationships that dictate who gets what, when, and at what price. As global power dynamics shift and new conflicts emerge, understanding the profound interplay between energy security and geopolitical risks is paramount for policymakers, businesses, and citizens alike.
