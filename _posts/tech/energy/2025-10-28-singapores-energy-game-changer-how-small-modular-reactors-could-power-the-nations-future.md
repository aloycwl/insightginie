Singapore, a bustling global hub, faces a unique and pressing challenge: how to power its vibrant economy and growing population sustainably, securely, and with minimal environmental impact. As a land-scarce island nation heavily reliant on imported natural gas, the quest for a diversified, low-carbon energy mix is not just an ambition, but an existential necessity. Enter Small Modular Reactors (SMRs) – a cutting-edge nuclear technology that's rapidly gaining traction worldwide. Could these compact powerhouses be the ultimate game-changer for Singapore's energy future?
