**Artificial Intelligence (AI)**
--------------------------------

Systems that perform tasks typically requiring human cognition: reasoning, learning, perception, decision-making.

---

**Machine Learning (ML)**
-------------------------

AI subset where systems learn patterns from data rather than following explicit programmed rules.

---

**Deep Learning (DL)**
----------------------

Using multi-layered neural networks to learn hierarchical representations from large datasets.

---

**Neural Network**
------------------

Computational model using interconnected nodes (neurons) organized in layers to process information.

---

**Learning Paradigms**
----------------------

* **Supervised Learning**  
  Training on labeled data where inputs are paired with correct outputs.
* **Unsupervised Learning**  
  Training on unlabeled data to discover hidden patterns or structures.
* **Reinforcement Learning (RL)**  
  Learning through trial and error, optimizing actions based on rewards and penalties.
* **Self-Supervised Learning**  
  Model generates labels from the data itself.
* **Transfer Learning**  
  Applying knowledge from one task/domain to another.
* **Fine-Tuning**  
  Adapting a pre-trained model to a specific task.
* **Zero-Shot Learning**  
  Performing tasks without task-specific training data.
* **Few-Shot Learning**  
  Learning from a very small number of examples.

---

**Model Architecture & Training**
---------------------------------

* **Transformer**  
  Architecture using self-attention mechanisms; foundation of modern LLMs.
* **Large Language Model (LLM)**  
  Neural network trained on massive text data to understand and generate language.
* **Foundation Model**  
  Large model trained on broad data adaptable to many downstream tasks.
* **Parameters**  
  Learnable weights in a model.
* **Pretraining**  
  Training on large datasets before task-specific training.
* **Fine-Tuning (Training Phase)**  
  Adjusting model parameters for a specific task.
* **Training Data**  
  Dataset used to teach a model.
* **Epoch**  
  One complete pass through the training dataset.
* **Batch Size**  
  Number of samples processed before updating parameters.
* **Loss Function**  
  Measures how far predictions are from actual values.
* **Gradient Descent**  
  Optimization algorithm to minimize loss.
* **Backpropagation**  
  Algorithm for computing gradients through the network.

---

**Generative AI**
-----------------

* **Generative AI**  
  AI that creates new content (text, images, audio, video, code).
* **Prompt**  
  Input or instruction given to a generative model.
* **Token**  
  Basic unit of text processing (word or sub-word).
* **Context Window**  
  Amount of text a model can process at once.
* **Temperature**  
  Controls randomness of output.
* **Inference**  
  Using a trained model to generate predictions.
* **Diffusion Models**  
  Generate content by reversing noise processes.
* **Autoregressive Model**  
  Generates output sequentially based on previous tokens.

---

**AI Agents & Systems**
-----------------------

* **AI Agent**  
  System that perceives environment, reasons, and acts autonomously.
* **Agentic AI**  
  AI capable of multi-step planning and tool use.
* **Tool Use**  
  AI calling APIs or services to complete tasks.
* **Chain-of-Thought (CoT) Prompting**  
  Technique encouraging step-by-step reasoning.
* **RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation)**  
  Combining external knowledge retrieval with generation.
* **MCP (Model Context Protocol)**  
  Open standard for connecting AI models to external tools and data.

---

**NLP & Language**
------------------

* **Natural Language Processing (NLP)**  
  AI focused on understanding and generating human language.
* **Embeddings**  
  Dense vector representations of text meaning.
* **Semantic Search**  
  Search based on meaning rather than keywords.
* **Tokenization**  
  Splitting text into tokens.
* **Attention Mechanism**  
  Allows models to focus on relevant parts of input.
* **RLHF (Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback)**  
  Training models using human preferences.

---

**Vision & Multimodal**
-----------------------

* **Computer Vision**  
  AI that interprets visual information.
* **Multimodal AI**  
  Models that process multiple data types (text, image, audio).
* **OCR (Optical Character Recognition)**  
  Extracting machine-readable text from images.
* **Image Segmentation**  
  Partitioning images into regions.
* **Object Detection**  
  Identifying and locating objects in images.

---

**Model Behavior & Problems**
-----------------------------

* **Hallucination**  
  Model generates plausible but false information.
* **Overfitting**  
  Model memorizes training data, performs poorly on new data.
* **Underfitting**  
  Model too simple to capture patterns.
* **Bias**  
  Systematic errors or unfair outcomes.
* **Alignment**  
  Ensuring AI behavior matches human values.
* **Jailbreaking**  
  Attempting to bypass model safety constraints.
* **Prompt Injection**  
  Manipulating inputs to alter model behavior.

---

**Evaluation & Metrics**
------------------------

* **Benchmark**  
  Standardized test for model performance.
* **Accuracy**  
  Proportion of correct predictions.
* **Precision**  
  Proportion of correct positive predictions.
* **Recall**  
  Proportion of actual positives identified.
* **F1 Score**  
  Harmonic mean of precision and recall.
* **Perplexity**  
  Measure of language model quality (lower is better).
* **BLEU Score**  
  Metric for evaluating generated text.
* **Ground Truth**  
  Correct reference data.

---

**Safety & Ethics**
-------------------

* **Explainable AI (XAI)**  
  Making AI decisions interpretable.
* **Red Teaming**  
  Adversarial testing to find vulnerabilities.
* **Constitutional AI**  
  Training guided by principles instead of labels.
* **Guardrails**  
  Constraints preventing harmful outputs.
* **Data Privacy**  
  Protecting personal information.
* **Model Card**  
  Documentation describing model capabilities and limitations.

---

**Infrastructure & Deployment**
-------------------------------

* **API**  
  Interface allowing software to interact with AI models.
* **Latency**  
  Delay between input and output.
* **Throughput**  
  Number of requests processed per unit time.
* **GPU**  
  Hardware optimized for parallel computation.
* **TPU**  
  Google-designed AI accelerator.
* **Quantization**  
  Reducing numerical precision to save resources.
* **Distillation**  
  Training smaller models from larger ones.
* **Edge AI**  
  Running models on devices instead of cloud servers.

---

**Advanced Concepts**
---------------------

* **Latent Space**  
  Compressed representation space of data.
* **Emergent Abilities**  
  Capabilities appearing at scale.
* **Scaling Laws**  
  Relationships between model size, data, and performance.
* **In-Context Learning**  
  Learning from examples in the prompt.
* **Synthetic Data**  
  Artificially generated training data.
* **Mixture of Experts (MoE)**  
  Architecture routing inputs to specialized sub-models.
* **AGI (Artificial General Intelligence)**  
  Hypothetical AI matching human intelligence across tasks.
* **ASI (Artificial Superintelligence)**  
  AI surpassing human intelligence in all areas.
