### H2: From Game Dev to Bitcoin Strategist

Before becoming one of Bitcoin’s most recognizable figures, **Samson Mow** built a career in video game publishing and tech innovation. He served as **Chief Strategy Officer at Blockstream**, a Bitcoin infrastructure company, and was pivotal in launching the **Liquid Network**, a Bitcoin sidechain for faster, confidential transactions.

But it wasn’t until early 2022 that Mow shifted his focus fully to **Bitcoin nation-state adoption**—launching **JAN3**, a startup laser-focused on helping governments embrace Bitcoin as legal tender and financial infrastructure.

---

What Is JAN3?
-------------

### H2: Building the Rails for a Bitcoin World

**JAN3**, named after January 3—the date of Bitcoin’s genesis block—was founded to accelerate global Bitcoin adoption. Its mission is clear: **empower countries, cities, and communities to integrate Bitcoin into their economic systems**.

The company works on:

* Bitcoin infrastructure development (wallets, nodes, and servers)
* Education and strategic consulting for nation-states
* Layer 2 payment solutions and Bitcoin-backed sovereign tech
* Supporting Bitcoin legal tender adoption

JAN3’s flagship project is **Aqua**, a mobile wallet optimized for both Bitcoin and the Liquid Network, giving users the ability to **send, receive, and hold BTC and stablecoins**.

---

Bitcoin as Legal Tender: The Nation-State Vision
------------------------------------------------

### H2: From El Salvador to the World

One of Samson Mow’s most notable collaborations was with **President Nayib Bukele of El Salvador**, the first country to adopt Bitcoin as legal tender in 2021. Mow has since **acted as an informal advisor to multiple governments**, advocating for Bitcoin-based monetary policy in regions struggling with:

* Inflation and currency debasement
* Financial exclusion
* Over-reliance on the US dollar

Countries in **Latin America, Africa, and Southeast Asia** have shown particular interest in Bitcoin adoption, viewing it as a path to monetary sovereignty and financial modernization.

---

Why Nation-States Are Turning to Bitcoin
----------------------------------------

### H2: Economic Sovereignty in a Multipolar World

As geopolitical tensions rise and confidence in fiat currencies wavers, **Bitcoin is emerging as a neutral settlement layer** for international trade and remittances. Here’s why countries are taking Bitcoin seriously:

#### H3: Inflation Hedge

Countries experiencing hyperinflation (like Argentina and Lebanon) see Bitcoin as a hedge against currency collapse.

#### H3: Uncensorable Money

In authoritarian regimes or politically unstable areas, **Bitcoin enables freedom of transaction**, bypassing traditional financial choke points.

#### H3: Remittances and Cross-Border Payments

With low fees and 24/7 availability, **Bitcoin’s Lightning Network** is becoming a viable solution for fast, low-cost remittances.

#### H3: Digital Infrastructure Leapfrog

Like how many developing countries skipped landlines and went straight to mobile, **Bitcoin offers an opportunity to leapfrog outdated banking infrastructure**.

JAN3’s Global Impact
--------------------

### H2: Strategic Partnerships and Ambitions

Samson Mow has been active on the global stage, holding meetings with presidents, ministers, and central bankers. Notable JAN3 initiatives include:

* **Advisory roles in El Salvador and other Latin American nations**
* Developing **Bitcoin City** infrastructure—an ambitious project in El Salvador powered by volcano energy
* Collaboration with **Blockstream and other Bitcoin-native companies** to deliver infrastructure at scale
* Launch of **Bitcoin bonds** to support national development without reliance on IMF loans

These efforts align with JAN3’s long-term strategy of **creating a Bitcoin standard for public finance**.

Critics vs. Visionaries
-----------------------

### H2: Controversy and Conviction

While Mow and JAN3 have received significant praise from Bitcoin maximalists, some critics argue that **nation-state Bitcoin adoption is premature**, citing:

* Bitcoin price volatility
* Technological barriers for rural populations
* Global regulatory uncertainty

Still, Mow believes the risks of **remaining in a broken fiat system** are far greater. He often argues that **Bitcoin adoption is not about timing the market, but about escaping a doomed financial architecture**.
