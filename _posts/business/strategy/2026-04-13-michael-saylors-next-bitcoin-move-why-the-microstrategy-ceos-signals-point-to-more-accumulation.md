<p>Michael Saylor has developed a distinctive investment philosophy centered around Bitcoin as a superior store of value compared to traditional fiat currencies. His approach is grounded in several key principles:</p>
<ol>
<li><strong>Monetary Properties:</strong> Saylor argues that Bitcoin possesses superior monetary properties compared to fiat currencies, including fixed supply, divisibility, portability, and durability.</li>
<li><strong>Inflation Hedge:</strong> He views Bitcoin as an effective hedge against inflation and currency devaluation, particularly in the current economic environment of monetary expansion.</li>
<li><strong>Network Effects:</strong> The growing adoption and network effects of Bitcoin reinforce its value proposition over time.</li>
<li><strong>Long-term Perspective:</strong> Saylor emphasizes a multi-year to decade-long holding period, rather than short-term trading.</li>
</ol>
<p>Comparing Bitcoin to traditional assets, Saylor has often highlighted Bitcoin&#8217;s digital scarcity as its most compelling feature, contrasting it with the theoretically unlimited supply of fiat currencies controlled by central banks.</p>
