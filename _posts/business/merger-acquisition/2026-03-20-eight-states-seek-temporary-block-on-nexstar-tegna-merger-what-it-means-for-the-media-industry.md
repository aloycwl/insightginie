<p>Nexstar Media Group, one of the largest television station operators in the United States, announced its plans to acquire Tegna Inc. in a deal valued at approximately $5.4 billion. <p>The merger was initially announced in December 2022 and has been under review by the Federal Communications Commission (FCC) and the Department of Justice (DOJ) since then. Both companies have argued that the merger would create efficiencies and allow for better competition against digital media giants like Google and Facebook.</p>

<p>The states argue that the merger would lead to further consolidation in the media industry, potentially resulting in fewer independent voices and less diverse coverage of local news. They contend that this consolidation could lead to:</p>
<ul>
<li>Reduced investment in local newsrooms</li>
<li>Fewer journalists employed across the merged entity</li>
<li>Homogenization of news content</li>

</ul>

<p>The states also express concerns about the potential for consumer harm if the merger is allowed to proceed. They argue that the combined entity would have unprecedented market power, potentially leading to:</p>
<ul>
<li>Higher advertising rates for local businesses</li>
<li>Reduced bargaining power for cable and satellite providers</li>
<li>Less competitive pricing for retransmission consent fees</li>
<li>Decreased incentives for innovation in local broadcasting</li>
</ul>

<p>The states have also criticized the merger review process, arguing that it lacks transparency and does not adequately consider the long-term impacts of media consolidation. They are calling for:</p>
<ul>
<li>Public hearings on the merger’s potential impacts</li>
<li>Extended comment periods for stakeholders</li>
<li>Independent economic analysis of the merger’s effects</li>
<li>Greater disclosure of internal communications between the companies and regulatory agencies</li>
</ul>

<ul>
<li>Fewer minority-owned broadcast stations</li>
<li>Reduced programming diversity</li>
<li>Less representation of minority communities in media decision-making</li>
</ul>
