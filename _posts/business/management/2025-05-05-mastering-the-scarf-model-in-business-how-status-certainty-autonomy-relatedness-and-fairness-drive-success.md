In the competitive world of entrepreneurship, investing, and small business leadership, understanding what truly motivates people can be a game-changer. That's where the **SCARF model** comes in. Developed by neuroscientist David Rock, the SCARF model provides a neuroscience-based framework to improve collaboration, productivity, and trust by focusing on five key domains that influence human behavior in social settings: **Status, Certainty, Autonomy, Relatedness, and Fairness**.

These five triggers deeply impact our decision-making, engagement, and performance — whether you're managing a startup team, scaling an SME, or deciding where to invest next. In this guide, we'll break down each SCARF domain, explain how it shows up in real-world business environments, and offer practical strategies to adapt and thrive using this powerful model.
