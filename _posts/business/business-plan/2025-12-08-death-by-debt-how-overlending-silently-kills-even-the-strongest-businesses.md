In the modern economic landscape, debt is often glamorized as “leverage.” We are told that you need to spend money to make money, and that taking on loans is the fast track to scaling operations, acquiring competitors, and dominating the market. When used correctly, this is true. Debt can be a powerful accelerator.

However, there is a fine line between a calculated risk and a death sentence. When that line is crossed, leverage transforms into “overlending”—a state where a company's debt obligations exceed its ability to generate the cash flow required to service them.

Overlending is arguably the most silent killer of businesses. Unlike a PR scandal or a sudden drop in product quality, debt erosion happens in the background. It looks like growth on the surface until the moment the foundation cracks. Here is a deep dive into the mechanics of how excessive borrowing destroys businesses from the inside out.
