In the vocabulary of Silicon Valley and the broader startup ecosystem, one phrase holds more weight than any other: **Product-Market Fit (PMF)**. It is the dividing line between life and death for a new company. Before PMF, a startup is a group of people searching for a business model. After PMF, a startup is a business ready to scale.

Marc Andreessen, the legendary venture capitalist who coined the term, famously stated, “The only thing that matters is getting to product-market fit.” Yet, despite its importance, PMF remains one of the most misunderstood concepts in business. Founders often mistake a few paying customers for a market, or a cool feature for a product.

This guide dismantles the myths surrounding Product-Market Fit, provides actionable frameworks to measure it, and outlines the strategic moves required to capture it.
