Every high-performing team started somewhere—often awkward, uncertain, and fragmented. Great collaboration isn't instant; it's built over time through experience, communication, and shared challenges.

Enter the **Tuckman Model of Team Development**, a classic framework developed by psychologist Bruce Tuckman in 1965. The model outlines four key stages teams go through—**Forming, Storming, Norming, and Performing**—each bringing unique dynamics, challenges, and leadership needs.

Understanding these stages helps team leaders, project managers, and executives **diagnose team issues**, **apply the right interventions**, and **guide teams to higher performance**.
