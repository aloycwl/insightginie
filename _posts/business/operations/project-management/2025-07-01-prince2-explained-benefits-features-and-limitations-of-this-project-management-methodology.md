When it comes to project management methodologies, few names are as globally recognized as **PRINCE2** (PRojects IN Controlled Environments). Initially developed as a UK government standard for IT projects, PRINCE2 has grown into a widely adopted framework across Europe and beyond.

Used in both public and private sectors, PRINCE2 emphasizes control, documentation, and structure. But with its benefits also come some significant limitations—especially for smaller, agile, or fast-moving projects.

This article breaks down the PRINCE2 approach, explains how it works, and evaluates when it's the best fit—and when it might not be.
