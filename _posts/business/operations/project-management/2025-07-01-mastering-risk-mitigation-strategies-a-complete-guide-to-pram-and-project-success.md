In today's dynamic project environments, managing risks effectively can mean the difference between success and failure. Projects face uncertainties that can derail schedules, inflate costs, or reduce quality if left unaddressed. That's where **risk mitigation strategies** come in—a set of deliberate actions designed to reduce the impact or likelihood of adverse events.

This article dives deep into essential risk mitigation approaches, explores contingency reserves, and introduces the **Project Risk Analysis & Management (PRAM)** framework—a comprehensive methodology for managing risks throughout the project life cycle.
