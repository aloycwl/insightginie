### Introduction

Effective reporting is the cornerstone of successful **Six Sigma** deployment. It bridges the gap between strategic planning and operational execution by keeping everyone—from frontline teams to senior leadership—informed and aligned.

This article explores the essential elements of Six Sigma reporting, including crafting a **strategy summary**, leveraging the **X Matrix**, utilizing **tracking charts**, maintaining a detailed **action plan**, and implementing **corrective actions**. We also examine how **local management reviews** differ from **leadership reviews** and why both are critical to continuous improvement.
