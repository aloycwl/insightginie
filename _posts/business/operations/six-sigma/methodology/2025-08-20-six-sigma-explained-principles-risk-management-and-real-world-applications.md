In today's competitive business environment, organizations cannot afford waste, inefficiency, or defects. That's where **Six Sigma** comes in—a data-driven methodology designed to improve processes, eliminate defects, and enhance customer satisfaction. Originally developed by Motorola in the 1980s and later popularized by companies like General Electric, Six Sigma has become a cornerstone of **quality management and operational excellence**.

But Six Sigma is more than just statistical analysis—it also provides frameworks for **risk management, failure detection, and process improvement**. This article explains Six Sigma's core concepts, explores how risks are ranked and managed, and demonstrates why Six Sigma remains essential across industries.
