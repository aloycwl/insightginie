Implementing Six Sigma projects often demands profound organizational change. Successfully managing this change is crucial to embed continuous improvement and achieve measurable results. **Lewin's 3 Phases of Change — Unfreeze, Change, and Refreeze —** offers a simple yet powerful model to guide Six Sigma transformations from initiation through sustainability.

This article explores how Six Sigma practitioners can apply Lewin's model to overcome resistance, implement new processes, and ensure lasting cultural shifts for operational excellence.
