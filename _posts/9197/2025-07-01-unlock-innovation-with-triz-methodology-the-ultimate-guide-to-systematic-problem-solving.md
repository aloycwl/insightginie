What is TRIZ Methodology?
-------------------------

![](https://insightginie.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/07/PM_TRIZ-1024x393.avif)

TRIZ, an acronym for **Teoriya Resheniya Izobretatelskikh Zadatch** (Theory of Inventive Problem Solving), was developed by Soviet engineer and inventor **Genrich Altshuller** in the mid-20th century. He analyzed thousands of patents and discovered universal patterns and principles underlying successful inventions.

TRIZ is a **systematic innovation methodology** designed to:

* Identify and analyze contradictions in a problem
* Apply inventive principles and patterns
* Generate innovative, practical solutions

Unlike brainstorming or trial-and-error, TRIZ provides a logical framework to transform specific problems into general ones, then back into novel solutions.

---

Core Concepts of TRIZ
---------------------

### 1. Contradiction

At the heart of TRIZ is the idea that innovative solutions resolve contradictions—situations where improving one feature worsens another (e.g., making a product lighter but less durable). TRIZ teaches how to identify and overcome these contradictions without compromise.

### 2. Ideal Final Result (IFR)

TRIZ encourages envisioning the **ideal solution** that achieves the desired function without additional complexity or cost—driving toward simplicity and efficiency.

### 3. 40 Inventive Principles

Altshuller distilled common solution patterns into 40 inventive principles, such as segmentation, taking out, merging, or using intermediaries, which guide creative thinking.

### 4. Patterns of Evolution

Technologies and systems tend to evolve along predictable paths (e.g., increasing dynamism, automation, or miniaturization). Understanding these patterns helps anticipate future innovations.

### 5. Resources

TRIZ promotes using existing resources in novel ways—whether materials, energy, information, or space—to solve problems efficiently.

---

How TRIZ Works: The Step-by-Step Process
----------------------------------------

### Step 1: Define the Problem

Clearly describe the problem and identify the contradictions involved. What do you want to improve, and what is being negatively affected?

### Step 2: Generalize the Problem

Convert the specific issue into a more abstract contradiction using TRIZ tools and terminology. This allows leveraging existing solutions from other fields.

### Step 3: Apply TRIZ Tools

Use the 40 inventive principles and contradiction matrix to find potential solution paths.

### Step 4: Develop Solutions

Adapt the suggested principles creatively to your problem context. Prototype and test ideas iteratively.

### Step 5: Implement and Refine

Select the most feasible solution, implement it, and refine based on feedback and results.
