<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Quiz Questions</strong></h3>

<p>You are a member of a theater group.</p>

<ol class="wp-block-list">
<li>What type of play do you want to play in, what is your big scene? Explain in detail.</li>

<li>What words does your main rival have for you on being chosen?</li>

<li>A director looks dissatisfied with your performance, why?</li>

<li>The crowds leave after the play, what do you say to the empty stage?</li>
</ol>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Answer Guide &amp; Reasoning</strong></h3>

<p><strong>1. What type of play do you want to play in, what is your big scene?</strong> – This reflects your future</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Reasoning:</strong> Your choice of role and scene reveals your aspirations, ambitions, and the path you envision for your life.</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>2. What words does your main rival have for you on being chosen?</strong> – How your future self will speak to you</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Reasoning:</strong> Your rival’s reaction mirrors your internal dialogue and guidance you may receive from your future self about challenges or achievements.</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>3. A director looks dissatisfied with your performance, why?</strong> – The areas you will make mistakes in the future</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Reasoning:</strong> Your interpretation of the director’s dissatisfaction shows the mistakes or growth opportunities you are likely to face on your life journey.</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>4. The crowds leave after the play, what do you say to the empty stage?</strong> – What you will say at the end of life</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Reasoning:</strong> Your final words reflect your legacy, personal reflection, and how you perceive your accomplishments once all external validation is gone.</li>
</ul>
