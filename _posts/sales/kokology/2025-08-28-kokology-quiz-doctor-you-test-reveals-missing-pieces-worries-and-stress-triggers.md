<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Quiz Questions</strong></h3>



<p>Imagine you are a psychotherapist with your own private counseling practice.</p>



<ol class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Describe the room</strong> in detail where you hold your therapy sessions.</li>



<li>What does your <strong>first patient</strong> want to talk to you about?</li>



<li>How does your patient <strong>react</strong> when you give your professional advice?</li>



<li>You are doing paperwork when suddenly someone <strong>barges in</strong>. Who is this person?</li>
</ol>



<p>What do your answers reveal about your hidden self?</p>



<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Answer Guide &amp; Reasoning</strong></h3>



<p><strong>1. The Therapy Room</strong></p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Answer:</strong> What you describe as missing or present in the room = something you feel missing from your life.</li>



<li><strong>Reasoning:</strong> The setting you create reflects what your subconscious believes you lack or deeply desire in your own world.</li>
</ul>



<p><strong>2. The First Patient’s Topic</strong></p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Answer:</strong> The patient’s problem = your own worries.</li>



<li><strong>Reasoning:</strong> The first concern that comes to mind often mirrors what occupies your own hidden anxieties or preoccupations.</li>
</ul>



<p><strong>3. The Patient’s Reaction</strong></p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Answer:</strong> Their reaction = how you accept good advice when it’s given to you.</li>



<li><strong>Reasoning:</strong> The way your patient responds is a projection of your own ability (or inability) to take constructive guidance.</li>
</ul>



<p><strong>4. The Person Who Barges In</strong></p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Answer:</strong> That person = the one who will cause you the most stress.</li>



<li><strong>Reasoning:</strong> The intruder represents unexpected conflict or pressure, pointing to who (or what) creates tension in your life.</li>
</ul>
