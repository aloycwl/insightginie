### **Quiz Question**

Imagine yourself as a seal in a zoo, performing tricks for the visitors.  
As the show goes on, what runs through your mind while waiting for the show to end and the next bucket of fish from your trainer?

Choose your answer carefully—your response reveals your approach to work.

---

### **Answer Guide & Reasoning**

**1. Excited for the next bucket of fish**

* **Reasoning:** You are highly motivated by tangible rewards at work. Recognition and bonuses drive your performance, and you thrive when incentives are clear.

**2. Thinking about the next trick you'll perform**

* **Reasoning:** You are proactive and ambitious. You focus on opportunities to shine and consistently look for ways to improve your skills.

**3. Feeling bored and waiting for the show to end**

* **Reasoning:** You may approach work with minimal enthusiasm. Routine tasks bore you, and you tend to do only what is required.

**4. Observing the audience and trainer closely**

* **Reasoning:** You are strategic and attentive. You evaluate your environment carefully, learning from others to maximize your success.
