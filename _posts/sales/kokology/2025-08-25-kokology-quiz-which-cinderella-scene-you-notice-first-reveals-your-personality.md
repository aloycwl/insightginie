<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Quiz Question</strong></h3>

<p>Which Cinderella scene stands out the most to you?</p>

<ol class="wp-block-list">
<li>Cinderella suffered at the hands of her wicked stepmother</li>

<li>Cinderella transformed into a princess by her fairy godmother</li>

<li>Cinderella loses her slipper when the clock strikes twelve</li>

<li>The prince finds Cinderella and fits the glass slipper on her foot</li>
</ol>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Answer Guide &amp; Reasoning</strong></h3>

<ol class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Answer:</strong> Tendency to look down at people
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Reasoning:</strong> Focusing on Cinderella’s suffering may indicate a habit of judging others or being critical, noticing flaws more than potential.</li>
</ul>
</li>

<li><strong>Answer:</strong> Impracticality
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Reasoning:</strong> Being drawn to the magical transformation reflects a tendency to dream big, sometimes overlooking reality or practical limits.</li>
</ul>
</li>

<li><strong>Answer:</strong> Highly dependent on others
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Reasoning:</strong> Noticing the lost slipper shows sensitivity to reliance on others or attachment to external help for success.</li>
</ul>
</li>

<li><strong>Answer:</strong> Too average
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Reasoning:</strong> Highlighting the prince fitting the slipper suggests an emphasis on conventional or expected outcomes rather than uniqueness.</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ol>
