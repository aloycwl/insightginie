<h1>Weekend Guide: Top Things to Do in Milwaukee Area This Weekend – Egg Hunts, Festivals, &#038; More</h1>
<p>If you are looking for a fun-filled weekend in the Milwaukee area, you have come to the right place. This guide highlights the best events, activities, and attractions happening Friday through Sunday, with a special focus on egg hunts that delight kids and adults alike. From lively festivals to serene lakefront walks, there is something for every taste and age group.</p>
<h2>Top Egg Hunts in Milwaukee</h2>
<p>Egg hunts remain a beloved spring tradition, and Milwaukee offers a variety of options ranging from community park events to themed hunts at local attractions.</p>
<h3>1. Downtown Milwaukee Eggstravaganza</h3>
<p>Held at Henry Maier Festival Park, this annual eggstravaganza features over 10,000 hidden eggs, live music, and a pancake breakfast. Families can enjoy face painting, balloon artists, and a visit from the Easter Bunny. The event starts at 9 a.m. on Saturday and runs until noon.</p>
<h3>2. Shorewood Community Egg Hunt</h3>
<p>Located at Shorewood Village Park, this hunt is perfect for younger children. Eggs are color‑coded by age group, ensuring a fair chance for everyone. After the hunt, participants can join a spring craft workshop and enjoy complimentary lemonade.</p>
<h3>3. Wauwatosa Easter Egg Roll</h3>
<p>Inspired by the White House tradition, the Wauwatosa Easter Egg Roll takes place at Hart Park. Kids race decorated eggs down a gentle slope, with prizes for the fastest rolls. The event also includes a petting zoo and a local farmer’s market stall.</p>
<h3>4. Miller Park Egg Hunt (Breweries Edition)</h3>
<p>For adults who still love the hunt, Miller Park hosts an evening egg hunt on Friday night. Participants search for glow‑in‑the‑dark eggs hidden around the concourse, with each egg containing a voucher for a free craft beer sample. Live DJ sets and food trucks keep the party going until midnight.</p>
<h2>Family-Friendly Activities Beyond Egg Hunts</h2>
<p>If you have already filled your basket or are looking for alternative ways to spend the weekend, Milwaukee offers plenty of family‑oriented fun.</p>
<h3>Milwaukee Public Museum – Discovery Days</h3>
<p>The museum’s Discovery Days program runs Saturday and Sunday, offering hands‑on science experiments, dinosaur digs, and a special exhibit on Great Lakes ecosystems. Admission is free for children under 12 when accompanied by an adult.</p>
<h3>Harbor View Playground and Splash Pad</h3>
<p>Located along the lakefront, this newly renovated playground features climbing structures, swings, and a splash pad that opens at 10 a.m. on sunny days. Parents can relax on nearby benches while watching Lake Michigan glisten in the background.</p>
<h3>Milwaukee County Zoo – Zoo Lights Preview</h3>
<p>Although the full Zoo Lights festival begins later in the season, the zoo offers a preview weekend with illuminated animal silhouettes, evening keeper talks, and a special feeding session for the red pandas. Tickets are discounted after 4 p.m.</p>
<h3>Betty Brinn Children’s Museum – STEM Saturday</h3>
<p>This interactive museum invites kids to build simple robots, experiment with water rockets, and explore a mini‑city exhibit. Workshops run every hour from 11 a.m. to 3 p.m., and all materials are included in the admission price.</p>
<h2>Outdoor Adventures and Parks</h2>
<p>Milwaukee’s natural beauty shines in the spring, making it an ideal time to explore trails, beaches, and gardens.</p>
<h3>Lakefront Trail Bike Ride</h3>
<p>The paved Lakefront Trail stretches from Bradford Beach to South Shore Park, offering stunning views of the lake and city skyline. Rent a bike from one of the many shore‑side shops and enjoy a leisurely ride, stopping at the Bradford Beach pier for a photo opportunity.</p>
<h3>Boerner Botanical Gardens – Tulip Festival</h3>
<p>Boerner Botanical Gardens hosts its annual Tulip Festival this weekend, showcasing over 200,000 tulips in vibrant rows. Guided tours are available at 11 a.m. and 2 p.m., and the garden café serves fresh salads and herbal teas made from garden‑grown herbs.</p>
<h3>Kletzsch Park – Disc Golf and Picnic</h3>
<p>Kletzsch Park features an 18‑hole disc golf course that is free to use. Bring your own discs or rent a set at the park office. After a round, spread a blanket near the picnic shelters and enjoy a homemade lunch while watching the ducks glide across the lagoon.</p>
<h3>Urban Ecology Center – Riverside Hikes</h3>
<p>The Urban Ecology Center offers guided hikes along the Milwaukee River, focusing on bird watching and native plant identification. The hikes start at the Riverside Park location and last approximately 90 minutes, suitable for all fitness levels.</p>
<h2>Food, Drink, and Festival Highlights</h2>
<p>Milwaukee’s culinary scene is bustling this weekend, with several festivals and special menus that celebrate local flavors.</p>
<h3>Milwaukee Food Truck Rally – Summer Kickoff</h3>
<p>Held at the Wisconsin State Fair Park, the food truck rally brings together over 30 trucks serving everything from gourmet grilled cheese to authentic Thai curry. Live local bands perform on a small stage, and there is a dedicated kids’ zone with games and face painting.</p>
<h3>East Side Wine Walk</h3>
<p>On Saturday evening, participating boutiques and cafés on the East Side open their doors for a wine walk. Guests receive a complimentary tasting glass and can sample selections from Wisconsin vineyards and imported labels. Stops include a chocolate shop, a boutique bookstore, and a flower studio.</p>
<h3>Brewery Tour – Lakefront Brewery</h3>
<p>Lakefront Brewery offers a behind‑the‑scenes tour that includes a walk through the malt room, a peek at the fermentation tanks, and a tasting flight of their flagship beers. Tours run every hour on Saturday and Sunday, and reservations are recommended.</p>
<h3>Farmers’ Market at Highland Square</h3>
<p>The Highland Square Farmers’ Market opens Saturday morning with stalls featuring fresh produce, artisan breads, honey, and handmade jams. Live acoustic music sets a relaxed atmosphere, and a kids’ corner offers seed‑planting activities.</p>
<h2>Arts, Culture, and Entertainment</h2>
<p>Milwaukee’s vibrant arts scene offers a variety of performances, exhibitions, and interactive experiences this weekend.</p>
<h3>Milwaukee Art Museum – Special Exhibition: &#8220;Innovators of the Midwest&#8221;</h3>
<p>This exhibition showcases works by contemporary artists from Illinois, Indiana, Wisconsin, and Minnesota. Highlights include large‑scale installations, digital art pieces, and a series of talks with the curators. Admission is free on Friday evenings after 5 p.m.</p>
<h3>Marcus Performing Arts Center – Broadway Preview: &#8220;Jersey Boys&#8221;</h3>
<p>The Marcus Center presents a preview run of the beloved jukebox musical &#8220;Jersey Boys.&#8221; Shows are scheduled for Friday night, Saturday matinee, and Sunday evening. Discounted tickets are available for students and seniors.</p>
<h3>Comedy Club Open Mic – The Comedy Spot</h3>
<p>Looking for a laugh? The Comedy Spot hosts an open mic night on Saturday at 9 p.m., featuring up‑and‑coming comedians from the Milwaukee scene. Drink specials and a relaxed vibe make it a great way to unwind after a day of exploring.</p>
<h3>Indie Film Screening – Oriental Theatre</h3>
<p>The Oriental Theatre presents a weekend indie film series, featuring two critically acclaimed dramas and a classic horror revival. Each screening includes a Q&#038;A with the filmmaker or a local film critic.</p>
<h2>Nightlife and Live Music</h2>
<p>When the sun sets, Milwaukee’s nightlife comes alive with live music, dance clubs, and cozy bars.</p>
<h3>Summerfest Grounds – Pre‑Season Concert Series</h3>
<p>Although the main Summerfest festival begins later in June, the grounds host a pre‑season concert series on Friday and Saturday nights. Expect performances by regional rock bands, folk groups, and a DJ set that spins electronic beats until late.</p>
<h3>Jazz at the Shank Hall</h3>
<p>Shank Hall offers an intimate jazz experience on Sunday evening, featuring a local quartet that blends traditional standards with modern improvisation. Seating is limited, so arrive early to secure a table near the stage.</p>
<h3>Rooftop Bar – The Wanderer</h3>
<p>The Wanderer rooftop bar, located atop the Historic Plankinton Building, offers panoramic views of the city skyline. Enjoy craft cocktails made with locally sourced ingredients while listening to a live acoustic set on Saturday afternoon.</p>
<h3>Dance Club – Club Garage</h3>
<p>For those who want to dance the night away, Club Garage features a spacious floor, state‑of‑the‑art lighting, and resident DJs spinning house and techno tracks. The club offers a complimentary coat check and a late‑night snack bar.</p>
<h2>Tips for Making the Most of Your Milwaukee Weekend</h2>
<ul>
<li>Check the weather forecast each morning and pack layers; spring temperatures can vary from cool mornings to warm afternoons.</li>
<li>Purchase a Milwaukee County Transit System day pass if you plan to use buses or the light rail frequently; it saves money and reduces parking hassle.</li>
<li>Arrive early to popular events like the egg hunts and food truck rally to secure the best spots and avoid long lines.</li>
<li>Bring a reusable water bottle; many parks and festivals have refill stations to help you stay hydrated.</li>
<li>Consider downloading the official Visit Milwaukee app for real‑time event updates, interactive maps, and exclusive discounts.</li>
<li>If you are traveling with kids, pack a small snack bag and a portable charger to keep devices powered throughout the day.</li>
</ul>
<h2>Conclusion</h2>
<p>Milwaukee offers a diverse array of activities that make any weekend memorable, especially when egg hunts add a touch of seasonal charm. Whether you are hunting for colorful eggs, exploring museum exhibits, biking along the lakefront, or savoring local brews and bites, the city provides something for everyone. Use this guide to plan your itinerary, stay flexible, and enjoy all the sights, sounds, and flavors that Milwaukee has to offer this weekend.</p>
<h2>Frequently Asked Questions</h2>
<div class='faq'>
<h3>What are the best egg hunts for toddlers in Milwaukee?</h3>
<p>The Shorewood Community Egg Hunt and the Wauwatosa Easter Egg Roll are both designed with younger children in mind, featuring age‑segregated zones and gentle activities.</p>
<h3>Are any of the egg hunts free to attend?</h3>
<p>Most community egg hunts, including those at Henry Maier Festival Park and Shorewood Village Park, are free of charge. Some special events, like the Miller Park glow‑in‑the‑dark hunt, may require a ticket or purchase of a food voucher.</p>
<h3>Can I bring my dog to the outdoor events?</h3>
<p>Many lakefront parks and trails allow leashed dogs, but it’s always wise to check specific event rules. The egg hunts at festival parks typically request that pets remain at home for safety and hygiene reasons.</p>
<h3>What transportation options are available if I don’t want to drive?</h3>
<p>Milwaukee’s bus system (MCTS) and the Hop light rail provide convenient access to downtown, the lakefront, and major attractions. Ride‑share services are also widely available.</p>
<h3>Are there any COVID‑19 restrictions I should be aware of?</h3>
<p>As of the latest guidelines, most outdoor events have lifted mask mandates, but individual venues may still encourage masks in crowded indoor spaces. Always check the event website for the most current information.</p>
