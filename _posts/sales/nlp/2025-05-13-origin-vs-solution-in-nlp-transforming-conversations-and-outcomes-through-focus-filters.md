In the field of **Neuro-Linguistic Programming (NLP)**, understanding how individuals focus their attention is essential for creating change, solving problems, and building rapport. One powerful meta-program that shapes how people approach challenges is the **Origin vs. Solution** filter.

This meta-program determines whether someone focuses primarily on the **cause of a problem (origin)** or on the **desired outcome and steps to fix it (solution)**. Recognizing and working with this filter allows NLP practitioners, coaches, therapists, and leaders to align communication styles, enhance decision-making, and facilitate more effective change strategies.

In this article, we'll explore the **Origin/Solution meta-program**, its behavioral patterns, how to identify it in others, and how to apply it in real-world contexts to maximize results.

---

### What is the Origin vs. Solution Meta-Program?

Meta-programs are unconscious cognitive filters that shape how we process information. The **Origin/Solution meta-program** specifically relates to **where a person places their attention when dealing with an issue**—on its **source** or on the **resolution**.

#### 1. **Origin-Oriented Thinkers**

* Focus on **understanding the root cause** of a situation.
* Want to explore “why it happened,” “who's responsible,” or “what led to this problem.”
* Often believe that uncovering the origin is necessary before moving forward.

**Common phrases:**

* “Let's understand where this came from.”
* “We need to figure out what caused this.”
* “Before solving it, I want to know why it happened.”

**Strengths:**

* Deep insight into patterns and long-term behavior.
* Prevents recurring mistakes by identifying root issues.
* Often useful in therapy and conflict resolution.

**Challenges:**

* May become stuck in analysis or blame cycles.
* Action and change may be delayed.

#### 2. **Solution-Oriented Thinkers**

* Focus on **resolving the issue and moving forward**.
* Ask, “What can we do now?” or “How do we fix this?”
* Are more action-driven and often less interested in the past.

**Common phrases:**

* “Let's fix it and move on.”
* “What's the next step?”
* “How can we get results quickly?”

**Strengths:**

* Fast-paced, action-focused, and pragmatic.
* Avoids dwelling on the past.
* Effective in high-performance, crisis, and innovation settings.

**Challenges:**

* May overlook root causes, leading to recurring issues.
* Can be perceived as dismissive or superficial in complex situations.

---

### Real-World Implications of the Origin/Solution Filter

Understanding whether a person is **origin-focused or solution-focused** has a significant impact on:

* **Coaching and Therapy** – Choosing between reflective exploration vs. outcome-oriented techniques.
* **Workplace Problem Solving** – Aligning team communication to reduce conflict and enhance performance.
* **Conflict Resolution** – Deciding when to uncover history vs. shift toward reconciliation and next steps.
* **Personal Growth** – Recognizing personal tendencies in how one processes setbacks or challenges.

### Example Situations:

| **Context** | **Origin-Oriented Approach** | **Solution-Oriented Approach** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Conflict | “Why did this happen between us?” | “What can we do to fix this now?” |
| Career Stagnation | “What led me to feel stuck?” | “What can I change starting today?” |
| Business Problem | “Where did the system break down?” | “What's the best solution moving forward?” |

### Identifying the Filter: Are They Origin or Solution Focused?

#### 1. **Language Patterns**

Listen to how someone speaks when faced with challenges:

* “Why,” “Because,” “It started when…” → Origin
* “What's next,” “How do we,” “Let's fix it…” → Solution

#### 2. **Decision-Making Behavior**

* **Origin** types tend to pause, analyze, reflect.
* **Solution** types act quickly, experiment, and course-correct.

#### 3. **Emotional Cues**

* Origin-focused people may appear more cautious or introspective.
* Solution-focused people are often upbeat, optimistic, and direct.

---

### Communicating with Origin vs. Solution Thinkers

#### When Speaking to an Origin-Focused Person:

* Validate their need to understand the past.
* Use reflective language.
* Offer insight into causes and long-term patterns before moving to action.

**Example:**

> “It makes sense to want to get to the root of what's been going wrong. Once we understand that, we can start shaping a strategy that avoids this happening again.”

#### When Speaking to a Solution-Focused Person:

* Keep the conversation present- and future-focused.
* Emphasize actionable steps and results.
* Avoid over-explaining backstory unless requested.

**Example:**

> “Let's focus on what we can control now. Here are three quick steps we can take to move forward.”

---

### Flexibility is Key: Blending Both Filters

While people may naturally lean toward one side of the spectrum, **both filters are valuable depending on context**. The most effective communicators and practitioners **adapt their approach** to match the situation or client.

**For Coaches and NLP Practitioners:**

* Use **origin orientation** when deep insight, healing, or long-term change is required.
* Use **solution orientation** when quick wins, motivation, or practical change is needed.

**For Individuals:**

* If you're stuck in overthinking (origin), ask: *“What one step could I take right now to shift this?”*
* If you're rushing into action (solution), ask: *“What might be causing this pattern to repeat?”*

---

### NLP Techniques for Shifting Filters

#### 1. **Meta-Model Challenges**

Help origin-focused individuals move toward solution:

* “What would happen if you didn't need to know why?”
* “What outcome do you want instead?”

Help solution-focused individuals explore deeper causes:

* “What's the pattern behind this?”
* “When did this start, and what triggered it?”

#### 2. **Reframing**

* “Looking at the cause gives us power to prevent it.”
* “Looking at the solution gives us momentum to change it.”

#### 3. **Future Pacing + Timeline Work**

Combine both:

* Explore the origin to release old beliefs.
* Anchor a solution with clear future imagery and steps.

### Final Thoughts

The **Origin vs. Solution meta-program** is a powerful lens through which to understand behavior, motivation, and resistance. In NLP, it serves as both a diagnostic tool and a pathway to more tailored interventions.

Whether you're a coach guiding transformation, a leader solving business problems, or simply striving for personal growth, being able to recognize and adapt to this meta-program unlocks **deeper empathy, faster change, and more sustainable results**.

By meeting people where they are—whether they need to reflect on the past or take action toward the future—you become a more impactful communicator and change agent.
