<p>In <strong>Neuro-Linguistic Programming (NLP)</strong>, understanding how language shapes our thoughts and behaviors is a powerful tool for personal growth, communication, and coaching. One of the core concepts in NLP that helps us examine how we express beliefs is the term <strong>Lost Performative</strong>. This term refers to language patterns that imply judgments or assessments without specifying who is making them.</p>



<p>The <strong>Lost Performative</strong> is a distortion of reality, and understanding how it works can significantly improve how we communicate and challenge limiting beliefs. In this article, we will explore what a Lost Performative is, how it affects our perceptions, and how to identify and address it in both our personal lives and coaching practices.</p>



<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>What Is a Lost Performative in NLP?</strong></h3>



<p>A <strong>Lost Performative</strong> occurs when a statement implies an evaluation, judgment, or opinion, but fails to identify who is making that evaluation. It typically removes the subject from the sentence, leaving the statement to appear as an objective fact. In essence, the <strong>performative</strong> is &#8220;lost,&#8221; because we can&#8217;t tell who the evaluator is or what criteria they are using.</p>



<p>For example, consider the sentence:</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>&#8220;This is a bad idea.&#8221;</strong></li>
</ul>



<p>While the statement communicates a judgment (that the idea is bad), it doesn&#8217;t clarify who is making that judgment. Is it you, the speaker? Or is it someone else? In this case, the <strong>performative</strong> (who is making the judgment) is lost.</p>



<p>In NLP, this type of statement can be problematic because it implies that the judgment is absolute, universal, and objective, when, in fact, it&#8217;s merely the opinion or evaluation of the speaker—or some unknown authority.</p>



<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Why Does the Lost Performative Matter in NLP?</strong></h3>



<p>The <strong>Lost Performative</strong> can be problematic for several reasons:</p>



<ol class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Distorted Beliefs:</strong><br>When we make or hear judgments without knowing who is making them, they can be interpreted as absolute truths rather than subjective opinions. This can lead to the formation of <strong>limiting beliefs</strong>, where we believe something is inherently true for everyone, even though it&#8217;s based on one person’s perspective.</li>



<li><strong>Confusion in Communication:</strong><br>The <strong>Lost Performative</strong> can make communication unclear and ambiguous. If we don&#8217;t know who is making the judgment, it&#8217;s difficult to engage with or challenge the idea. This can lead to misunderstandings, especially in situations that require collaboration or feedback.</li>



<li><strong>Lack of Accountability:</strong><br>Without identifying who is making the judgment, there is no accountability for the evaluation. For example, if someone says, &#8220;This is a bad decision,&#8221; it becomes difficult to have a constructive discussion about it because we don&#8217;t know the reasoning behind the judgment. It makes it harder to learn from different perspectives.</li>
</ol>



<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Examples of Lost Performative Statements</strong></h3>



<p>Here are some common examples of <strong>Lost Performative</strong> language:</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>&#8220;This is the wrong approach.&#8221;</strong>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>The judgment here isn&#8217;t attributed to anyone. It’s unclear who believes the approach is wrong, which makes it harder to understand why it is wrong or to discuss potential alternatives.</li>
</ul>
</li>



<li><strong>&#8220;That&#8217;s not good.&#8221;</strong>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Similar to the first example, we don’t know who thinks it’s not good, nor do we understand their criteria for making that judgment.</li>
</ul>
</li>



<li><strong>&#8220;He’s a failure.&#8221;</strong>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Who is calling him a failure? This language implies a universal, absolute judgment that may not reflect the opinions of others, potentially leading to an unfair or biased conclusion.</li>
</ul>
</li>



<li><strong>&#8220;It’s impossible.&#8221;</strong>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>This is a universal evaluation with no identifiable source. While some people might believe something is impossible, others may see it as achievable. The Lost Performative makes the statement seem universally true, even though it&#8217;s subjective.</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>



<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>How to Identify Lost Performative in Communication</strong></h3>



<p>In NLP, one of the most important skills is identifying when <strong>Lost Performative</strong> language is being used, both in our own speech and in the speech of others. Here are a few tips to recognize and respond to these language patterns:</p>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading">1. <strong>Listen for General Judgments Without a Source</strong></h4>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Pay attention to statements that seem like judgments but don’t specify who is making them. For example, if someone says, “This is a waste of time,” ask, “Who decides that this is a waste of time?”</li>
</ul>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading">2. <strong>Look for Unsubstantiated Evaluations</strong></h4>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>If you hear a statement that involves an evaluation (e.g., good/bad, right/wrong) but lacks any reasoning or context, it could be a Lost Performative. Ask for clarification, like, “Who thinks this is wrong, and what makes it wrong?”</li>
</ul>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading">3. <strong>Ask Clarifying Questions</strong></h4>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>If you&#8217;re unsure about who is making a judgment, simply ask. Questions like, “Who says this?” or “On what basis is this being judged?” can help bring clarity to ambiguous statements.</li>
</ul>



<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>How to Challenge Lost Performatives in NLP</strong></h3>



<p>Once you&#8217;ve identified a <strong>Lost Performative</strong>, there are several NLP techniques you can use to challenge it and regain clarity in communication.</p>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading">1. <strong>Meta-Model Questioning</strong></h4>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>The <strong>Meta-Model</strong> in NLP involves asking specific questions to challenge language distortions. When confronted with a <strong>Lost Performative</strong>, you can ask:
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>“Who says that?”</li>



<li>“According to whom is this wrong/good?”</li>



<li>“What makes you think that?”</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>



<p>These questions help bring the lost evaluation into the open and allow for a deeper discussion about the criteria behind the judgment.</p>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading">2. <strong>Reframe the Statement</strong></h4>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>After identifying the Lost Performative, you can reframe the statement to make it more specific. For example, instead of saying, “This is a bad idea,” reframe it as, “I think this idea might not work because of X, Y, and Z.” This makes it clear that the judgment is the speaker’s personal perspective and opens the conversation for discussion.</li>
</ul>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading">3. <strong>Encourage Exploration of Perspectives</strong></h4>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>When you hear a <strong>Lost Performative</strong>, invite others to explain their reasoning or offer alternative viewpoints. For example, ask, “What criteria are you using to evaluate this?” or “How would someone with a different perspective view this?”</li>
</ul>



<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Conclusion</strong></h3>



<p>The concept of the <strong>Lost Performative</strong> in NLP reveals how language patterns can distort our perception of reality and influence our communication. By recognizing and challenging <strong>Lost Performatives</strong>, we can develop a deeper understanding of how judgments are made, improve the clarity of our communication, and create more flexible, open-minded perspectives.</p>



<p>Whether you&#8217;re a coach, therapist, or simply someone interested in improving your communication skills, identifying and addressing Lost Performatives can be a powerful tool for fostering clearer, more constructive conversations.</p>
