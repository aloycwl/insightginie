In **Neuro-Linguistic Programming (NLP)**, understanding how individuals mentally structure information is critical for effective communication, personal growth, and behavioral change. One key framework used in NLP is the distinction between **Black & White thinking** and **Continuum thinking**.

This article explores the meaning of this distinction, its implications for communication and problem-solving, and how NLP practitioners use this model to expand cognitive flexibility, reframe limiting beliefs, and create more empowering mental maps.
