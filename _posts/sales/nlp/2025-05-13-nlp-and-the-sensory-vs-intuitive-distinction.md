In the realm of **Neuro-Linguistic Programming (NLP)**, one of the foundational principles is that people perceive and interpret the world in unique ways based on their internal representations. A critical, though often misunderstood, dimension in this perceptual model is the **Sensory vs. Intuitive** distinction.

While this dichotomy is more prominently discussed in **personality typologies** such as the Myers-Briggs Type Indicator (MBTI), NLP integrates similar ideas to enhance rapport, precision in communication, and behavioral flexibility. This article explores what Sensory and Intuitive orientations mean in NLP, how they affect language patterns, and how you can apply this understanding to become a more effective communicator, coach, leader, or negotiator.
