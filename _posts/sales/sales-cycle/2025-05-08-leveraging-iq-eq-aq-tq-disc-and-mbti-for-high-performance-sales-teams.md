The effectiveness of a salesperson no longer hinges solely on charisma or product knowledge. In today's dynamic, data-driven sales environments, assessing a salesperson's performance and potential requires a **holistic, multi-dimensional approach**. From **innate intelligence (IQ)** to **emotional intelligence (EQ)**, **technological aptitude (TQ)**, and **acquired intelligence (AQ)**, organizations need to evaluate capabilities that align with the demands of modern selling. Personality frameworks such as **DISC** and **MBTI** further enhance the assessment process by illuminating behavioral tendencies and communication styles. This article explores these frameworks and their significance in creating high-performance sales teams.
