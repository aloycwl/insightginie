Neuro-Linguistic Programming (NLP) is a transformative tool that enables individuals to better understand and influence human behavior. Developed in the 1970s by Richard Bandler and John Grinder, NLP provides a set of techniques and strategies that can help individuals communicate more effectively, break negative patterns, and ultimately achieve their goals. The practice draws from the idea that by studying the connection between our neurological processes, language, and behavioral patterns, we can enhance our ability to achieve personal and professional success.

In this article, we will delve into the key principles of NLP, such as rapport building, the six levels of change, and effective techniques like the bell jar distraction. We will also explore the core assumptions that guide NLP and how they can be applied to improve interpersonal relationships, decision-making, and overall self-development.
