Oh, the future of digital labor—where your most reliable colleague doesn't ask for a raise, never complains about the office coffee, and won't judge you for eating lunch at your desk. No, it's not your overworked intern; it's your new AI overlord, disguised as a helpful little algorithm. Human-AI collaboration is here, and if you're not already sweating about whether your job will be outsourced to a chatbot, you're either in denial or the CEO of a tech startup.

But let's not get ahead of ourselves. The future of digital labor isn't just about robots stealing jobs—it's about them \*helping\* us do our jobs so poorly that we'll beg for the sweet release of unemployment. Imagine a world where your AI assistant drafts emails so passive-aggressive they make your actual passive-aggressive coworker look like a saint. Or where your “collaborative” AI suggests meeting times that somehow always conflict with your lunch break. Progress, folks.
