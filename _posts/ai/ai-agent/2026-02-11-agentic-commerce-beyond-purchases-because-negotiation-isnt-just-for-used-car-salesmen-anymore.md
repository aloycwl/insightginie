Let's be honest—commerce has always been a bit of a circus. You've got your ringmasters (the sellers), your clowns (the marketers), and your poor, exhausted audience (the buyers) clapping along while getting pickpocketed. But what if I told you the future of buying and selling isn't just about slapping a price tag on something and hoping for the best? Oh no, we're entering the era of **agentic commerce beyond purchases**, where negotiation and contracts aren't just for high-stakes boardrooms or shady back-alley deals. Welcome to the big leagues, folks.

Agentic commerce isn't just another buzzword to make consultants sound smart at conferences. It's the idea that autonomous agents—whether AI, bots, or some hyper-caffeinated intern—can handle the messy, complicated, and often soul-crushing parts of commerce that humans would rather avoid. Think of it as outsourcing the awkwardness of haggling, the tedium of contract reviews, and the existential dread of realizing you just agreed to terms that would make a medieval serf blush.
