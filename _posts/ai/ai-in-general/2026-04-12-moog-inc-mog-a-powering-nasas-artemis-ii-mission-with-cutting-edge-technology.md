<h2>What is Artemis II?</h2>
<p>The Artemis program represents NASA&#8217;s ambitious initiative to return humans to the moon and establish a sustainable presence there. Artemis II, scheduled for launch in the coming years, will be the first crewed mission of the Artemis program and will mark humanity&#8217;s first return to lunar orbit since the Apollo missions of the 1960s and 1970s.</p>
<p>Unlike its predecessor Artemis I, which was an uncrewed test flight, Artemis II will carry astronauts on a mission to orbit the moon and return to Earth. This mission will serve as a comprehensive test of the Space Launch System (SLS) rocket, Orion spacecraft, and all associated systems with humans aboard, paving the way for Artemis III, which aims to land the first woman and the next man on the lunar surface.</p>
<p>The mission&#8217;s objectives include:</p>
<ul>
<li>Demonstrating NASA&#8217;s commitment to deep space exploration</li>
<li>Testing Orion&#8217;s systems in the actual environment of deep space</li>
<li>Validating critical operations and procedures for future lunar missions</li>
<li>Expanding human presence beyond low Earth orbit</li>
</ul>
