Artificial General Intelligence (AGI) has long been a concept from science fiction. But today, it's no longer confined to the pages of novels or futuristic films. AGI — machines with the ability to perform any intellectual task that a human can — is drawing closer to reality thanks to exponential advances in deep learning, compute power, and massive data access.

While AGI holds the promise of solving some of the world's most complex problems — from climate change to disease eradication — it also brings significant and potentially existential risks. Unlike Narrow AI, which is built for specific tasks, AGI could become unpredictable, uncontrollable, and even superior to humans in virtually every field.

In this article, we'll examine the real and pressing risks posed by AGI, why experts are sounding the alarm, and what actions society must take to ensure a safe and ethical future with artificial intelligence.
