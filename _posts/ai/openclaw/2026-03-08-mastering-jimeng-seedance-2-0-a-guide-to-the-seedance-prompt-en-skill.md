<h1>Mastering Jimeng Seedance 2.0: A Comprehensive Guide to the Seedance-Prompt-En Skill</h1>
<p>In the rapidly evolving world of artificial intelligence, multimodal video generation has become a cornerstone of creative expression. ByteDance&#8217;s Jimeng Seedance 2.0 stands at the forefront of this technology, allowing users to synthesize high-quality video content from a mix of text, images, videos, and audio. However, the true potential of such a powerful model is often locked behind the complexity of prompt engineering. This is where the <strong>Seedance-Prompt-En</strong> skill from the OpenClaw repository becomes an indispensable tool for content creators, marketers, and video editors alike.</p>
<h2>What is the Seedance-Prompt-En Skill?</h2>
<p>The Seedance-Prompt-En skill is a specialized prompt engineering template designed specifically for Jimeng Seedance 2.0. It acts as an expert advisor, providing users with the syntax, best practices, and structured frameworks required to generate professional-grade AI videos. Whether you are creating e-commerce ads, cinematic short dramas, or educational content, this skill ensures that your inputs are interpreted correctly by the AI.</p>
<h2>The Core: The @ Reference System</h2>
<p>The most important feature within Seedance 2.0 is the <code>@</code> reference system. Without proper referencing, the AI may struggle to maintain character consistency, replicate specific camera movements, or match audio beats. The Seedance-Prompt-En skill teaches you how to assign specific roles to your uploaded assets:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Asset Assignment:</strong> By typing <code>@Image1</code>, <code>@Video1</code>, or <code>@Audio1</code>, you tell the model exactly which file to analyze.</li>
<li><strong>Role Definition:</strong> It is not enough to just upload an image; you must define its purpose. For example, using the prompt syntax &#8220;<code>@Image1 as the subject</code>&#8221; or &#8220;<code>scene references @Image3</code>&#8221; creates clear instruction sets for the generative model.</li>
<li><strong>Multi-Reference Combinations:</strong> The real magic happens when you layer references. You can command the AI to take the character from one image, the camera movement from a specific video, and the background music from an audio track all in a single prompt.</li>
</ul>
<h2>Understanding System Constraints</h2>
<p>Before diving into creation, it is vital to understand the technical boundaries. Seedance 2.0 has specific input limits to maintain stability and output quality:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Input Types:</strong> You can upload up to 9 images, 3 videos, and 3 audio files, with a maximum of 12 files combined.</li>
<li><strong>Duration and Resolution:</strong> Users can select video durations between 4 and 15 seconds, with resolutions ranging from 480p up to 720p.</li>
<li><strong>Safety Compliance:</strong> Please note that the platform strictly forbids the use of realistic human faces in uploaded materials. Attempting to bypass this will lead to blocked uploads, so ensure your source material adheres to safety guidelines.</li>
</ul>
<h2>The Prompt Structure Blueprint</h2>
<p>To avoid &#8220;AI hallucinations&#8221; or inconsistent output, the Seedance-Prompt-En guide suggests a specific formula for success: <strong>[Subject/Character Setup] + [Scene/Environment] + [Action/Motion Description] + [Camera Movement] + [Timing Breakdown] + [Transitions/Effects] + [Audio/Sound Design] + [Style/Mood]</strong>.</p>
<p>For videos longer than 10 seconds, the skill recommends a time-segmented approach. By breaking your prompt into chunks—such as 0–3s for the opening scene and 10–15s for the resolution—you gain granular control over the narrative flow, ensuring the video doesn&#8217;t lose momentum or coherence halfway through.</p>
<h2>Camera Language and Advanced Techniques</h2>
<p>The Seedance-Prompt-En skill empowers users to speak the &#8220;language of film.&#8221; Instead of vague descriptions, you are encouraged to use industry-standard camera terminology:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Basic Movements:</strong> Use terms like &#8220;Push in,&#8221; &#8220;Pan left,&#8221; or &#8220;Orbit&#8221; to direct the virtual camera.</li>
<li><strong>Shot Sizes:</strong> Control the framing by specifying &#8220;Close-up,&#8221; &#8220;Medium shot,&#8221; or &#8220;Wide shot.&#8221;</li>
<li><strong>Advanced Cinematography:</strong> For more complex scenes, utilize terms like &#8220;Hitchcock zoom&#8221; (dolly zoom) or &#8220;First-person POV&#8221; to create immersive visual experiences.</li>
</ul>
<h2>Capability-Specific Use Cases</h2>
<p>The beauty of this skill lies in its versatility. Here are just a few ways you can apply it:</p>
<h3>1. Character Consistency</h3>
<p>By anchoring a character to a reference image, you prevent the common AI issue of facial &#8220;morphing.&#8221; The skill guide provides examples of how to keep a character&#8217;s outfit and features consistent across multiple shots, whether they are walking down a hallway or interacting with other characters.</p>
<h3>2. Video Extension</h3>
<p>If you have an existing clip that needs to be longer, Seedance 2.0 can generate footage that continues the action. By setting the generation duration to match the desired extension length, you can add new scenes or even prepend footage to the start of a clip.</p>
<h3>3. Creative Template Replication</h3>
<p>Perhaps you have an ad style you love. You can reference the camera movement and transition style of a source video while replacing the content with your own subjects, effectively &#8220;skinning&#8221; your video with a professional aesthetic.</p>
