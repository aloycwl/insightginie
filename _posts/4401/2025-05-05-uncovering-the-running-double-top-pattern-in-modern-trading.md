In technical analysis, the **Double Top** is a well-known reversal pattern signaling a shift from bullish to bearish sentiment. However, not all double tops are created equal. The **Running Double Top** adds a unique dimension—it occurs in fast-moving markets where the second peak marginally exceeds the first before a strong reversal. Recognizing and properly trading this variation requires a nuanced understanding of price action and momentum.

In this guide, we’ll break down the **Running Double Top**, highlight its distinctive features, and provide actionable strategies to help traders confidently spot and trade this dynamic reversal pattern.

---

### **What Is a Running Double Top Pattern?**

A **Running Double Top** is a variant of the traditional double top, characterized by a **second peak that slightly exceeds the first high**, rather than matching it precisely. This subtle breakout often **traps breakout buyers** before the market swiftly reverses—hence the term “running.” The price action is often deceptive, giving the appearance of trend continuation before flipping to the downside.

**Key Characteristics:**

* **Two peaks**: The second high exceeds the first slightly (a “fake breakout”).
* **Neckline support**: Price pulls back to a common support level between the peaks.
* **False breakout trap**: Momentum stalls after the second high, leading to a sharp sell-off.
* **Increased volume or divergence**: Often accompanied by **bearish RSI/MACD divergence** or low breakout volume.

---

### **Why the Running Double Top Matters for Traders**

The running variation is significant because it highlights **market psychology**—specifically the **exhaustion of buyers** and the **aggressiveness of sellers** following a fake breakout. It’s a valuable pattern for:

* Catching high-probability **reversal trades**
* **Trapping late buyers** at the top of the trend
* Managing risk through clearly defined **entry and stop-loss levels**

This pattern is especially effective in **volatile markets** like Forex, cryptocurrencies, and high-growth tech stocks.

---

### **Real-World Example: Running Double Top in Action**

Let’s consider a common example in the **cryptocurrency market**:

#### **Bitcoin (BTC/USD) Example**

* First peak forms at $48,000
* A brief retracement down to $45,500
* Price surges to $48,500 (second top), triggering breakout traders
* Momentum fades, RSI shows divergence
* Price sharply reverses and breaks the neckline at $45,500
* Traders enter short positions, targeting $43,000 or lower

This scenario shows how the **running double top** traps late buyers, reverses aggressively, and rewards early bears with strong follow-through.

---

### **How to Trade the Running Double Top Pattern**

#### **1. Spot the First Peak and Pullback**

Track the initial rally and identify a clear retracement. Draw a horizontal **neckline** connecting the bottom of the retracement.

#### **2. Watch for the Second Top (and False Breakout)**

Once the price exceeds the first peak marginally, stay alert. If **momentum is weaker**, or indicators diverge, it signals a possible fake-out.

#### **3. Wait for Confirmation (Neckline Break)**

Enter a **short position** after the neckline breaks. This confirms the failure of the breakout and validates the pattern.

#### **4. Set Entry, Stop, and Target**

* **Entry:** On neckline break
* **Stop-loss:** Just above the second peak
* **Target:** Measure the height from the peaks to the neckline and project downward

#### **5. Use Divergence and Volume**

Bearish divergence in RSI or MACD, or weak volume on the second peak, can strengthen the signal.

---

### **Best Practices and Pro Tips**

* Avoid early entries: Wait for neckline break to confirm the pattern.
* Use candlestick confirmation: Look for bearish engulfing or reversal candles near the second peak.
* Combine with Fibonacci: Second peaks often align with the 127.2% or 161.8% extensions of the first peak-to-valley leg.

---

### **Conclusion: Running Double Tops as a Tool for Tactical Traders**

The **Running Double Top** pattern is a powerful variation that traps the unwary and rewards the prepared. By understanding its structure, psychology, and indicators, traders can capitalize on quick reversals in overheated markets. It’s a dynamic tool to add to any trader’s technical analysis playbook—especially when market momentum begins to fade.
