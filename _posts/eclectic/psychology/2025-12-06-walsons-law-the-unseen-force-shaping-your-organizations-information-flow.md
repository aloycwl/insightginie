In the intricate dance of modern organizations, information is the lifeblood. It fuels decisions, drives innovation, and connects teams. Yet, despite unprecedented access to data and sophisticated communication tools, a pervasive challenge often undermines even the most well-intentioned efforts: the silent erosion of information as it travels. This phenomenon, often referred to as **Walson's Law**, is a critical principle that every leader, manager, and team member must understand to ensure their organization thrives.

At its core, Walson's Law posits that *the value, accuracy, and actionable relevance of information diminish exponentially as it traverses increasing layers of an organizational structure or communication network*. It's not merely about information overload; it's about the inherent decay that occurs when messages are filtered, interpreted, summarized, or simply delayed across multiple touchpoints. Ignoring Walson's Law can lead to costly miscommunications, flawed decisions, and a significant drag on organizational efficiency.
