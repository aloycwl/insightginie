Why Everyone Thinks You Agree With Them: Unmasking the False Consensus Effect
=============================================================================

Have you ever found yourself in a discussion, utterly convinced that your perspective is the common-sense, widely accepted view, only to be baffled by strong disagreement? Or perhaps you've launched a new idea at work, certain it would be a hit, only for it to fall flat? Chances are, you've encountered the powerful, often invisible, force known as the **False Consensus Effect**.

This pervasive cognitive bias leads us to overestimate the extent to which others share our beliefs, attitudes, and behaviors. It's a fundamental quirk of human psychology that shapes our perceptions, influences our decisions, and can lead to significant misunderstandings in both personal and professional life. In a world increasingly polarized, understanding this bias isn't just an academic exercise – it's a crucial step towards fostering empathy, improving communication, and making more informed choices.
