The Wallenda Effect: How Fear of Failure Can Sabotage Your Success
==================================================================

In 1978, the legendary aerialist Karl Wallenda, patriarch of the Flying Wallendas, attempted a walk between two towers in San Juan, Puerto Rico. He was 73 years old, and though he had walked countless high wires in his lifetime, this particular walk was different. Before the performance, he was quoted as saying, “I can't afford to fall this time.” Tragically, he did fall, plummeting to his death. His family later speculated that this was the only time Wallenda had focused on *not falling*, rather than on the act of walking the wire itself. This profound shift in focus gave birth to a psychological phenomenon known as **The Great Wallenda Effect**.
