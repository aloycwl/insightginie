Why Your Memories Fade: Unpacking the Science of Decay Theory
=============================================================

Have you ever struggled to recall a fact you were sure you knew yesterday? Or found details of a past event surprisingly hazy? This common human experience is at the heart of one of psychology's foundational explanations for forgetting: **Decay Theory**. It's a concept that suggests our memories, much like an old photograph, simply fade away if not actively maintained. But is it truly that simple? And what can we do to preserve those precious recollections?

In this comprehensive guide, we'll dive deep into Decay Theory, exploring its origins, its core principles, and the evidence that supports — and challenges — its claims. We'll also examine its relationship to other theories of forgetting and, crucially, uncover practical strategies to help you combat the relentless march of time against your memory.
