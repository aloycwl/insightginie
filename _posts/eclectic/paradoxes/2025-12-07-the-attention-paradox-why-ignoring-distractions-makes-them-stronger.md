Try this experiment right now: For the next ten seconds, do not think of a white bear.

You can think of a car, your lunch, or your job. You can think of anything in the universe, as long as it is not a white bear.

Did you succeed? Probably not. In fact, if you are like most people, the image of a white bear likely flooded your mind the moment you were told to suppress it.

This is the **Attention Paradox**. It reveals a fundamental and often frustrating truth about the human mind: **The act of trying to ignore something is, in itself, a form of paying attention.**

We tend to view attention as a spotlight—we assume we can simply turn it away from what we don't want to see. But the brain doesn't work that way. When you actively try to “un-see” or “un-think” something, you are engaging in a mental wrestle that requires significant cognitive energy. By resisting the thought, you are tethering yourself to it.
