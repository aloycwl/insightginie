We all buy into the same dream. We tell ourselves, “If I just got that promotion, if I just hit six figures, or if I could just afford that house in the hills, I would finally be happy.”

We view our lives as a linear equation: More Money = More Happiness.

On a micro level, this feels true. When you get a raise, you feel a burst of excitement. When you buy a new car, you feel a surge of pride. But if we zoom out and look at the data over the span of decades, this equation breaks down completely.

This is the **Easterlin Paradox**. It is one of the most famous and perplexing findings in the field of economics, and it challenges everything we think we know about the pursuit of wealth. It suggests that while we are richer than our grandparents, we are not one bit happier.
