The Dark Side of Agreement: Navigating the Unanimity Paradox
============================================================

In countless scenarios, from jury rooms to corporate board meetings, unanimity is often hailed as the gold standard. A decision reached by every single member, without a dissenting voice, feels inherently right, robust, and beyond reproach. It signals complete consensus, eliminating doubt and fostering confidence. But what if this seemingly perfect agreement hides a dangerous flaw? What if the absence of dissent is not a sign of optimal decision-making, but rather a symptom of a deeper, more insidious problem? Welcome to the intriguing and often counter-intuitive world of the **Unanimity Paradox**.
