It is the ultimate fantasy of every investor. You are sitting at your computer, you read a news headline or notice a trend that no one else has spotted, and you buy a stock right before it skyrockets. You outsmarted the system. You beat the market.

But according to one of the most controversial and resilient theories in finance, this fantasy is mathematically impossible.

Welcome to the **Efficient Market Paradox**. It is a concept that suggests that the stock market is a ruthlessly efficient information-processing machine. It claims that the price of any given stock at any given moment already reflects every scrap of available information—earnings reports, global news, rumors, and future expectations.

If this is true, then “undervalued” stocks do not exist. There are no bargains. And the hours you spend researching companies are not just difficult work—they are a complete waste of time.
