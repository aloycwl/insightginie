We live in the age of the “Happiness Industrial Complex.”

Scroll through your social media feed, walk through the self-help section of a bookstore, or listen to the latest top-charting podcasts. The message is singular, relentless, and deafening: *You should be happy, and if you aren't, you are doing something wrong.*

We treat happiness as a metric to be optimized, a goal to be achieved, or a destination to be reached. We meditate, we journal, we manifest, and we buy products promising bliss. Yet, data suggests that despite our obsession with well-being, anxiety and depression rates are climbing.

This is the **Happiness Paradox**.

The paradox posits a cruel psychological reality: **The more you value and actively search for happiness, the more elusive it becomes.** By making happiness your primary goal, you ironically create the conditions for misery.
