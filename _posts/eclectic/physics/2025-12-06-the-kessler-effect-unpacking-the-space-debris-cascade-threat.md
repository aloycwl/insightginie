The Kessler Effect: Unpacking the Space Debris Cascade Threat
=============================================================

Imagine a future where the sky above us, once a symbol of endless possibilities, is instead a dangerous minefield. A future where launching a satellite, sending humans to the International Space Station, or even relying on GPS for navigation becomes an impossible gamble. This isn't science fiction; it's a very real scenario known as the **Kessler Effect**, and it looms large over humanity's continued presence in space.

For decades, we've been launching satellites, probes, and human missions into orbit. Each launch leaves behind a trail, not just of accomplishments, but also of spent rocket stages, defunct satellites, and fragments from collisions. This ever-growing cloud of space junk, or orbital debris, poses a critical threat, and the Kessler Effect describes the terrifying tipping point where this problem spirals out of control.
