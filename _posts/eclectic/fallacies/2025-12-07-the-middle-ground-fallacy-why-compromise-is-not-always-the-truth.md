In a polarized world, we are often taught that the most virtuous position is the center. We are told to “meet in the middle,” to “find common ground,” and to avoid extremism at all costs. In negotiation, politics, and relationships, compromise is indeed a valuable tool for maintaining peace.

However, when it comes to logic and the pursuit of objective facts, this instinct can lead us into a dangerous trap.

This is the **Middle Ground Fallacy** (formally known as *Argumentum ad Temperantiam* or the Argument to Moderation).

This fallacy posits that the truth is always a compromise between two opposing positions. It assumes that if two groups have conflicting viewpoints, the correct answer must lie somewhere in the gray area between them. But reality is rarely so convenient. Sometimes, one side is completely right, and the other is completely wrong. By blindly seeking the middle, we validate falsehoods and dilute the truth.
