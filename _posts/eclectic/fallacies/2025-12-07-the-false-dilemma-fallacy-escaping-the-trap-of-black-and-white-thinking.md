“You are either with us, or you are against us.”  
“It's my way or the highway.”  
“Choose: do you want a clean environment or a strong economy?”

We encounter these ultimatums every day. They appear in political speeches, high-pressure sales tactics, and even heated arguments with loved ones. They force us to pick a side, creating a sense of urgency and clarity. The problem? They are almost always lying to you.

This rhetorical device is known as the **False Dilemma Fallacy** (often called the False Dichotomy or the “Either-Or” Fallacy).

It is a manipulative logic trap that reduces a complex spectrum of possibilities into two mutually exclusive extremes. By erasing the nuance, the middle ground, and the creative alternatives, the False Dilemma forces you into a corner. To become a better thinker, negotiator, and decision-maker, you must learn to recognize when your options are being artificially limited—and how to break free.
