We have all been there. You are in the middle of a discussion—perhaps about politics, a workplace project, or even household chores—and you make a nuanced point. Suddenly, your opponent counters with an exaggerated, twisted version of what you just said, attacking that distortion instead of your actual argument.

You stand there baffled, thinking, *“That is not what I said at all.”*

Welcome to the **Straw Man Fallacy**. It is one of the most prevalent and frustrating logical fallacies in modern discourse. From cable news pundits to Twitter threads and dinner table arguments, the Straw Man is the weapon of choice for those who are more interested in “winning” a debate than finding the truth.

To navigate the complex world of communication effectively, you must understand how this manipulation tactic works, why it is so effective, and, most importantly, how to dismantle it.
