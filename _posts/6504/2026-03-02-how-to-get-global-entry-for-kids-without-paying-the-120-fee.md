Introduction
------------

Traveling with kids can be a daunting experience, especially when it comes to navigating through airport security and customs. The Global Entry program offers a solution by providing expedited entry into the United States for pre-approved, low-risk travelers. However, the $120 fee for a five-year membership can be a significant expense for families. In this blog post, we will explore how to get Global Entry for your kids without paying the full fee, ensuring a smoother and more cost-effective travel experience.

What is Global Entry?
---------------------

Global Entry is a U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) program that allows expedited clearance for pre-approved, low-risk travelers upon arrival in the United States. Members can use automated kiosks at select airports to complete their entry process quickly, avoiding long lines and reducing wait times. The program is particularly beneficial for frequent travelers and families, as it includes TSA PreCheck benefits, allowing for faster security screening at airports.

Why Consider Global Entry for Kids?
-----------------------------------

While the $120 fee might seem steep, the benefits of Global Entry for kids can outweigh the cost, especially for families who travel frequently. Here are some reasons to consider Global Entry for your children:

* **Reduced Stress:** Expedited processing means less time spent in lines, which can be particularly challenging with young children.
* **Time Savings:** Faster entry and security screening can help you make tight connections and reduce overall travel time.
* **Convenience:** TSA PreCheck benefits allow kids to keep their shoes on and avoid removing laptops and liquids, making the security process smoother.

How to Get Global Entry for Kids Without Paying the Full Fee
------------------------------------------------------------

Fortunately, there are several ways to obtain Global Entry for your kids without paying the full $120 fee. Here are some strategies to consider:

### 1. Use a Credit Card with Global Entry Reimbursement

Many premium travel credit cards offer Global Entry fee reimbursement as a benefit. By using one of these cards to pay for the application fee, you can effectively get Global Entry for free. Some popular credit cards that offer this benefit include:

* Chase Sapphire Reserve
* American Express Platinum Card
* Citi / AAdvantage Executive World Elite Mastercard

Be sure to check the terms and conditions of your credit card to ensure you are eligible for the reimbursement.

### 2. Take Advantage of Military Benefits

If you or your spouse is an active duty military member, you may be eligible for Global Entry at no cost. The Department of Defense (DoD) offers this benefit to service members and their families as part of their travel benefits. Check with your installation’s travel office for more information on how to apply.

### 3. Look for Promotional Offers

Occasionally, CBP and other organizations may offer promotional deals or discounts on Global Entry applications. Keep an eye out for these opportunities, especially during travel expos or through travel-related newsletters and websites.

### 4. Apply for a NEXUS Card

If you frequently travel between the U.S. and Canada, consider applying for a NEXUS card instead of Global Entry. The NEXUS program includes Global Entry benefits and is available for a lower fee of $50 for a five-year membership. Additionally, if you are applying for a family, the fee covers up to five people, making it a cost-effective option for families.

How to Apply for Global Entry for Kids
--------------------------------------

Applying for Global Entry for your children is a straightforward process. Here’s a step-by-step guide to help you through the application:

### Step 1: Create a CBP Account

Start by creating an account on the Trusted Traveler Programs (TTP) website. This account will allow you to complete the application and track its status.

### Step 2: Complete the Application

Fill out the online application for each child, providing accurate information about their identity, travel history, and background. Be prepared to answer questions about their eligibility and any potential concerns.

### Step 3: Pay the Application Fee

If you are not using a reimbursement benefit or promotional offer, you will need to pay the $120 application fee. Use a credit card that offers Global Entry reimbursement to minimize costs.

### Step 4: Schedule an Interview

After submitting the application, you will need to schedule an in-person interview at a Global Entry enrollment center. For children, this typically involves a shorter interview process, as they are considered low-risk travelers.

### Step 5: Attend the Interview

Bring necessary documents to the interview, including passports, birth certificates, and any other identification required. The interview will confirm the information provided in the application and complete the enrollment process.

Tips for a Smooth Global Entry Experience with Kids
---------------------------------------------------

Once your children have Global Entry, here are some tips to ensure a smooth travel experience:

* **Prepare Documentation:** Keep copies of your children’s Global Entry cards and passports handy during travel.
* **Practice the Process:** Before your trip, explain the Global Entry process to your kids so they know what to expect at the airport.
* **Stay Informed:** Check for any updates or changes to the Global Entry program that may affect your travel plans.

Conclusion
----------

Global Entry can significantly enhance your family’s travel experience by reducing wait times and simplifying the entry process. By taking advantage of credit card reimbursements, military benefits, promotional offers, or the NEXUS program, you can obtain Global Entry for your kids without paying the full $120 fee. With a little planning and research, you can enjoy a more convenient and cost-effective travel experience with your family.
