In today’s fast-paced forex markets, **precision timing** and **trend alignment** are key to successful trading. This daily strategy leverages **multi-timeframe analysis**, combining the **H4 chart** for trend direction with the **M15 chart** for pinpoint entry setups.

By blending higher timeframe trend logic with lower timeframe entries—such as **double highs/lows**, **clean left structures**, or **breaks in structure (BoS)**—traders can achieve **greater confidence** in their positions while minimizing risk and maximizing reward.

This professional, detailed guide will walk you through the methodology, setup conditions, stop-loss and take-profit placement, and profit securing techniques that make this strategy reliable for both swing traders and intraday participants.

---

### **Core Strategy Concept**

| Component | Timeframe | Purpose |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **H4 (4-Hour)** | Trend Identification | Determines whether to **buy or sell** |
| **M15 (15-Minute)** | Entry Setup | Pinpoints precise entry using market structure |
| **Entry Pattern** | Double low (sell), double high (buy), BoS or structure tap |  |
| **Stop Loss** | Just above the M15 entry candle |  |
| **Take Profit** | Based on H4 levels or market structure |  |
| **Profit Securing** | Flexible — partial take-profits and runners encouraged |  |



---

### **Step-by-Step Guide**

#### 1. **Determine Trend Using the H4 Chart**

* Look at **swing structure**: higher highs = bullish, lower lows = bearish.
* Mark key **support/resistance zones**.
* Determine whether the market is **trending** or **ranging**.

🟢 **If H4 is bullish**, prepare to **buy** on M15  
🔴 **If H4 is bearish**, prepare to **sell** on M15

---

#### 2. **Setup Conditions on the M15 Chart**

Once the H4 direction is clear, zoom into M15 and wait for one of the following:

* **Double High (for sells)** or **Double Low (for buys)**: Identifies exhaustion or liquidity trap
* **Clean Left**: A structure where the left side is free of recent traffic, indicating potential for smooth move
* **Break in Structure (BoS)**: Price breaks a previous swing high/low, then returns to **tap** into that zone

📌 **Entry Trigger**: Candle that forms the double top/bottom or taps back into the BoS zone.

---

#### 3. **Entry and Stop-Loss Placement**

* **Enter** on the confirmation candle (e.g., bearish engulfing after double top, bullish engulfing after double bottom)
* **Place stop-loss** slightly above (or below) the M15 entry candle’s wick – ensures you’re not stopped by noise.

✅ This tight SL placement enhances your **risk-to-reward ratio** and gives better control over drawdown.

---

#### 4. **Take-Profit (TP) Strategy**

Use the H4 chart to determine realistic profit zones:

* Identify **previous highs/lows**, fair value gaps, or key resistance/support
* **Measure risk-to-reward** for each TP zone:
  + TP1: **1:1**, take **50%**
  + TP2: **1:2**, take **20%**
  + TP3: **Leave runner** to trail with structure
* Always **review stop-loss range** before entry to confirm you’re not risking too much relative to reward

---

### **Example Trade Scenario**

**Asset:** GBP/USD  
**H4 Trend:** Bullish – price forming higher lows, nearing key resistance  
**M15 Setup:** Forms a double bottom with a clean left zone  
**Entry:** After bullish engulfing on the second bottom  
**Stop-Loss:** 8 pips below entry candle  
**TP1:** 15 pips (1:1) – secure 50%  
**TP2:** 30 pips (1:2) – secure 20%  
**TP3:** Leave runner with trailing stop under M15 higher lows

### **Risk Management Tips**

* Never risk more than **1-2% per trade**
* Always **measure SL distance** before entry — don’t force trades with wide SLs
* Use **trade journaling** to document which setups yield best results (double top vs BoS, etc.)
* Backtest and forward test before scaling position size
